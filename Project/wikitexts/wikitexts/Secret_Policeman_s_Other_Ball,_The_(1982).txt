It was the second of many shows to bear the celebrated \"Secret Policeman's\" title that became the iconic series known informally as [[The Secret Policeman's Balls]].\n\nThe show took place at the [[Drury Lane theatre]] in London over four nights between Wednesday 9 September and Saturday 12 September 1981.  It was a successor to the 1979 show [[The Secret Policeman's Ball (1979)|The Secret Policeman's Ball]]. Not all artists appeared at all performances: [[Phil Collins]], for example, could not commit to all four and was present at the last two shows only.<ref>{{cite book|last=Coleman|first=Ray|authorlink=Ray Coleman|title=Phil Collins The Definitive Biography|edition=illustrated|publisher=Simon & Schuster|location=London|year=1997|isbn=0-684-81784-5|page=110}}</ref>\n\nThe show was directed by [[Monty Python]] alumnus [[John Cleese]] and produced by [[Martin Lewis (humorist)|Martin Lewis]] and Peter Walker (Amnesty's fundraising officer).<ref>{{cite news |title=The Secret Policeman's Other Ball |url=http://www.variety.com/review/VE1117794729.html?categoryid=31&cs=1&p=0 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090426043026/http://www.variety.com/review/VE1117794729.html?categoryid=31&cs=1&p=0 |url-status=dead |archive-date=26 April 2009 |work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |date=31 December 1981 |accessdate=24 August 2012}}</ref> It subsequently yielded two separate movies (one version for the UK, directed by [[Julien Temple]], and a quite different version for the US) and two record albums (one each of comedy and music performances). The film was distributed by [[Miramax]].\n\nThe budget of the 1981 film was \u00a3125,000.<ref>BRITISH PRODUCTION 1981\nMoses, Antoinette. Sight and Sound; London Vol. 51, Iss. 4,  (Fall 1982): 258.</ref>\n\nThe show was very influential in galvanizing rock musicians to become involved in the human rights issue and in other political and social causes in subsequent decades.  Musicians who performed at the show who subsequently became activists in various fields include [[Sting (musician)|Sting]], [[Bob Geldof]], [[Eric Clapton]], [[Phil Collins]], [[Donovan]] and [[Midge
