The film stars [[Adrien Brody]] as an ex-office worker who becomes a [[Ventriloquism|ventriloquist]]. It also stars [[Milla Jovovich]], [[Illeana Douglas]], [[Vera Farmiga]], [[Jessica Walter]], [[Ron Leibman]], and [[Jared Harris]]. It premiered at the [[American Film Market]] on February 21, 2002, and received a [[limited theatrical release]] on September 12, 2003.\n\n==Plot==\nSteven Schoichet is a recently unemployed ne'er-do-well who has difficulty expressing himself. Steven finds he has a knack for [[ventriloquism]]. Steven's best friend is Fangora \"Fanny\" Gurkel, an aspiring [[punk rock]] singer who, along with Steven, is just looking for her niche. Eventually, Fanny takes a shine to [[klezmer]] music when she learns of an opportunity to get an actual gig. He was coached prior to and during production by veteran actor-ventriloquist-puppetmaker Alan Semok, who is credited in the film as special consultant and designer/creator of the title character.\n\n===Filming===\n[[Principal photography]] took place in [[New York City]], [[Wayne, New Jersey]], and [[Long Island, New York]] in July and August 2000.\n\n==Reception==\n\n===Box office===\nThe film was given a [[limited release]] in the United States. ''Dummy'' made $30,130 from 5 theaters in its opening weekend.
