Written by [[Dani\u00e8le Thompson]] and Pinoteau, the film is about a thirteen-year-old French girl finding her way at a new high school and coping with domestic problems. The film earned 4,378,500 admissions in France and was an international box-office hit.<ref name=\"allmovie\"/><ref name=\"imdbboxoffice\"/> The music was written by [[Vladimir Cosma]], with [[Richard Sanderson]] singing the song \"[[Reality (Richard Sanderson song)|Reality]]\". A sequel, ''[[La Boum 2]]'', was released in 1982.\n\n==Plot==\nFran\u00e7ois, a dentist, and his wife Fran\u00e7oise, an illustrator, move to Paris and place their daughter Vic, aged 13, in one of the capital's best schools. Making friends, her free time becomes a whirl of discos, cinemas, and parties. Fran\u00e7ois is contacted by Vanessa, a former lover, who insists he spends another night with her and, when he tries to go home, rings Fran\u00e7oise to say he is in hospital. Seeing through this ruse, Fran\u00e7oise kicks Fran\u00e7ois out, smashes up Vanessa's shop, and starts an affair with \u00c9ric, one of Vic's teachers. who is then punched in the street by Fran\u00e7ois. Trying to make sense of her parents' behaviour, Vic is helped by her great-grandmother Poupette, who encourages her in her relationship with Matthieu, the boy of her dreams, that results in a night together in a beach cabin. When Fran\u00e7ois goes to pick Vic up from school, Matthieu insults him, not knowing who he is, and gets punched in the street. Fran\u00e7oise discovers that she is pregnant and decides to reconcile with Fran\u00e7ois.
