It is the debut feature of director Jeff Malmberg. It was the inspiration for ''[[Welcome to Marwen]]'', a 2018 drama directed by [[Robert Zemeckis]].<ref name=\"hollywoodreporter.com\">{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/robert-zemeckis-direct-marwencol-universal-650730|title=Robert Zemeckis to Direct 'Marwencol' for Universal|website=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|date=24 October 2013 |accessdate=22 December 2018}}</ref><ref name=\"variety.com\">{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/universal-robert-zemeckis-steve-carell-pic-for-november-2018-1202403338/|title=Universal Dates Robert Zemeckis-Directed Steve Carell Film for November 2018|first1=Justin|last1=Kroll|date=28 April 2017|website=Variety.com|accessdate=22 December 2018}}</ref>\n\n==Synopsis==\nOn April 8, 2000, Mark Hogancamp was attacked outside of a bar by five men who beat him nearly to death after he drunkenly told them he was a cross-dresser. After nine days in a coma and 40 days in the hospital, Hogancamp was discharged with brain damage that left him little memory of his previous life. Unable to afford therapy, he creates his own by building a 1/6-scale [[World War II]]\u2013era [[Belgians|Belgian]] town in his yard and populating it with dolls representing himself, his friends, and even his attackers. He calls that town [[Marwencol (art installation)|Marwencol]], blending the names Mark, Wendy, and Colleen.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/04/07/garden/07marwencol.html|title=In a Tiny Universe, Room to Heal: At Home With Mark Hogancamp|last=Green|first=Penelope|date=2011-04-06|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-03-27|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>\n\nHogancamp was initially discovered by photographer David Naugle, who documented and shared his story with [[Esopus (magazine)|''Esopus'']] magazine.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.esopusmag.com/archivesubright.php?Id=3199&pID=3014|title=Marwencol on My Mind|last=Hogancamp|first=Mark|date=Fall 2005|website=[[Esopus (magazine)|Esopus]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060511082836/http://www.esopusmag.com/archivesubright.php?Id=3199&pID=3014|archive-date=2006-05-11|url-status=dead|issue=5}}</ref>\n\n==Production==\nThe film was shot in [[New York State]] between 2006 and 2010, using a combination of [[DVCAM]] video and [[Super-8]] film formats.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.documentary.org/magazine/2010-jacqueline-donnet-emerging-documentary-filmmaker-award-cutting-path-understanding-jeff|title=2010 Jacqueline Donnet Emerging Documentary Filmmaker Award--Cutting a Path of Understanding: Jeff Malmberg|last=Lanthier|first=Joseph Jon|date=2010-12-02|work=International Documentary Association|access-date=2017-09-23|language=en}}</ref>\n\n==Reception==\nOn [[review aggregator]] [[Rotten Tomatoes]], the film holds an approval rating of 98% based on 63 reviews, with an average rating of 8.4/10.
