|publisher=BoxOfficeMojo.com}}</ref>\n}}\n'''''Eros''''' is a 2004 [[anthology film]] consisting of three [[short film|short segments]]: ''The Hand'' directed by [[Wong Kar-wai]] in [[Mandarin Chinese|Mandarin]], ''Equilibrium'' by [[Steven Soderbergh]] in English, and ''The Dangerous Thread of Things'' by [[Michelangelo Antonioni]] in Italian. Each segment addresses the themes of love and sex.<ref name=\"imdb\">{{cite web|title=Eros |website=Internet Movie Database |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0343663/ |access-date=22 May 2012}}</ref>\n\n==Plot==\n;''The Hand''\nMiss Hua, a 1960s high-end [[call girl]] is visited by a shy dressmaker's assistant Zhang, to take her measure. He hears the sounds of sex, as he waits in her living room. He is drawn towards her but there is no meeting ground between the two individuals from completely different classes. She summons him when her client leaves. She tells him, she will supply him with an aid to his memory. He will think about her while designing her clothes, she says.<ref name=ebert>{{cite web|url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/eros-2004|title=Eros review by Roger Ebert|access-date=6 August 2015|publisher=rogerebert.com}}</ref><ref name=rotten>{{cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/eros_2005/|title=Film Info|access-date=6 August 2015|website=Rotten Tomatoes}}</ref>\n\n;''Equilibrium''\nNick Penrose is an advertising executive in the year 1955 under enormous pressure at work. He tells his psychiatrist Dr. Pearl about a recurring dream of a beautiful naked woman in his apartment, as they discuss the possible reasons why his stress seems to manifest itself in the erotic dream.<ref name=ebert/><ref name=rotten/>\n\n;''The Dangerous Thread of Things''\nA bored couple, Christopher and Cloe,  take a stroll near a resort on a lake on the coast of Tuscany. Visiting a restaurant on the beach, they see a sexy young woman, Linda. Linda tells him where she lives, inside a crumbling medieval tower. He goes to visit her and they have sex. As Christopher leaves the place, the two women later encounter each other on the beach, both naked.<ref name=ebert/><ref name=rotten/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2005/04/08/movies/sex-sex-sex-seen-through-experienced-cinematic-eyes.html?_r=0|newspaper=New York Times|title=Sex, Sex, Sex, Seen Through Experienced Cinematic Eyes|date=8 April 2005|access-date=6 August 2015}}</ref>\n\n==Cast==\n''The Hand''\n* [[Gong Li]] as Miss Hua\n* [[Chang Chen]] as Xiao Zhang, Jin's apprentice\n* [[Feng Tien]] as Master Jin \n* Luk Auntie as Hua's Servant, Ying\n* Jianjun Zhou as Hua's Lover, Zhao\n\n''Equilibrium''\n* [[Robert Downey Jr.]] as Nick Penrose\n* [[Alan Arkin]] as Dr. Pearl / Hal\n* [[Ele Keats]] as The Woman / Cecelia\n\n''The Dangerous Thread of Things''\n* [[Christopher Buchholz]] as Christopher\n* Regina Nemni as Cloe\n* [[Luisa Ranieri]] as The Girl / Linda\n* Cecilia Luci as Girl by the Cascade\n* Karima Machehour as Girl by the Cascade\n\n==Production==\n===Development===\nAfter having produced Antonioni's ''[[Beyond the Clouds (1995 film)|Beyond the Clouds]]'' in 1995, France-based producer [[St\u00e9phane Tchalgadjieff]] came up with the idea of doing a trilogy with \"eros\" as the subject, with one segment directed by the Italian director. The concept was to have two major younger directors, who have been on record to say that they have been influenced by his filmmaking, accompany him. Each would do a segment in total freedom, unaware of the other two. Two French producers, Rapha\u00ebl Berdugo and [[Jacques Bar]]along with Italian producer [[Domenico Procacci]] joined him.<ref name=\"Presskit\">\"Eros\" Original Venice Film Festival Presskit by www.filmpressplus.com</ref> Amedeo Pagani, producer and Antonioni's friend, was asked to participate but given Antonioni's medical status, he refused to be involved in the movie. \"Michelangelo is a good friend but I think it's not a good idea to have him directing an erotic feature in his condition\".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.cinecitta.com/IT/it-it/news/53/17410/amedeo-pagani-rinuncia-ad-antonioni.aspx|title=Amedeo Pagani rinuncia ad Antonioni|date=April 17, 2002|work=News Cinecitt\u00e0|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>\n\nAfter Antonioni's approval, production development on ''Eros'' officially began in 2001: after discussing possible directors, the group of producers selected [[Wong Kar-wai]] and [[Pedro Almod\u00f3var]] to helm the other two short films. During the Italian press tour for ''[[Talk to Her]]'' in March 2002, Almod\u00f3var declared to be \"happy, thrilled and honored to share a film with a master like Antonioni\"<ref name=\"Almodovar\">{{cite web|url=https://news.cinecitta.com/IT/it-it/news/53/17178/almodovar-honoured-to-work-with-antonioni.aspx\n|title=Almodovar: \"Che onore lavorare con Antonioni\"|date=March 22, 2002|work=News Cinecitt\u00e0|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> Wong Kar-wai, for his part, said that the Italian director \"had been the guiding light for me and filmmakers of my generation so I'm deeply honored to participate in this project and show him my gratitude\".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.corriere.it/speciali/2004/Spettacoli/cannes/diario/22052004.shtml|title=Anche Wong Kar-wai punta alla Palma|date=March 21, 2004|work=Corriere della Sera|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>\n\n==== Almod\u00f3var's withdrawal====\nAlmod\u00f3var was supposed to start pre-production and location scouting in early April 2002, on a screenplay based on the sexual initiation of a 8-year-old boy.<ref name=\"Almodovar\" /> After several delays in finishing the script and being pressed for time as the production of his film ''[[Bad Education (2004 film)|Bad Education]]'' approached, he was forced to withdraw.\nDuring an event to celebrate Antonioni's 90th birthday in September 2002, his wife Enrica announced that Almod\u00f3var had left the project and would be replaced by Steven Soderbergh.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.aintitcool.com/node/13393|title=Steven Soderbergh to replace Pedro Almodovar & join Wong Kar-Wai and Michelangelo Antonioni on EROS!!!\n|date=September 27, 2002|work=Ain't It Cool News|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> It was also announced that the Spanish director was in talks to create the interstitial segment that would link the three parts of the film together.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.e-duesse.it/News/Cinema/Cinema-Steven-Soderbergh-entra-nel-progetto-Eros|title=Cinema: Steven Soderbergh entra nel progetto \"Eros\"\n|date=September 27, 2002|work=Ain't It Cool News|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>\nSoderbergh declared that he had accepted the job because he simply wanted his name \"on a poster with Michelangelo Antonioni's\"<ref name=\"Presskit\" />\n\n===Casting===\nAntonioni didn't want familiar faces in his segment and also needed actors that were at ease with the nude scenes. Casting commenced in summer 2001. [[Christopher Buchholz]], son of German actor [[Horst Buchholz]], was cast in the role of Christopher, the husband. The actor was originally uncomfortable with the sex scenes, since a particular sequence from the original script required full front nudity. The scene was eventually cut and never filmed.<ref name=\"PressConference\">{{cite web|url=https://news.cinecitta.com/IT/it-it/news/53/54505/eros.aspx/|title=Eros|date=September 9, 2004|work=Cinecitt\u00e0 News|access-date=December 4, 2018|author1=Stefanutto Rosa, Stefano }}</ref> Former dancer [[Regina Nemni]] got the part of Cloe, the wife and newcomer Luisa Ranieri, popular at the time for a [[Nestea]] commercial, was cast as Linda.\n\nHaving written a less erotic script, Soderbergh got easy access to A-list actors for the main roles. On February 27, 2003, ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' reported that both [[Robert Downey Jr.]] and [[Alan Arkin]] had been chosen for the yet-untitled segment.<ref name=\"Variety\">{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2003/film/markets-festivals/downey-in-eros-for-soderbergh-1117881400/|title=Downey in 'Eros' for Soderbergh|date=February 27, 2003|work=Variety|access-date=December 4, 2018|author1=Fleming, Michael }}</ref> For the dual role of the \"Woman in the Dream\" (whose scenes required full nudity) and Nick's wife Cecelia, dozens of actresses auditioned for casting director [[Debra Zane]]. Ultimately, 29-year-old actress (and former model) [[Ele Keats]] booked the part.\n\nIn spring 2003, [[Wong Kar-wai]] completed the casting for his segment: young Taiwanese actor [[Chang Chen]], who had already worked with the director in ''[[Happy Together (1997 film)|Happy Together]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0151654/|title=Chen Chang|work=IMDb|access-date=December 4, 2018 }}</ref> landed the lead role of Zhang. [[Gong Li]], who was about to start filming Wong's ''[[2046 (film)|2046]]'', joined the cast as Miss Hua.\n\n===Filming===\nThe first episode to be filmed was Antonioni's ''Il filo pericoloso delle cose''. Production began in late October 2001 in [[Capalbio]] ([[Tuscany]]), on a scheduled 6-week shoot.<ref name= \"Repubblica\">{{cite web|url=http://trovacinema.repubblica.it/news/dettaglio/antonioni-al-via-la-produzione-di-eros/194777/|title=Antonioni: al via la produzione di 'Eros'|date= October 18, 2001 |work=Repubblica|access-date=December 4, 2018 }}</ref> With a few exceptions (like cinematographer [[Marco Pontecorvo]]), the crew was mainly composed by young newcomers. Rome-based architect Stefano Luci debuted as a production designer with ''Eros'' thanks to his friendship with Antonioni and helped him finding the right locations for the shooting.<ref name= \"Tirreno\">{{cite web|url=http://ricerca.gelocal.it/iltirreno/archivio/iltirreno/2002/02/06/LG501.html|title=L'\"Eros\" di Antonioni nella terra dei ciak Lo scenografo del grande maestro svela i segreti del set capalbiese|date= February 6, 2002 |work=Il Tirreno|access-date=December 4, 2018 }}</ref> Other locations included the [[Natural Park of Maremma]] and the [[Lago del Turano|Lake Turano]]. Mainly shot during the day to capture summer's end's natural light with both digital and 35mm cameras,<ref name= \"Repubblica\" /> filming officially wrapped in early December.<ref name= \"Tirreno\" /> The final budget for the segment was 2,5 billion liras.<ref name= \"Tagli per Antonioni\">{{cite web|url=https://news.cinecitta.com/IT/it-it/news/55/59455/eros-tagli-per-antonioni.aspx|title=\"Eros\": tagli per Antonioni|date= November 29, 2004 |work=Cinecitt\u00e0 News|access-date=December 4, 2018 }}</ref>\n\nDespite being located in 1950s [[New York City]], Soderbergh's ''Equilibrium'' was completely shot in a [[Los Angeles]] studio. Produced by Soderbergh's long-time collaborator [[Gregory Jacobs]], the segment entered production in late February 2003 and principal photography lasted one week. Production designer Philip Messina and [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]]-winner costume designer [[Milena Canonero]] teamed up to recreate the 1950s look required by the script. Knowing that the other two filmmakers would be more direct in their approach to the subject, Soderbergh chose a lighter, more elliptical path. \"I liked the idea that what is supposed to be 'an erotic film' stars [[Alan Arkin]] and [[Robert Downey Jr.]]\"<ref name=\"Presskit\" /> Actress [[Ele Keats]], as the only erotic element of the story, spent several hours totally naked on set but, as she later declared, Soderbergh \"had meticulously planned everything ahead of time so for me it was like being relaxed at home\".<ref name= \"Dichiarazioni Press\">{{cite web|url=https://news.cinecitta.com/IT/it-it/news/55/54505/eros.aspx|title=\"Eros\"|date= September 10, 2004 |work=Cinecitt\u00e0 News|access-date=December 4, 2018 }}</ref>\n\nWong Kar Wai began production on ''The Hand'' in early 2003, during the outbreak of [[SARS]] in [[South China|southern China]]. The original plan of shooting in [[Shanghai]] had to be revoked so production had to be moved to [[Hong Kong]].<ref name=\"Presskit\" />  Many crew members quit because of the risks, forcing Wong to work with a reduced staff. Because of the risks, the cast & crew had to wash hands regularly on set and had to work wearing masks, avoiding any possible physical contact with each other. To speed up production, the last two days of shooting were done in a 48-hour period of continuous work.<ref name=\"Presskit\" /> \"It was a nightmare but this situation inspired me to make a film about the sense of touch,\" declared the director during the press conference in [[Venice]].<ref name= \"Corriere\">{{cite web|url=https://www.comune.re.it/cinema/catfilm.nsf/PES_PerTitoloRB/CDD1CE76432698FFC125742E004A4671?opendocument|title=Tre maestri del cinema raccontano l'erotismo.|date= September 9, 2004 |work=Corriere della Sera|access-date=December 4, 2018 }}</ref>\n\n[[Luisa Ranieri]] said her masturbation scene in ''The Dangerous Thread of Things'' was traumatic. \"It was one of the first scenes, Antonioni made me understand that I had to strip naked and get on the bed and touch myself,\" she explained. \"I had no intention of doing it, but then he convinced me ...On the set I was rubbing my eyes, I'm not doing a hardcore movie I said to myself. It was a shock. After that I got sick and I threw up.\"<ref>{{cite web |title=Luisa Ranieri definita la nuova Sophia Loren si racconta: \"Sono una timida\u2026\" |work=www.novella2000.it |url=https://www.novella2000.it/luisa-ranieri-definita-la-nuova-sophia-loren-si-racconta-sono-una-timida/ |access-date=2022-01-30 }}</ref>\n\n==Reception==\n===Critical response===\nIn North America, critical response for ''Eros'' was very mixed. [[Rotten Tomatoes]] reported that 34% of critics have given the film a positive review based on 68 reviews, with an average rating of 4.80 out of 10. The site's critics consensus reads, \"Though Wong's short lives up to the promise of the title, Antonioni's is a serious disappointment.\"<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/eros_2005 |title=Eros (2004) |website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] |publisher=[[Fandango (company)|Fandango]] |access-date=February 9, 2021}}</ref> On [[Metacritic]], the film has a weighted average score of 51 out of 100 based on 22 critic reviews, indicating \"mixed or average reviews\".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.metacritic.com/movie/eros |title=Eros Reviews |website=[[Metacritic]] |publisher=[[CBS Interactive]] |access-date=February 9, 2021}}</ref> American critics were almost unanimous in their praise of [[Wong Kar-wai]]'s segment, and almost unanimous in their disapproval of the [[Michelangelo Antonioni]] piece. [[Steven Soderbergh]]'s contribution drew mixed notices.\n\n[[Roger Ebert]] gave Wong's segment four out of four stars, Soderbergh's three stars, and Antonioni's a mere one star.<ref>[http://rogerebert.suntimes.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20050407/REVIEWS/50323002/1001] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060308183914/http://rogerebert.suntimes.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=%2F20050407%2FREVIEWS%2F50323002%2F1001 |date=2006-03-08 }}</ref>  On the syndicated television show ''[[At the Movies with Ebert & Roeper|Ebert & Roeper]]'', he gave the film a \"thumbs up\" rating.  In his ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]'' review, he wrote:\n{{blockquote|Are the three films in ''Eros'' intended to be (a) erotic, (b) about eroticism or (c) both? The directors respond in three different ways. [[Wong Kar-wai]] chooses (c), [[Steven Soderbergh]] chooses (b) and [[Michelangelo Antonioni]], alas, arrives at None of the Above...The Antonioni film is an embarrassment. Regina Nemni acts all of her scenes wearing a perfectly transparent blouse for no other reason, I am afraid, than so we can see her breasts. [[Luisa Ranieri]] acts mostly in the nude. The result is soft-core porn of the most banal variety, and when the second woman begins to gambol on the beach one yearns for [[Russ Meyer]] to come to the rescue. When you see a woman gamboling in the nude in a Meyer film, you stay gamboled with...I return to [[Wong Kar-wai]]'s \"The Hand.\" It stays with me. The characters expand in my memory and imagination. I feel empathy for both of them: Miss Hua, sadly accepting the fading of her beauty, the disappearance of her clients, the loss of her health, and Mr. Zhang, who will always be in her thrall. \"I became a tailor because of you,\" he says. It is the greatest compliment it is within his power to give, and she knows it. Knows it, and is touched by it as none of the countless words of her countless clients have ever, could ever, touch her.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://rogerebert.suntimes.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20050407/REVIEWS/50323002/1001 | work=Chicago Sun-Times | title=Eros | url-status=dead | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060308183914/http://rogerebert.suntimes.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=%2F20050407%2FREVIEWS%2F50323002%2F1001 | archive-date=2006-03-08 }}</ref>}}\n\n===Box office===\n''Eros'' was distributed for theatrical release in North America by [[Warner Independent Pictures]] on April 8, 2005.  Promotion was poor; for example, on ''[[At the Movies (1986 TV program)|At the Movies with Ebert and Roeper]]'', critic [[Richard Roeper]] remarked that he was surprised that Warner Independent did not send any clips to be broadcast on the show and that this was the only movie reviewed on the show he remembered for which the studio had taken such a step (incidentally, the critics gave the film a \"Two Thumbs Up\" rating). Opening on twelve screens, box office was weak, earning just US $53,666 ($4,472 per screen) in its opening weekend on its way to a low US $188,392 final gross.\n\nBoxofficemojo.com reports that the total worldwide gross for ''Eros'' is $1,535,829.\n\n==References==\n{{reflist}}\n\n==External links==\n* {{IMDb title|id=0343663|title=Eros}}\n\n{{Wong Kar-wai}}\n{{Michelangelo Antonioni}}\n{{Steven Soderbergh}}\n\n{{DEFAULTSORT:Eros (Film)}}\n[[Category:2004 films]]\n[[Category:Hong Kong New Wave films]]\n[[Category:Italian anthology films]]\n[[Category:2004 drama films]]\n[[Category:Films directed by Steven Soderbergh]]\n[[Category:Films directed by Wong Kar-wai]]\n[[Category:Italian independent films]]\n[[Category:Warner Independent Pictures films]]\n[[Category:Films directed by Michelangelo Antonioni]]\n[[Category:Films with screenplays by Steven Soderbergh]]\n[[Category:Films produced by Gregory Jacobs]]\n[[Category:Films set in 1955]]\n[[Category:Films set in the 1960s]]\n[[Category:American independent films]]\n[[Category:American anthology films]]\n[[Category:Hong Kong independent films]]\n[[Category:2000s American films]]"}]}}}}
