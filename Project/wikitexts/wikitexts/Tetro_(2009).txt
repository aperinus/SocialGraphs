Production was scheduled to begin in [[Buenos Aires]], [[Argentina]] in late 2007.<ref name=\"cops\">{{cite journal | first=Dave | last=McNary | url=https://www.variety.com/article/VR1117959462.html?categoryid=1236&cs=1 | title=Coppola cops Italo Argentina tale | journal=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] | date=February 13, 2007 | accessdate=August 6, 2008 }}</ref> Coppola was attracted to Argentina as a location, \"I knew Argentina has a great cultural, artistic, literary, musical, cinema tradition, and I like those kinds of atmospheres very much because you usually find creative people to work with.\"<ref name=\"deutsche\">{{cite news | first=Astrid | last=Riehn | title=Coppola starts shooting new film ''Tetro'' in Buenos Aires | work=[[Deutsche Presse-Agentur]] | date=March 29, 2008 }}</ref> Production did not begin as scheduled, and by March 2008, [[Vincent Gallo]] and [[Maribel Verd\u00fa]] joined the cast.<ref name=\"french\">{{cite journal | first=Winter | last=Miller | url=https://www.variety.com/article/VR1117982112.html?categoryid=1236&cs=1 | title=French cops lead in 'Tetro' | journal=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] | date=March 9, 2008 | accessdate=August 6, 2008 }}</ref> The Spanish company Tornasol Films and the Italian company BIM Distribuzione signed with the director to co-produce the film.<ref name=\"tornasol\">{{cite web | first=Charles | last=Newberry |url=https://variety.com/2008/film/markets-festivals/tornasol-bim-join-coppola-s-tetro-1117983052/ | title=Tornasol, BIM join Coppola's 'Tetro' | work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] | date=March 27, 2008 | accessdate=31 March 2018 }}</ref> Production began on March 31, 2008 with a budget of $5&nbsp;million,<ref name=\"imdb.com\">{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0964185/business|title=Tetro (2009) - IMDb}}</ref> with Coppola using the production style similar to his previous film ''[[Youth Without Youth (film)|Youth Without Youth]]''.<ref name=\"french\" /> Filming took place in [[La Boca]] in Buenos Aires and other parts of the capital city. Filming also followed in the Andean foothills in [[Patagonia]] and at the [[Ciudad de la Luz]] studios in [[Alicante]], [[Spain]].<ref name=\"tornasol\" /> Production concluded in June.<ref name=\"cool\">{{cite journal | first=Brian | last=Byrnes | title=All's cool with Coppola, Argentine actors union | journal=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] | date=May 29, 2008 }}</ref>\n\nIn May 2008, during filming in Argentina, the Argentina Actors Association, an actors' union, claimed that production of ''Tetro'' was shut down due to union members working on the film without a contract. According to ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'', \"Local press reports say that script changes and communication problems between the multi-national cast and crew have extended filming days beyond regularly scheduled hours, and that some of the Argentine actors are still not certain of their salary.\" The director's spokesperson, Kathleen Talbert, denied that production was halted, saying, \"There are no holds on shooting, no problem with actors. In fact, the majority of the Argentine actors have already wrapped the shooting.\"<ref>{{cite journal | first=Brian | last=Byrnes | title=Union claims 'Tetro' shut down | journal=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] | date=May 23, 2008 }}</ref> By the end of the month, the union said the issue was resolved, reporting, \"The lawyers for the producers presented the necessary documentation and recognized the errors that they had made.
