The 70-minute special was Murphy's first feature stand-up and the predecessor to the wide theatrical release in 1987, ''[[Eddie Murphy Raw]]''. ''Delirious'' was also released as an album on October 24, 1983, titled ''[[Eddie Murphy: Comedian]]'', which won [[Grammy Award for Best Comedy Album|Best Comedy Album]] at the 1984 [[Grammy Award]]s.\n\n==Overview==\nUnlike his acts on ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'', Murphy's performance was very [[profanity|profane]], saying the word ''[[fuck]]'' a total of 230 times, and ''[[shit]]'' 171 times.<ref>{{cite web |first=Jeff |last=Swindoll |title=Eddie Murphy: Delirious - 25th Anniversary \u2013 DVD Review |url=http://www.monstersandcritics.com/dvd/reviews/article_1480982.php/Eddie_Murphy_Delirious_-_25th_Anniversary_\u2013_DVD_Review |work=[[Monsters and Critics]] |date=June 2, 2009 |access-date=July 10, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121221015009/http://www.monstersandcritics.com/dvd/reviews/article_1480982.php/Eddie_Murphy_Delirious_-_25th_Anniversary_%E2%80%93_DVD_Review |archive-date=December 21, 2012 }}</ref>\n\nThe show was recorded at [[DAR Constitution Hall]] in Washington, D.C.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/they-played-here/2015/02/26/d59b5514-b926-11e4-9423-f3d0a1ec335c_story.html|title=They played here|work=Washington Post|date=February 26, 2015}}</ref>\n\nBefore the show started, [[The BusBoys]] performed \"(The Boys Are) Back in Town\" over a montage of pre-show footage of Murphy traveling with his road crew. Then, he is introduced after the song and thanks The BusBoys for the opening act before starting his comedy routine.\n\nAmong the topics Murphy addresses is the lure [[Ice cream van|ice cream trucks]] have on children. Once the ice cream was bought, they would sing and dance mockingly in front of kids who could not afford it.<ref>{{cite web | first=Willa |last=Paskin | title=Nostalgia Fact-Check: How Do Eddie Murphy's Delirious and Raw Hold Up? | url=http://www.vulture.com/2011/09/nostalgia_fact-check_how_do_ed.html | work=Vulture | publisher=[[New York (magazine)|New York Media]] | location=New York City| date=September 8, 2011 | access-date=November 2, 2013}}</ref> Other topics that he addresses are family parties (his cookout skit featuring a humanoid cryptid for an aunt-in-law and an uncle who ignites the whole backyard in an attempt to work the grill) parental discipline (his shoe-throwing mothers monologue), [[Michael Jackson]], [[James Brown]], [[Stevie Wonder]], [[racism]], [[Reaganomics]], [[LGBT|gay people]], (including a routine depicting \"[[The Honeymooners]]\" Ralph Kramden and Ed Norton as same-sex sweethearts), [[HIV/AIDS|AIDS]], and [[Marian Anderson]].
