It focuses on the conservation work of park rangers within the [[Democratic Republic of the Congo|Congo]]'s [[Virunga National Park]] during the rise of the violent [[M23 Rebellion]] in 2012 and investigates the activity of the British oil company [[Soco International]] within the UNESCO [[World Heritage Site]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Virunga National Park|url=https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/63|publisher=[[UNESCO]]|access-date=20 August 2014|work=[[World Heritage Site|World Heritage List]]}}</ref> Soco International ended up officially exploring oil opportunities in Virunga in April 2014.<ref>{{cite news|title= Oil firm Soco to begin seismic testing in Congo's Virunga park|url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/congodemocratic-oil-idUKL6N0NF36A20140423|access-date=20 August 2014|work=[[Reuters]]|publisher=[[Thomson Reuters]]|date=23 April 2014|author=Reuters Staff}}</ref> The film premiered at the [[Tribeca Film Festival]] on 17 April 2014. After airing on [[Netflix]], it was nominated for an [[Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature]].\n\n==Synopsis==\nThe documentary tells the story of four characters fighting to protect [[Virunga National Park]] in the [[Democratic Republic of Congo]], home to the world's last [[mountain gorillas]], from war, poaching, and the threat of oil exploration. Following gorilla caregiver Andr\u00e9 Bauma, central sector warden [[Rodrigue Mugaruka Katembo]], chief warden [[Emmanuel de Merode]], and the French investigative journalist [[M\u00e9lanie Gouby]], the film focuses on the natural beauty and biodiversity of Virunga, as well as the complex political and economic issues surrounding oil exploration and armed conflict in the region.\n\n==Production==\nProduction began in 2012, when von Einsiedel traveled to Virunga National Park in the Democratic Republic of Congo with the intention of documenting the positive progress which had been made by the park authorities in encouraging tourism and development in the region. However, within three weeks of arriving in Virunga, conflict began with the [[M23 rebellion]] in April 2012,<ref>{{cite web|title=About|url=http://virungamovie.com/#about|website=Virunga official website|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref> shifting the focus of the film to cover the emerging conflict.\n\nVon Einsiedel collaborated with park officials and French journalist M\u00e9lanie Gouby to investigate the role of the British oil company Soco International, which had been undertaking activities in the area.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Fuchs|first1=Ellise|title='A Constantly Driving Feeling': Interview with Orlando von Einsiedel|url=http://www.popmatters.com/feature/183563-a-constantly-driving-feeling-interview-with-orlando-von-einsiedel-di/|website=[[PopMatters]]|access-date=20 August 2014|date=11 July 2014}}</ref> Undercover filming showed Soco representatives offering bribes to park rangers.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Rosen|first1=Jon|title=Confronting Threats to Virunga, Africa's Oldest Park|url=http://news.nationalgeographic.com/news/2014/04/140429-virunga-gorillas-congo-conservation-oil-soco/|publisher=[[National Geographic]]|access-date=20 August 2014|date=29 April 2014}}</ref>\n\nSoco International has strongly denied the allegations made in the documentary.<ref>{{cite web|title=SOCO comments on unfounded allegations in film and re-emphasises commitment to helping local communities|url=http://www.socointernational.com/soco-comments-on-unfounded-allegations-in-film-and|publisher=[[SOCO International]]|access-date=20 August 2014|date=18 April 2014}}</ref>\n\n==Release==\n''Virunga'' had its world premiere at the [[Tribeca Film Festival]] in New York City on 17 April 2014. The premiere of the film came just two days after Virunga National Park's chief warden, Emmanuel de Merode, was shot by unidentified gunmen on the road from the city of Goma to the park's headquarters in Rumangabo.<ref>{{cite news|title=Belgian Emmanuel de Merode shot in DR Congo ambush|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-27049627|access-date=20 August 2014|work=[[BBC News]]|publisher=[[BBC]]|date=16 April 2014}}</ref> De Merode survived the attack and the premiere of ''Virunga'' went ahead afterwards.<ref>[https://www.samaritanmag.com/news/virunga-documentary-honours-defenders-few-living-mountain-gorillas Virunga Documentary Honours Defenders Of Few Living Mountain Gorillas \u2014 Samaritanmag.com]</ref>\n\nThe film has been screened at multiple film festivals around the world, including Canadian festivals [[Hot Docs]] and DOXA; [[Docville]] in Leuven, Belgium; in the U.S. at [[Little Rock Film Festival]] in Arkansas, Mountainfilm in Colorado, [[AFI Docs]] in Washington DC, [[Traverse City Film Festival]] in Michigan. The UK premiere of the film was at the [[Edinburgh International Film Festival]] on 24 June 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=Past Screenings|url=http://virungamovie.com/screenings/past|website=Virunga official website|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref>\n\nOn 28 July 2014, it was announced that [[Netflix]] had picked up exclusive rights to the film.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Steel|first1=Emily|title=Netflix Bolsters Offerings in Documentary Genre|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/28/business/media/netflix-bolsters-offerings-in-documentary-genre.html|access-date=20 August 2014|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=28 July 2014}}</ref> The documentary was released onto the service on 7 November the same year.<ref>{{cite news|last=Sinha-Roy |first=Pifa |title=Netflix's 'Virunga' uncovers Congo's fight to protect resources |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-film-netflix-virunga-idUSKBN0IQ2OB20141107 |work=[[Reuters]]|publisher=[[Thomson Reuters]] |date=6 November 2014 |access-date=8 November 2014}}</ref> On June 23, 2020, it was announced that [[Barry Jenkins]] would write the feature film adaptation of the ''Virunga'', and that [[Leonardo DiCaprio]] would produce it.<ref>{{Cite web|last=N'Duka|first=Amanda|date=2020-06-23|title=Oscar Winner Barry Jenkins Teams With Leonardo DiCaprio & Netflix On Film Adaptation Of 'Virunga' Documentary|url=https://deadline.com/2020/06/oscar-winner-barry-jenkins-leonardo-dicaprio-netflix-film-adaptation-virunga-documentary-1202967349/|access-date=2020-07-24|website=Deadline|language=en}}</ref>\n\n==Reception==\n\n===Critical reception===\n[[File:Joanna Natasegara and Orlando von Einsiedel at the 74th Annual Peabody Awards.jpg|thumb|right|Joanna Natasegara and Orlando von Einsiedel at the 74th Annual Peabody Awards]]\n''Virunga'' has received universal acclaim by critics. On [[Rotten Tomatoes]], the film has a [[List of films with a 100% rating on Rotten Tomatoes|100% rating]] based on 21 reviews with an [[Weighted arithmetic mean|average rating]] of 9.09/10. The site's critical consensus reads \"''Virunga'' offers a heart-rending glimpse of natural wonders vulnerable to the atrocities of greed -- and the people devoting their lives to defending them\".<ref name=\"rt\">{{cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/virunga/|title=Virunga (2014)|work=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|publisher=[[Fandango Media]]|access-date=1 July 2019}}</ref> [[Metacritic]], another [[review aggregator]], assigned the film a weighted average score of 95 out of 100 based on 5 reviews from mainstream critics, indicating \"universal acclaim\". It is currently one of the site's highest-rated films.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/movie/virunga|title=Virunga Reviews|publisher=[[CBS Interactive]]|work=[[Metacritic]]|access-date=8 (Netflix)|url=http://www.peabodyawards.com/award-profile/virunga|publisher=[[Peabody Award]]s|access-date=9 October 2015}}</ref> the Feature Documentary Award at DOXA Documentary Festival in Vancouver, Canada; the Award of International Emerging Filmmaker at Hot Docs in Toronto; the Golden Rock Documentary Award at Little Rock Film Festival; and the Action and Change Together (ACT Now) Award at the [[Crested Butte Film Festival]]. It won two gongs at the One World Media Awards at BAFTA - Best Documentary, and the Corruption Reporting award. The film was also nominated for Best Documentary Feature at Tribeca Film Festival. The film was nominated for the [[Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature]] for the [[87th Academy Awards]].<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Oscars 2015: Full List of Nomiations|url=http://insidemovies.ew.com/2015/01/15/oscars-2015-nominations/|magazine=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|date=15 January 2015|access-date=15 January 2015|first=Jeff|last=Labrecque}}</ref><ref>[https://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2015 2015|Oscars.org]</ref>\n\n==Impact==\nThe allegations brought against Soco International by the documentary, and supported by local NGOs and civil society organizations working in and around Virunga National park, put increased pressure on the company to put an end to its exploration for oil within the protected World Heritage Site.<ref>{{cite web|title=WWF calls on Soco to explain Virunga allegations in new film|url=http://wwf.panda.org/wwf_news/?219933/WWF-calls-on-Soco-to-explain-Virunga-allegations-in-new-film%22|date=18 April 2014|first=Kate|last=Holt|publisher=[[World Wildlife Fund]]|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref>\n\nOn 11 June 2014, Soco International and the [[World Wildlife Fund|WWF]] announced a joint statement in which the oil company committed \"not to undertake or commission any exploratory or other drilling within Virunga National Park unless UNESCO and the DRC government agree that such activities are not incompatible with its World Heritage status\".<ref>{{cite web|title=SOCO and WWF agree way forward on future activity in Virunga National Park (DRC)|url=http://www.socointernational.com/soco-and-wwf-agree-way-forward-on-future-activity-|publisher=[[Soco International]]|access-date=20 August 2014|date=11 June 2014}}</ref> This was widely cited as a victory for WWF, which had long been campaigning for Soco to leave the region, and credit was also given to the filmmakers. However, strong concerns about the credibility of this agreement were raised by the filmmakers,<ref>{{cite web|title=Soco International's Latest Move in Virunga National Park Leaves More Questions than it Answers|url=http://virungamovie.com/blog/details/8001/soco-internationals-latest-move-in-virunga-national-park-leaves-more-questions-than-it-answers|date=11 June 2014|author=Producers of Virunga|website=Virunga official website|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref> alongside other NGOs such as [[Global Witness]],<ref>{{cite web|title=British oil company's back-tracking on World Heritage Site looks like a ruse|url=http://www.globalwitness.org/library/british-oil-company%E2%80%99s-back-tracking-world-heritage-site-looks-ruse|publisher=[[Global Witness]]|date=13 June 2014|first1=Daniel|last1=Balint-Kurti|first2=Nathaniel|last2=Dyer|access-date=20 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141001074144/http://www.globalwitness.org/library/british-oil-company%E2%80%99s-back-tracking-world-heritage-site-looks-ruse|archive-date=1 October 2014|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> [[Human Rights Watch]],<ref>{{cite news|last1=Sawyer|first1=Ida|title=Dispatches: Congo's Virunga Park Rangers, Activists Still Need Justice|url=https://www.hrw.org/news/2014/06/11/dispatches-congo-s-virunga-park-rangers-activists-still-need-justice|publisher=[[Human Rights Watch]]|date=11 June 2014|access-date=3 July 2018}}</ref> and local civil society organisations.<ref>{{cite web|title=Congo Civil Society Express Concerns About SOCO's Deal With WWF on Virunga Oil Exploration|url=http://www.africanconservation.org/in-focus/virunga/item/congo-civil-society-express-concerns-about-soco-s-deal-with-wwf-on-virunga-oil-exploration|publisher=[[African Conservation Foundation]]|access-date=20 August 2014|date=12 June 2014}}</ref>\n\nWorld Wildlife Fund executives now acknowledge that the battle over Virunga is hardly over. campaigns.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/11/16/world/oil-dispute-takes-a-page-from-congos-bloody-past.html?_r=0|title= Oil Dispute Takes a Page From Congo's Bloody Past|date=15 November 2014 |access-date=18 November 2014|work=[[The New York Times]]|first=Jeffrey|last=Gettleman}}</ref>\n\nOn 13 March 2015, BBC reported that the Democratic Republic of Congo says it could
