It premiered on September 1, 2014. The series stars [[Adrien Brody]] as [[Harry Houdini]] and features the life of the legendary [[Magic (illusion)|illusionist]] and [[Escapology|escape artist]] from poverty to worldwide fame.\n\n==Plot==\nThe miniseries follows the man behind the magic as he finds fame, engages in espionage, battles spiritualists and encounters the greatest names of the era. The drama will chronicle the life of a man who can defy death through his stunts, his visions and his mastery of illusion.\n\n==Cast==\n* [[Adrien Brody]] as [[Harry Houdini]], world-famous magician<ref name=\"Hollywood Reporter\"/>\n* [[Kristen Connolly]] as [[Bess Houdini]], Harry's wife<ref name=\"Hollywood Reporter\"/>\n* [[Evan Jones (actor)|Evan Jones]] as Jim Collins, Harry's assistant\n* Eszter \u00d3nodi as Cecilia Weiss, Harry's mother\n* Tom Benedict Knight as [[Theodore Hardeen|Dash Houdini]], Harry's younger brother \n* Louis Mertens as Erich Weiss (young Harry)\n* [[Tim Pigott-Smith]] as [[William Melville]] - It was shot entirely in [[Budapest]], [[Hungary]] (coincidentally the real Harry Houdini's birthplace), which executive producer Gerald W. Abrams described as having \"more turn-of-the-century architecture\u2014that's the 19th century\u2014than almost any city in Western culture.\"<ref name=\"NYPost\">{{cite web |last=Rorke |first=Robert |title=Adrien Brody is a lock star in 'Houdini' miniseries |work=[[New York Post]] |date=August 28, 2014 |url=https://nypost.com/2014/08/28/adrien-brody-is-a-lock-star-in-houdini-miniseries/ |access-date=August 29, 2014}}</ref> Brody, who had studied magic as a child, performed many of the show's stunts himself, including the suspended strait jacket escape and the famous [[Chinese Water Torture Cell]].<ref name=\"NYPost\" /><ref>{{cite web |last=Ryan |first=Patrick |title=Adrien Brody's love of Houdini is no illusion |work=[[USA Today]] |date=August 27, 2014 |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/tv/2014/08/27/adrien-brody-houdini-history-channel-role/14677701/ |access-date=August 29, 2014}}</ref>\n\n===Music===\nThe score to ''Houdini'' was written by the veteran film composer [[John Debney]]. The series currently holds a 44% \"Rotten\" rating on the review aggregator [[Rotten Tomatoes]], with a consensus \"Houdini's cast is talented, but given the decades of fascination surrounding its subject, the show is oddly lacking in intrigue.\"<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/tv/houdini/ |title=Houdini (2014) |access-date=September 16, 2014 |publisher=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]}}</ref>\n\nAllison Keene of ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'' praised Brody's Houdini as possessing \"infectious zest\" and commented that \"... the miniseries nails the most important thing: spectacle. Edel's refreshingly dynamic direction and Brody\u2019s buoyant performance allow Houdini's tricks to retain their wonder, even for the jaded modern viewer. That's a magical feat indeed.\"<ref name=\"HRreview\">{{cite web |last=Keene |first=Allison |title='Houdini': TV Review |work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |date=August 25, 2014 |url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/houdini-tv-review-727566 |access-date=August 28, 2014}}</ref> Despite referring to some aspects of the series as \"a bit heavy-handed,\" Mark Perigard of the ''[[Boston Herald]]'' gave ''Houdini'' a positive \"B\" rating and opined, \"You\u2019ll almost believe in magic\u2014the magic of a miniseries to capture the tics and tricks of a complicated, driven man.\"<ref>{{cite web |last=Perigard |first=Mark |title=History's 'Houdini' has its share of magic moments |work=[[Boston Herald]] |date=August 29, 2014 |url=http://bostonherald.com/entertainment/television/television_reviews/2014/08/history_s_houdini_has_its_share_of_magic_moments |access-date=August 29, 2014}}</ref>\n\nNeil Genzlinger of ''[[The New York Times]]'' called Brody \"a treat to watch,\" but criticized the screenplay and direction.  He added, \"A better version of this amazing life will surely be made in one genre or another, but this one's at least diverting.\"<ref>{{cite web |last=Genzlinger |first=Neil |title=Driven to Break Free From an Ordinary Life: 'Houdini,' With Adrien Brody, on the History Channel |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=August 31, 2014 |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/09/01/arts/television/houdini-with-adrien-brody-on-the-history-channel.html |access-date=September 2, 2014}}</ref> Brian Lowry of ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'', however, spoke less favorably of the series, describing Brody's performance as being \"ultimately overwhelmed\" by Meyer's \"misbegotten, heavy-handed, narrated-ad-nauseam script (...) and Uli Edel's equally obtrusive direction.\"<ref>{{cite web |last=Lowry |first=Brian |title=TV Review: 'Houdini' |work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |date=August 28, 2014 |url=https://variety.com/2014/tv/reviews/tv-review-houdini-1201287194/ |access-date=August 28, 2014}}</ref>\n\nMeyer addressed the voiceovers in a subsequent interview with StarTrek.com, stating \"Well, they \u2013 you know, sort of re-cut the movie, and tore a lot of it out, and put in a lot of voiceovers and stuff that I just wasn't crazy about. And [it] wasn't our intention. It hadn't been written.\"<ref>{{cite web |title=EXCLUSIVE INTERVIEW: Nicholas Meyer on DST3 and Beyond |publisher=StarTrek.com |date=September 25, 2014 |url=http://www.StarTrek.com/article/exclusive-interview-nicholas-meyer-on-dst3-and-beyond |access-date=September 27, 2014}}</ref>\n\n==Awards and nominations==\n{| class=\"wikitable sortable\"\n|-\n!
