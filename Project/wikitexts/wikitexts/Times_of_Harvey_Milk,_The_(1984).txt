The film documents Milk's rise from a neighborhood activist to a symbol of gay political achievement, through to his assassination in November 1978 at San Francisco's city hall, and the [[Dan White]] trial and aftermath.\n\n==Participants==\n;Narrator\n* [[Harvey Fierstein]]\n\n;Interview subjects\n* [[Anne Kronenberg]] (city hall aide to Harvey Milk)\n* Tory Hartmann (political consultant)\n* [[Tom Ammiano]] (schoolteacher)\n* Jim Elliot (auto machinist)\n* Henry Der (executive director, Chinese for Affirmative Action)\n* Jeannine Yeomans (TV reporter)\n* [[Bill Kraus]] (gay activist)\n* [[Sally Miller Gearhart|Sally M. Other politicians including San Francisco mayor [[George Moscone]] (who was assassinated with Milk), and Moscone's successor and now United States Senator [[Dianne Feinstein]] appear in archival footage. The film opens with a tearful Feinstein delivering her announcement to the media that Moscone and Milk had been assassinated by [[Dan White]].\n\nAlso featured in the film is schoolteacher [[Tom Ammiano]], who would go on to be a member of the [[California State Assembly]].\n\n==Awards and honors==\nThe film won the [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]] for [[Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature|Best Documentary Feature]] in 1985,<ref>[https://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/1985 1985|Oscars.org]</ref> and was awarded the Special Jury Prize at the first [[Sundance Film Festival]], among other awards.\n\n==Home media==\nA digitally restored version of the film was released on DVD and Blu-ray by [[The Criterion Collection]] in March 2011. The release includes an audio commentary featuring director [[Rob Epstein]], co-editor Deborah Hoffmann, and photographer [[Daniel Nicoletta]]; a few interview clips and news clips not used in the film; a new interview with documentary filmmaker Jon Else; a new program about ''The Times of Harvey Milk'' and [[Gus Van Sant]]\u2019s 2008 film ''[[Milk (2008 American film)|Milk]]'', featuring Epstein, Van Sant, actor [[James Franco]], and Milk's friends [[Cleve Jones]], Anne Kronenberg, and Nicoletta; a rare collection of audio and video recordings of Milk; excerpts from Epstein's preproduction research tapes of interviews he conducted with a number of people who were ultimately not interviewed for the final film, including Milk's partner [[Scott Smith (activist)|Scott Smith]]; footage from the film's Castro Theatre premiere and the 1984 Academy Awards; a panel discussion from 2003 with Dan White's attorneys; and excerpts from the 25th anniversary commemoration of Milk's and Mayor George Moscone's assassinations.\n\n==References==\n{{reflist}}\n\n==External links==\n* {{IMDb title|0088275}}\n* {{mojo title|timesofharveymilk}}\n* {{rotten-tomatoes|times_of_harvey_milk}}\n*[http://www.criterion.com/current/posts/1789-harvey-s-enduring-legacy ''Harvey\u2019s Enduring Legacy''] an essay by [[Stuart Milk]] at the [[Criterion Collection]]\n*[http://www.criterion.com/current/posts/1788-the-times-of-harvey-milk-making-history ''The Times of Harvey Milk: Making History''] an essay by [[B.
