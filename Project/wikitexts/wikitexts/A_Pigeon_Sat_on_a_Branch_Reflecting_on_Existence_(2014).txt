It is the third installment in his \"Living\" trilogy, following ''[[Songs from the Second Floor]]'' (2000) and ''[[You, the Living]]'' (2007). It premiered at the [[71st Venice International Film Festival]]<ref name=\"Venice\">{{cite web |url=http://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema/71st-festival/line-up/off-sel/venezia71/ |title=International competition of feature films |access-date=24 July 2014 |work=Venice |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006083927/http://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema/71st-festival/line-up/off-sel/venezia71/ |archive-date=October 6, 2014 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref><ref name=\"Deadline\">{{cite web |url=https://www.deadline.com/2014/07/venice-film-festival-lineup-2014-movie-list/ |title=Venice Film Festival Lineup Announced |access-date=24 July 2014 |work=Deadline|date=July 24, 2014 }}</ref> where it was awarded the [[Golden Lion]] for Best Film.<ref>{{cite news|title=Roy Andersson film scoops Venice Golden Lion award|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-29098103|access-date=7 September 2014|work=BBC News|date=7 September 2014}}</ref> It was selected as the Swedish entry for the [[Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film|Best Foreign Language Film]] at the [[88th Academy Awards]] but it was not nominated.<ref name=\"Sweden\">{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2015/film/global/sweden-sends-roy-anderssons-pigeon-to-foreign-language-oscar-race-1201582628/ |title=Sweden Sends Roy Andersson's 'Pigeon' to Foreign-Language Oscar Race |work=Variety |date=1 September 2015 |access-date=1 September 2015}}</ref><ref name=\"HWR\">{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/oscars-sweden-picks-a-pigeon-819278 |title=Oscars: Sweden Picks 'A Pigeon Sat on a Branch' for Foreign Language Category |last=Roxborough |first=Scott |work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |date=1 September 2015 |access-date=1 September 2015}}</ref>\n\nIts title is a reference to the 1565 painting ''[[The Hunters in the Snow]]'' by [[Pieter Bruegel the Elder]]. The painting depicts a rural wintertime scene, with some birds perched on tree branches. Andersson said he imagined that the birds in the scene are watching the people below, wondering what they are doing. He explained the title of the film as a \"different way of saying 'what are we actually doing', that's what the movie is about.\"<ref>{{cite news |date=27 January 2013 |url=http://www.svd.se/kultur/roy-andersson-siktar-pa-guldpalmen_7863666.svd |title=Roy Andersson siktar p\u00e5 Guldpalmen |newspaper=[[Svenska Dagbladet]] |language=sv|quote=Det \u00e4r egentligen bara en slags omskrivning f\u00f6r 'vad h\u00e5ller vi p\u00e5 med egentligen', det \u00e4r det som filmen handlar om.}}</ref> At the Venice Film Festival, Andersson said that the film had been inspired by the 1948 Italian film ''[[Bicycle Thieves]]'' by [[Vittorio De Sica]].<ref name=\"BBCAwards\">{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-29098103 |title=Roy Andersson film scoops Venice Golden Lion award |access-date=7 September 2014 |work=BBC News|date=September 7, 2014 }}</ref>\n\n== Plot ==\n\nThe [[slow cinema]] movie, hyper reality, consists of a series of mostly self-contained [[Scene (drama)|tableaux]], sometimes connected by recurring themes or characters.
