was after his untimely death. Her world is suddenly disrupted by the presence of his lover. Together, they attempt to overcome their grief whilst struggling against not having a shared language.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.screendaily.com/news/uk-ireland/tony-grisonis-kingsland-amongst-new-microwave-projects/5042214.article|title=Tony Grisoni's Kingsland amongst new Microwave projects|publisher=}}</ref>\n\n== Cast ==\n* [[Ben Whishaw]] as Richard\n* [[Cheng Pei-pei]] as Junn\n* Naomi Christie as Vann\n* [[Peter Bowles]] as Alan\n* [[Morven Christie]] as Margaret\n* Andrew Leung as Kai\n\n== Production ==\nThe script, originally titled ''Lilting the Past'', won third spot in the 2011 Brit List, a list of the best unproduced British screenplays.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.variety.com/article/VR1118045861|title='Call Up' tops Brit List|work=Variety|date=9 November 2011}}</ref>\n\nThe film was one of three films greenlit by Microwave in early 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.variety.com/article/VR1118054312?refCatId=1061|title=Microwave heats up three pics|work=Variety}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://unitedagents.co.uk/node/5328|title=Hong Khaou's LILTING announced among new Microwave projects \u2013 United Agents|publisher=}}</ref> A casting call was later released for the three lead roles,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chinatownartsspace.com/files/casting_for_lilting.pdf |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2012-11-29 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121113150536/http://www.chinatownartsspace.com/files/casting_for_lilting.pdf |archivedate=13 November 2012 }}</ref> later filled by [[Cheng Pei-pei]], [[Ben Whishaw]], and Andrew Leung.\n\nFilming began in November 2012 <ref>http://filmlondon.org.uk/news/2012/may/three_new_films_for_microwave {{Dead link|date=February 2022}}</ref> and completed principal photography in December 2012.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://filmlondon.org.uk/news/2014/january/lilting_premieres_to_critical_acclaim |title=Film London - News |access-date=21 February 2015 |archive-date=25 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160325072328/http://filmlondon.org.uk/news/2014/january/lilting_premieres_to_critical_acclaim |url-status=dead }}</ref> Director Khaou has said the film will be visually inspired by [[Wong Kar-wai]]'s ''[[In the Mood for Love]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://microwave.filmlondon.org.uk/get_inspired/lilting/directors_vision/|title=Microwave \u2013 Get Inspired \u2013 Lilting \u2013 Directors Vision|publisher=}}</ref>\n\nDuring production, as part of the Microwave scheme, Michael Winterbottom mentored writer/director Khaou, while producer Buchanan was mentored by Ken Marshall, producer of ''[[London to Brighton]]'', ''[[Filth (film)|Filth]]'' and ''[[Song for Marion]]''. As with all Microwave films, the budget was \u00a3120,000.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.screendaily.com/news/production/microwaves-lilting-starring-ben-whishaw-underway-in-london/5049666.article?blocktitle=Latest-news&contentID=1846|title=Microwave's Lilting, starring Ben Whishaw, underway in London|publisher=}}</ref> It is the first bilingual film to be made under the Microwave scheme.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/news/celebritynews/ben-whishaw-goes-from-skyfall-q-to-budget-film-8399271.html|title=Ben Whishaw goes from Skyfall Q to budget film|work=The Evening Standard|date=10 December 2012}}</ref>\n\n== Reception ==\n[[File:\"Lilting\" Wins the World Cinema Dramatic Cinematography Award (12186021145).jpg|200px|thumb|upright|right|''Lilting'' won the Cinematography Award at the [[2014 Sundance Film Festival]].]]\n\n=== Critical response ===\n''Lilting'' was met with positive reviews from critics. {{As of|2020|06}}, the film holds an 84% approval rating on review aggregator [[Rotten Tomatoes]], based on 55 reviews with an average rating of 6.88/10. The website's critics consensus reads: \"Skillfully weaving multiple delicate tonal strands into a quietly affecting whole, ''Lilting'' serves as a thoroughly compelling calling card for writer-director Hong Khaou.\"<ref>{{cite web |title=Lilting (2014)|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/lilting/|work=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|publisher=[[Flixster]]|accessdate=19 August 2014}}</ref> On [[Metacritic]] the film has 61 rating from 22 reviews indicating \"Generally favorable reviews.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/movie/lilting|title=Lilting|publisher=|accessdate=29 June 2020|via=www.metacritic.com}}</ref>\n\nJustin Chang, in his review for ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'', said that the film \"Hong Khaou makes a fine debut with this quietly resonant cross-cultural chamber piece.\"<ref>{{cite web |title=Sundance Film Review: 'Lilting'|date=17 January 2014|url=https://variety.com/2014/film/reviews/sundance-film-review-lilting-1201061892/|accessdate=19 January 2014}}</ref> David Rooney of ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'' praised the film, saying, \"Delicate and unhurried almost to a fault, though also hauntingly sexy and even humorous at times.\"<ref>{{cite web |title=Lilting: Sundance Review|website=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|date=17 January 2014|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/lilting-sundance-review-671773|accessdate=19 January 2014}}</ref> Amber Wilkinson from ''[[The Daily Telegraph|Telegraph]]'' gave the film three out of five stars and praised the lead actor that \"Whishaw is magnetic as a man pushed to the edge of fragility by mourning, but who still suggests a quiet strength.\"<ref>{{cite web |title=Sundance 2014: Lilting, review|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/film/filmreviews/10581409/Sundance-2014-Lilting-review.html|accessdate=19 January 2014}}</ref> Dominic Mill of ''We Got This Covered'' gave a positive review and said, \"The subject matter is powerful, and the performances are wonderful \u2013 in a world of big and showy dramatism, Lilting gets its point across without feeling the need to shout about it.\"<ref>{{cite web |title=Lilting Review [LLGBT 2014]|url=https://wegotthiscovered.com/movies/lilting-london-lgbt-film-festival/|accessdate=19 January 2014}}</ref>\n\n===
