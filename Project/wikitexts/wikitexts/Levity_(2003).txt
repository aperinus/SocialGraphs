The soundtrack was composed by [[Mark Oliver Everett]] of the band [[Eels (band)|Eels]]. ''Levity'' was filmed in [[Montreal|Montreal, Quebec]], Canada. It was the opening film at the [[2003 Sundance Film Festival]]<ref name=hello>{{cite news |title=Sundance Festival Kicks Off with Murderous 'Levity'|url=https://www.hellomagazine.com/film/2003/01/17/sundance/ |access-date=16 October 2022 |work=Hello |date=January 17, 2003}}</ref> and went into a limited release on April 4, 2003.<ref name=\"allmovie\">{{cite web |title=Levity (2003) |url=https://www.allmovie.com/movie/levity-v276389 |website=[[AllMovie]] |access-date=16 October 2022}}</ref>\n\n== Plot ==\n{{more plot|reason=ending needed|date=November 2020}}\nManual Jordan, a man who served nearly 23 years<!-- One year, five months plus 21 years, six months --> for killing a teenager during an attempted robbery, is released on parole in spite of his objections. After spending his time staring at a clipping of Abner Easley, the boy he killed, he returns to the city he used to live in to find redemption. He ends up living at a community house which is run by Miles Evans, a preacher. He offers Manual work so he can pay for the room, and Manual places Abner's photo in his room to remind himself of his crime.\n\nWhile staying at the community house, he befriends Sofia Mellinger, a wild young woman with no adult figure in her life. Manual also encounters Adele Easley, the elder sister of Abner. She does not recognize Manual and in his pursuit for forgiveness, he forms a friendship with her, and their relationship begins to develop.\n\nManual gets his chance for redemption when Adele's rebellious teenage son becomes involved in violence. Manual tries to befriend him and steer him in the right direction, ever mindful of the past.\n\n== Cast ==\n{{Cast listing|\n* [[Billy Bob Thornton]] as Manual Jordan\n* [[Morgan Freeman]] as Miles Evans\n* [[Holly Hunter]] as Adele Easley\n* [[Kirsten Dunst]] as Sofia Mellinger\n* [[Manuel Aranguiz]] as Senor Aguilar\n* [[Geoffrey Wigdor]] as Abner Easley\n** [[Luke Robertson]] as young Abner \n* [[Dorian Harewood]] as Mackie Whittaker\n* Catherine Colvey as Claire Mellinger\n* Billoah Greene as Don\n* Sadiki Burke as Sadiki\n* Abede Burke as Abede\n* [[Diego Abella]] as Raul\n* Brent Rogers as Ty\n* Cordell Clyde as Cleve\n* [[David Acer]] as Comedian\n}}\n\n==Production==\nWriter and director Ed Solomon crafted the story based on an early experience he had as a tutor for teenagers in prison. \"I met this kid who had killed somebody and had been tried as an adult and sentenced to life in prison. And he kept a photograph of the person he had killed. The judge had told him to keep this and have it...He would stare at it like he didn\u2019t know it was a human being, like he was trying to take this two-dimensional image and have it become three dimensional. And then he was gone; he turned 18 and he went to the state prison.\"<ref name=\"patheos\">{{cite web |last1=Overstreet |first1=Jeffrey |title=The Hard Work of \u201cLevity\u201d: A Conversation with Director Ed Solomon |url=https://www.patheos.com/blogs/lookingcloser/2008/11/levity-an-interview-with-director-ed-solomon/ |website=Patheos |access-date=16 October 2022 |date=November 24, 2008}}</ref><ref name=\"austin\">{{cite news |last1=Hepola |first1=Sarah |title=Seeking Levity |url=https://www.austinchronicle.com/screens/2003-07-11/167766/ |access-date=16 October 2022 |work=Austin Chronicle |date=July 11, 2003}}</ref> \n\nHaving written the screenplays for comedy films like ''[[Bill & Ted\u2019s Excellent Adventure]]'' and ''[[Men in Black (1997 film)|Men in Black]]'', Solomon originally envisioned a lighter take on the story, where Miles and Manual take charge of kids who aspire to be comedians.
