''Electric Boogaloo'' was released seven months after its predecessor by [[TriStar Pictures]]. In some international locations the film was released under the title '''''Breakdance 2: Electric Boogaloo'''''. Another sequel, ''[[Rappin']]'' (also known as ''Breakdance 3'') was made but had an unconnected plot and different lead characters \u2013 only [[Ice-T]] features in all three films.\n\n==Plot==\nThe three main dancers from ''[[Breakin']]'' \u2013 Kelly \"Special K\" Bennett ([[Lucinda Dickey]]), Orlando \"Ozone\" Barco ([[Adolfo Quinones]]), and Tony \"Turbo\" Ainley ([[Michael Chambers]]) \u2013 struggle to stop the demolition of a community recreation center by a developer who wants to build a shopping mall.
