The film is about the intersecting lives of several [[Cinema of the United States|Hollywood]] players and wannabes.<ref name=\"Hurlyburly\">{{cite web|url=http://www.tcm.com/tcmdb/title/415910/Hurly-Burly/full-credits.html|title=Hurlyburly|work=[[Turner Classic Movies]]|accessdate=May 22, 2016}}</ref> Rabe condensed the action of his three-hour plus play into two hours and updated the setting from the mid-1980s to the late 1990s.\n\n==Plot==\n{{See also|Hurlyburly}}\n\nEddie, a cocaine-addicted womanizing [[Casting (performing arts)|casting director]], lives in the Hollywood Hills, where his friend Mickey has  come to stay with him temporarily after a fallout with his wife. Along with their buddies, Artie and out-of-work actor Phil, Eddie and Mickey live a life of decadence and immorality. Eddie is in love with Darlene, but she is also seeing the married Mickey. Eddie comes to question his lifestyle and purpose, while Mickey is content with his situation.
