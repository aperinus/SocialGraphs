The TV version ran at around ninety minutes, cutting out nearly an hour of content.\n\n==Content==\nThe documentary is strictly chronological, divided into five parts, from the beginning of George Lucas' career as a filmmaker, to the complicated production of ''Star Wars'' in 1976, to the impact of the film, and its sequels, have made on the world today. The documentary puts ''Star Wars'' into a sociological and political perspective by using interviews with spectators such as [[Walter Cronkite]].\n\nThe film was shot in 1.78:1 [[aspect ratio (image)|aspect ratio]].\n\nThe documentary is available for streaming on [[Disney+]], but was [[Changes in Star Wars re-releases|edited]] to show a newer version of [[Darth Vader]]'s duel with [[Obi-Wan Kenobi]] from ''[[A New Hope]]''.<ref>''Empire of Dreams: The Story of the Star Wars Trilogy'' (2004).
