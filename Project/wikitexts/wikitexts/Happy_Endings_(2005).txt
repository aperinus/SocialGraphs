The film\u2019s plot uses [[Hyperlink cinema|interconnected storylines]] to tell three stories of Los Angeles natives that center around love and family. \n\nThe expression \"happy ending\" is a [[colloquialism|colloquial]] term for [[Erotic massage|offering sexual release]] to a client at the end of a massage.\n\n==Plot==\nThe film follows a diverse group of mostly middle-class residents of [[Los Angeles]] through the emotional ups and downs in their lives, loosely connected to each other through a restaurant.\n\nIn the first story, Mamie reluctantly agrees to work with a would-be young filmmaker in order to locate the now-grown son she secretly gave up for adoption after becoming pregnant from her stepbrother Charley{{spnd}}who is later revealed to be gay{{spnd}}19 years earlier.\n\nIn the second story, Charley and his domestic partner, Gil, are deciding whether or not to confront their friends, a lesbian couple, regarding the paternity of their son.\n\nAnd in the third, a young man, Otis, is involved with a band and trying to keep his father, Frank, from learning that he is gay, while also dealing with a seemingly gold-digging woman, Jude, who inserts herself into their lives.\n\n==Cast==\n{{div col}}\n* [[Lisa Kudrow]] as Mamie Toll\n**[[Hallee Hirsh]] as Mamie Toll (age 17)\n* [[Steve Coogan]] as Charley Peppitone\n* [[Tom Arnold (actor)|Tom Arnold]] as
