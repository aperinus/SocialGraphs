Starring [[Madonna]], [[Rupert Everett]], and [[Benjamin Bratt]], it opened to the number-two position in the North American box office and poor critical reviews.\n\n==Plot==\nTwo best friends{{spaced ndash}}a heterosexual woman Abbie and a gay man Robert{{spaced ndash}} decide to have a child together. Five years later, Abbie falls in love with a heterosexual man and wants to move away with him and Robert's little boy Sam, and a nasty custody battle ensues.\n\n==Cast==\n* [[Madonna]] as Abigail \"Abbie\" Reynolds\n* [[Rupert Everett]] as Robert Whittaker\n* [[Benjamin Bratt]] as Benjamin \"Ben\" Cooper\n* [[Michael Vartan]] as Kevin Lasater\n* [[Josef Sommer]] as Richard Whittaker\n* [[Lynn Redgrave]] as Helen Whittaker\n* Malcolm Stumpf as Samuel \"Sam\"\n* [[Neil Patrick Harris]] as David\n* [[Illeana Douglas]] as Elizabeth Ryder\n* [[Mark Valley]] as Cardiologist\n* [[Suzanne Krull]] as Annabel\n* [[Stacy Edwards]] as Finn\n* [[William Mesnik]] as Ashby\n* [[Linda Larkin]] as Kelly\n\n==Production==\nThe film began as an original screenplay titled ''The Red Curtain'' by [[Tom Ropelewski]], which he intended to direct, with his wife [[Leslie Dixon]] to produce. It was announced to be made in 1995 with [[Richard Dreyfuss]] attached to star as Robert; he dropped out, then [[Helen Hunt]] was named as female lead to play Abbie. She was replaced by Madonna and then [[Rupert Everett]] signed on as star. Filming took place between 23 April and 30 June 1999. It later was claimed the script was rewritten extensively by [[Ryan Murphy (writer)|Ryan Murphy]] and Rupert Everett.<ref name=\"nat\">Nat Segaloff, ''Final Cuts: The Last Films of 50 Great Directors'', Bear Manor Media 2013 p 258-260</ref>\n\n==Reception==\nRoger Ebert gave the film one star, stating: \"''The Next Best Thing'' is a garage sale of gay issues, harnessed to a plot as exhausted as a junkman's horse.\"<ref>{{cite news|last=Ebert |first=Roger |author-link=Roger Ebert |url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/the-next-best-thing-2000 |title=The Next Best Thing|publisher=Ebert Digital LLC |work=RogerEbert.com |date= 3 March 2000|access-date=6 March 2018}}</ref>\n\nThe film received a 19% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes, based on 94 reviews, with an average rating of 3.8/10. The website's critical consensus states: \"Story elements clash and acting falls short.\"<ref>{{rotten-tomatoes|next_best_thing|The Next Best Thing}}</ref> On Metacritic, the film has rating of 25/100 based on 31 reviews, indicating \"generally unfavorable reviews\".<ref>{{Metacritic film|title=The Next Best Thing}}</ref>\n\n===Box office===\nThe film opened at number two at the North American box office, making [[United States dollar|USD]]$5,870,387, behind ''[[The Whole Nine Yards (film)|The Whole Nine Yards]]''.
