It was nominated for [[Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature|Best Documentary Feature]] for the [[78th Academy Awards]].<ref name=\"NY Times\">{{cite web |url=https://movies.nytimes.com/movie/318807/Murderball/details |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090211031759/http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/318807/Murderball/details |url-status=dead |archive-date=2009-02-11 |department=Movies & TV Dept. |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=2009 |title=NY Times: Murderball |access-date=2008-11-23}}</ref> ''Murderball'' was the first and only [[MTV Films|MTV film]] released through [[ThinkFilm|THINKFilm]] as well as [[Participant Media]].\n\n==UN screening==\nThe film was screened at the [[United Nations]] uncut. One of its stars, [[Mark Zupan (sportsman)|Mark Zupan]], winced when describing how embarrassed he was to have his mother hear his remarks on the [[Sexuality after spinal cord injury|sex lives of persons with quadriplegia]].\n\n==Production==\n''Murderball'' was shot on a low budget. The main camera used was a [[Panasonic AG-DVX100]]; a [[Sony camcorder#Sony DCR-VX2000 & Sony DSR-PD150|Sony PD150]] was used to shoot some of the early interviews. The crew rigged a Sennheiser shotgun microphone to use as a boom, and relied heavily on Lavaliere wireless microphones as well. Available lighting was used almost exclusively. Additional light was provided using an inexpensive [[china ball]]. In one example of on-the-spot lighting, a flashlight was diffused using only a napkin.<ref>[http://www.doublefeatureshow.com/2009/06/murderball-monster-man.html \"Murderball + Monster Man\"]. Double Feature. June 4, 2009</ref>\n\n==Reception==\n''Murderball'' garnered almost universally positive reviews.
