Using a mix of film of the Zephyr skateboard team (Z-Boys) shot in the 1970s by [[Craig Stecyk]], along with contemporary interviews, the documentary tells the story of a group of teenage surfer/skateboarders and their influence on the history of skateboarding (and to a lesser extent [[surfing]]) culture.\n\n==Synopsis==\n''Dogtown and Z-Boys'', narrated by [[Sean Penn]], begins with the history of [[skateboarding]] in [[Southern California]] and how it had been strongly influenced by the [[surf culture]] in the surrounding areas of [[Santa Monica, California|Santa Monica]] and [[Venice, California|Venice]], nicknamed ''[[Santa Monica neighborhoods#Ocean_Park_Neighborhood|Dogtown]]''.<ref name=nytimes>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9D06E3DC153EF935A15757C0A9649C8B63|title=Dogtown and Z Boys (2001) FILM REVIEW; Skating on Top of the World During an Endless Summer| author=Stephen Holden|date=April 26, 2002|work=The New York Times|access-date=July 27, 2014}}</ref> Surf shop owners [[Jeff Ho Surfboards and Zephyr Productions|Jeff Ho]], [[Skip Engblom]], and [[Craig Stecyk]] established the Zephyr Skateboard Team with local teenagers from broken homes.<ref name=hollywood>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywood.com/static/lords-of-dogtown-interviews-tony-alva-skip-engblom-and-stacy-peralta|title=\"LORDS OF DOGTOWN\" INTERVIEWS: TONY ALVA, SKIP ENGBLOM AND STACY|publisher=Hollywood.com|access-date=July 27, 2014}}</ref> The sport of skateboarding continued to evolve as the [[Z-Boys]] continued to bring edgy moves influenced by surfing. During one of California's record-breaking droughts, local backyard pools were emptied and became hotspots for these young skateboarders looking for places to skateboard.<ref name=nytimes/> The members of the Zephyr team gained notability and national attention when they competed in skateboard championships and started to receive media attention for their skills as young athletes. Testimonials and commentary provided by the members and founders of the Zephyr team combined with the rock-and-roll soundtrack and vintage footage all come together in this documentary about the history and lives of the original Z-Boys and skateboarding subculture of California.<ref name=cave>{{cite web|url=http://skateboard.about.com/od/videosreviews/fr/DogtownZBoysV.htm|title=Dogtown and Z-Boys DVD Documentary Review|author=Steve Cave|publisher=About.com|access-date=July 27, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140713171616/http://skateboard.about.com/od/videosreviews/fr/DogtownZBoysV.htm|archive-date=July 13, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>\n\n==Production==\nThe documentary features vintage video footage and photos of the Zephyr skateboard team from the 1970s, along with contemporary interviews from the original members of the Z-Boys group.<ref name=sundance>{{cite web|url=http://history.sundance.org/films/2222/dogtown_and_z-boys|title=Dogtown and Z-Boys|date=2002|publisher= Sundance Institute|access-date=July 27, 2014}}</ref> The film combines the 8-mm and 16-mm vintage footage with modern editing and a soundtrack crafted from music of the 1970s era.<ref name=sundance/>\n\n''Dogtown and Z-Boys'' was directed by [[Stacy Peralta]], an original member of the Zephyr team, and written by Peralta and Craig Stecyk, a leading surf and skateboard film producer and photojournalist.<ref name=nytimes/>\n\nThe film operated on a budget of $400,000 financed by [[Vans|Vans, Inc.]]<ref>{{cite journal |first = Steve |last = Olson |year = 2001 |title = Dogtown Chronicles: Stacy Peralta interview |journal = Juice Magazine |issue = 53 |publisher =[[Juice (skateboarding magazine)]] |url = http://juicemagazine.com/home/dogtown-chronicles-stacy-peralta/ |access-date = September 1, 2001}}</ref> Stecyk and photojournalist [[Glen E. Friedman]], were the film's co-writer and co-producer, respectively,<ref>{{cite journal |id={{ProQuest|2356719}} |last1=Steinhart |first1=Daniel |title=Stacy Peralta: Dogtown and Z-Boys |journal=Independent Film & Video Monthly |volume=25 |issue=3 |date=April 2002 |page=12 }}</ref> Daniel Ostroff and Stephen Nemeth were also co-producers, and Debra MacCulloch and Christine Triano were associate producers involved with the film.<ref name=IMDB/>\n\n==Cast==\nThe documentary includes footage, commentary, and interviews from eleven of the original members of the Z-Boys team, along with the team's co-founders, skateboarding champions, and other relevant skateboarding figures, journalists, and musicians from the era.<ref name=IMDB>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0275309/fullcredits?ref_=ttco_ql_1|title=Dogtown and Z-Boys (2001)|publisher=IMDb|access-date=July 27, 2014}}</ref>\n\n* [[Sean Penn]] as the narrator \n* [[Jay Adams]] (Zephyr Skate Team member) as himself\n* [[Tony Alva]] (Zephyr Skate Team member) as himself\n* [[Stacy Peralta]] (Zephyr Skate Team member) as himself\n* [[Jeff Ament]] as himself\n* [[Steve Caballero]] as himself \n* [[Skip Engblom]] (Zephyr Co-Founder) as himself\n* [[Craig Stecyk]] (Zephyr Co-Founder) as himself\n* [[Tony Hawk]] as himself\n* [[Henry Rollins]] as himself\n* [[Tom Sims]] as himself\n* [[Peggy Oki]] (Zephyr Skate Team member) as herself\n* [[Jeff Ho Surfboards and Zephyr Productions|Jeff Ho]] (Zephyr Co-Founder) as himself\n\n==Reception==\nThe documentary initially gained notability after its debut at the [[Sundance Film Festival]] where it won several awards. The film was well received by many critics, including reporter Steve McKee of ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'' who stated that the documentary had opened with \"boffo reviews\" from around the country.<ref name=wsj>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/news/articles/SB1020090914919364040|title=Sun's Shining, Pool's Empty: It's a Great Day for the Z-Boys|author=Steve McKee|date=April 29, 2001|publisher=Wall Street Journal|access-date=July 27, 2014}}</ref> The film received a rating of 92% on [[Rotten Tomatoes]] and a generally favorable rating of 76 on [[Metacritic]].<ref name=rottentom>{{cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/dogtown_and_zboys/|title=Dogtown and Z-Boys (2001)|publisher=Rotten Tomatoes|access-date=July 27, 2014}}</ref><ref name=metacritic>{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/movie/dogtown-and-z-boys|title=Dogtown and Z-Boys|work=[[Metacritic]]|publisher=[[CBS Interactive]]|access-date=July 27, 2014}}</ref> Stephen Holden of ''[[The New York Times]]'' said the film was a \"giddy, thrilling, rock 'n' roll-saturated history of skateboarding in Southern California.\"<ref name=nytimes/>\n\nOn the opening weekend of April 2002, ''Dogtown and Z-Boys'' made $103,355.
