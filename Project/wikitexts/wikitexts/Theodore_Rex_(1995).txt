Abramson filed a US$20 million lawsuit against Goldberg, which was settled quickly. Goldberg agreed to star in the film for $7 million,<ref name=\"love_people\" /> $2 million more than the amount originally agreed upon.<ref name=\"cox_variety\" />\n\nOne of the attorneys on the case described this as being similar to the legal battle of [[Kim Basinger]] when she backed out of the film ''[[Boxing Helena]]''.<ref name=\"cox_variety\" />\n\n== Distribution ==\nThe film was originally intended for theatrical release in North America during Christmas 1995, but a glut of competition as well as a rush on post production work for the effects heavy film led to [[New Line Cinema]] delaying release.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/1995/more/news/h-w-d-banks-on-xmas-laugh-riot-99128561/|title=H'w'd banks on Xmas laugh riot|date=9 October 1995}}</ref> They subsequently intended to release it to coincide with Goldberg's hosting stint at the [[68th Academy Awards|Academy Awards]] the following year,<ref name=\"releasedate\" /> but ultimately decided that it was in their best interests to release the film [[direct-to-video]].
