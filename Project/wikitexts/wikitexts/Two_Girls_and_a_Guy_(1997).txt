It was shot almost entirely in real time, and within a single setting, leading some reviewers to compare the film to a stage play.<ref name=\"Variety\" />\n\nDirector James Toback told interviewers he wrote the film's screenplay in only four days and shot the film in eleven days.<ref name=\"latimes-review\" />\n\n== Plot ==\nTwo girls, Carla and Lou meet on the street outside a loft waiting for their boyfriends. After a short time, they find out that they're waiting for the same guy \u2013 young actor Blake, who said that he loves both of them but had actually been leading a double life for several months. Angry, they break into his loft and when he returns, a round of accusations and explanations begins.\n\n== Cast ==\n* [[Robert Downey Jr.]] as Blake Allen\n* [[Heather Graham]] as Carla Bennett\n* [[Natasha Gregson Wagner]] as Lou\n* [[Frederique van der Wal]] as Carol\n*  Angel David as Tommy\n\n== Production ==\n===Casting===\nAlongside ''[[The Gingerbread Man (film)|The Gingerbread Man]]'', the film was [[Robert Downey Jr.]]'s first role after a 1996 drug arrest.
