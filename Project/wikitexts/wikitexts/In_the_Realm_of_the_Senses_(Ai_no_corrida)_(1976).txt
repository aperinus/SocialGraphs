coproduction of France and Japan, the film generated great [[controversy]] at the time of its release.<ref name=\"Buckley\"/> While intended for mainstream [[wide release]], the film contains scenes of [[Unsimulated sex|unsimulated sexual activity]] between the actors ([[Eiko Matsuda]] and [[Tatsuya Fuji]], among others).\n\n==Plot==\nIn 1936 Tokyo, Sada Abe is a former prostitute who now works as a maid in a hotel. The hotel's owner Kichizo Ishida [[Sexual assault|molests]] her, and the two begin an intense affair that consists of sexual experiments and various self-indulgences. Ishida leaves his wife to pursue his affair with Sada. Sada becomes increasingly possessive and jealous of Ishida, and Ishida more eager to please her. Their mutual obsession escalates until Ishida finds that she is most excited by [[Erotic asphyxiation|strangling him during lovemaking]], and he is killed in this fashion. Sada then [[Penis removal|severs his penis]]. While she is shown next to him naked, it is mentioned that she will walk around with his penis inside her for several days. Words written with blood can be read on his chest: \"Sada Kichi the two of us forever.\"\n\n==Cast==\n{{Cast listing|\n* [[Eiko Matsuda]] as [[Sada Abe]]\n* [[Tatsuya Fuji]] as Kichiz\u014d Ishida\n* Aoi Nakajima as Toku\n* Yasuko Matsui as Tagawa Inn manager\n* Meika Seri as Matsuko\n* Kanae Kobayashi as Old geisha Kikury\u016b\n* [[Taiji Tonoyama]] as Old beggar\n* Ky\u00f4ji Kokonoe as Teacher \u014cmiya\n* Naomi Shiraishi as Geisha Yaeji\n* Komikichi Hori as Mitsuwa Geisha\n}}\n\n==Title==\nThe film was released under the title of ''In the Realm of the Senses'' in the U.S. and the U.K., and under ''L'Empire des sens'' (''Empire of the Senses'') in France. The French title was taken from [[Roland Barthes]]'s book about Japan, ''L'Empire des signes'' (''Empire of Signs'', 1970).<ref name=Turim/>\n\nThe original Japanese title ''Ai no Kor\u012bda'' was later reimagined as the title of the 1979 funk song \"[[Ai No Corrida (song)|Ai No Corrida]]\", originally recorded by [[Chaz Jankel]] and later brought to success by [[Quincy Jones]] in 1981.<ref>{{cite book|author=Simon Matthews |title=Looking for a New England|url={{Google Books |rHMLEAAAQBAJ|page=1842|plain-url=yes}}|date=2021|isbn=9780857304124|page=1842 |via=Google Books}}</ref>\n\n==Censorship==\nStrict censorship laws in Japan would not have allowed the film to be made according to \u014cshima's vision.<ref name=\"Buckley\"/> This obstruction was bypassed by officially listing the production as a French enterprise, and the undeveloped footage was shipped to France for processing and editing. At its premiere in Japan, the film's sexual activity was optically [[censorship in Japan|censored]] using reframing and blurring.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.criterion.com/current/posts/1108-in-the-realm-of-the-senses-some-notes-on-oshima-and-pornography|title=In the Realm of the Senses: Some Notes on Oshima and Pornography|work=The Criterion Collection}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/04/26/movies/homevideo/26kehr.html|title=Nagisa Oshima's Realm of Restraint and Precision|date=26 April 2009|work=The New York Times}}</ref>\n\nIn the United States, the film was initially banned upon its premiere at the 1976 [[New York Film Festival]]<ref name=\"bbcnews\">{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-21030965 |title=In the Realm of the Senses director Nagisa Oshima dies at 80 |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=2013-01-15 |access-date=2019-05-16 |quote=The film fell foul of censors in Germany, the UK and the US - where it was seized by customs officials ahead of a planned screening at the New York Film Festival. }}</ref> but was later screened uncut, and a similar fate awaited the film when it was released in Germany. It was also banned because of a scene in which Kichi pushes an [[egg]] into Sada's [[vulva]], forcing her to push it out of her [[vagina]] before Kichi eats the egg. The film was not available on home video until 1990, although it was sometimes seen uncut in [[Film society|film clubs]].\n\nAt the time, the only European country in which the film was banned was [[Belgium]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cinemazuid.be/nl/film/het-rijk-der-zinnen-l%E2%80%99empire-des-sensai-no-corrida |title=Cinemazuid |website=Cinemazuid.be |date=2013-04-25 |access-date=2016-01-11 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160125215805/http://www.cinemazuid.be/nl/film/het-rijk-der-zinnen-l%E2%80%99empire-des-sensai-no-corrida |archive-date=2016-01-25 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://cobra.canvas.be/cm/cobra/videozone/dagopdag2014/november%2B2014/1.2144641|title=Video: Op 17 november 1977 zond de VRT dit uit|work=Cobra.be|language=nl|access-date=2016-02-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160302220940/http://cobra.canvas.be/cm/cobra/videozone/dagopdag2014/november%2B2014/1.2144641|archive-date=2016-03-02|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dbnl.org/tekst/_ons003198601_01/_ons003198601_01_0134.php |title=Ons Erfdeel. Jaargang 29 \u00b7 dbnl |website=Dbnl.org |access-date=2016-01-11}}</ref> The ban was lifted in 1994,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nova-cinema.org/prog/2013/139b-and-cut/belgique-ceci-n-est-pas-de-la/?lang=nl|title=Nova Cinema|date=11 June 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmmagie.be/nieuws/adieu-nagisa-oshima-1932-2013|title=Adieu Nagisa Oshima (1932\u20132013) - Filmmagie|access-date=14 May 2019|archive-date=5 August 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200805043517/https://filmmagie.be/nieuws/adieu-nagisa-oshima-1932-2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> and Belgium has not censored a film of any kind since.\n\nAt the time of its initial screening at the 1976 [[BFI London Film Festival|London Film Festival]], the [[British Board of Film Classification|British Board of Film Censors]] recommended that it be shown under private cinema club conditions to avoid the need for heavy cuts, but only after the [[Obscene Publications Act 1959|Obscene Publications Act]] had been extended to films in 1977 to avoid potential legal problems.<ref name=\"BBFC\">[http://www.sbbfc.co.uk/CaseStudies/LEmpire_des_Sens_In_The_Realm_Of_The_Senses Case Study: ''L'Empire des Sens (In The Realm Of The Senses)''] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110113181511/http://www.sbbfc.co.uk/CaseStudies/LEmpire_des_Sens_In_The_Realm_Of_The_Senses |date=13 January 2011 }}, Students' British Board of Film Classification page</ref> The film opened at the Gate Cinema Club in 1978. It was given an official countrywide cinema release in 1991, though the video release was delayed until 2000 when it was passed with an \"18\" certificate (suitable for adults only). All of the adult sexual activity was left intact, but a shot in which Sada yanks the penis of a prepubescent boy after he misbehaves was reframed, zooming in so that only the reaction of the boy was shown.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.movie-censorship.com/report.php?ID=372337|title=In The Realm of the Senses (Comparison: BBFC 18 - US Blu-ray) - Movie-Censorship.com|first=Gerald|last=Wurm}}</ref> In [[Australia]], the film was originally banned, but a censored version was made available in 1977. In 2000, it finally became available in its complete version.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.refused-classification.com/censorship/films/in-the-realm-of-the-senses-1976.html|title=In the Realm of the Senses (1976) - Censor - Refused-Classification.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=QPQAgMM79bcC&pg=PT42|title=Offensive to a Reasonable Adult|isbn=9780987242556|last1=Cetti|first1=Robert|date=September 2014}}</ref> The graphic sexual content of the production also caused it to be banned in [[Israel]] in 1987.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gDqsCQAAQBAJ&q=realm+of+senses+banned+in+israel&pg=PA1240|title=Censorship|isbn=9781136798641|last1=Jones|first1=Derek|date=December 2001}}</ref><ref name=\"articles.latimes.com\">{{cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2004/may/05/entertainment/et-johnston5|title=Director of banned film nixes accord|work=Los Angeles Times|date=5 May 2004}}</ref>\n\nThe film is available in uncut form in France, Germany, the United States (as part of [[List of Criterion Collection DVD and Blu-ray releases|The Criterion Collection]]), the Netherlands, Belgium and several other territories.{{citation needed|date=August 2013}}\n\nIn Canada, when originally submitted to the provincial film boards in the 1970s, the film was rejected in all jurisdictions except [[Quebec]] and [[British Columbia]]. It was not until 1991 that individual provinces approved the film and gave it a [[wikt:certificate|certificate]]. However, in the [[Maritimes]], the film was rejected again as the [[Maritime Film Classification Board#1960s to December 31, 1993|policies followed in the 1970s]] were still enforced.\n\nIn Brazil, the film was banned during the [[Military dictatorship in Brazil|military dictatorship]] due to its explicit sex scenes. The ban was lifted in 1980.<ref>{{cite web |title=Cinco filmes que incomodaram a censura durante a ditadura militar no Brasil |url=https://gauchazh.clicrbs.com.br/cultura-e-lazer/noticia/2013/01/cinco-filmes-que-incomodaram-a-censura-durante-a-ditadura-militar-no-brasil-4012934.html |website=GZH |access-date=24 January 2022 |language=pt-BR |date=16 January 2013}}</ref>\n\n==Controversy==\nBecause of its sexual themes and explicit scenes, the film was the cause of great controversy in Portugal in 1991 after it aired on [[R\u00e1dio e Televis\u00e3o de Portugal|RTP]].<ref>[http://whatculture.com/film/17-sex-scenes-that-supposedly-werent-simulated?page=4 What Culture#15]; In the Realm of the Senses</ref> Some deemed it inappropriate even for the [[watershed (television)|watershed]] slot, while others appreciated its airing.
