The story deals with themes of [[Substance use disorder|addiction]] and the resulting [[Dysfunctional family|dysfunction]] of the [[nuclear family]], and is drawn from O'Neill's own experiences. It was shot at [[Chelsea Studios]] in [[New York City|New York]], with exteriors filmed on [[City Island, Bronx|City Island]].<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=wDpSaPkSt-8C&pg=PA231&dq=%22221+West+26th%22#v=onepage&q=%22221%20West%2026th%22 New York: The Movie Lover's Guide: The Ultimate Insider Tour of Movie New York \u2013 Richard Alleman \u2013 Broadway (February 1, 2005)] {{ISBN|0-7679-1634-4}}</ref>\n\nThe film won [[Cannes Film Festival Award for Best Actor|Best Actor]] (for Richardson) and [[Cannes Film Festival Award for Best Actress|Best Actress]] (for Hepburn) at the [[1962 Cannes Film Festival|Cannes Film Festival]] and was named by the [[National Board of Review]] as one of the [[National Board of Review: Top Ten Films|Top Ten Films of 1962]]. Hepburn\u2019s performance earned her an [[Academy Award for Best Actress|Oscar]] and [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture, Drama|Golden Globe]] nomination for Best Actress.\n\nO\u2019Neill\u2019s play was adapted to film again in [[Long Day's Journey into Night (1996 film)|1996]], directed by [[David Wellington (director)|David Wellington]].\n\n==Plot==\n{{main article|Long Day's Journey into Night#Synopsis}}\n\n[[File:Hepburn long days journey.jpg|250px|thumb|right|[[Ralph Richardson]] (left) as James Tyrone Sr., and [[Katharine Hepburn]] (right) as Mary.]]\n\nThe film is a direct translation of O\u2019Neill\u2019s stage play, without any major cuts or changes to the source material.\n\n==Cast==\n* [[Katharine Hepburn]] as Mary Tyrone\n* [[Ralph Richardson]] as James Tyrone Sr.\n* [[Jason Robards]] as Jamie Tyrone\n* [[Dean Stockwell]] as Edmund Tyrone\n* Jeanne Burr as Cathleen\n\n==Production==\n=== Development ===\nProducer Ely Landau did a version of ''[[The Iceman Cometh]]'' for TV. This impressed the widow of Eugene O'Neill enough for her to give him the screen rights to ''Long Day's Journey''. The cast and director formed a cooperative and agreed to work for a lower fee in exchange for a percentage of the profits.<ref name=\"Making Movies\">{{cite book |last1=Lumet |first1=Sidney |title=Making Movies |url=https://archive.org/details/makingmovies00lumerich |url-access=registration |date=1995 |publisher=Vintage Books |location=New York |isbn=978-0-679-75660-6 |page=[https://archive.org/details/makingmovies00lumerich/page/24 24]}}</ref>\n\n=== Casting ===\n[[Jason Robards]] was the only actor from the stage version to also star in the film, he had won the [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actor in a Play]] for playing Jamie Tyrone in the 1957 Broadway staging, and reprised the part in O\u2019Neill\u2019s sequel ''[[A Moon for the Misbegotten]]''. He later played James Tyrone in several productions of the play.\n\n[[Marlon Brando]] was offered the role of Jamie, but turned it down.\n\n=== Filming ===\nThe film was reportedly shot for $435,000 over 37 days, two days over schedule.<ref name=\"new\"/> The entire film was shot in-sequence, after three weeks of rehearsals. Lumet later wrote that the total budget was $490,000.<ref name=\"Making Movies\"/> Exteriors were shot at a house on [[City Island, Bronx|City Island]] in [[the Bronx]], and interiors were on sets at [[Chelsea Studios|Chelsea Television Studios]] in [[Manhattan]].\n\n==Release==\n[[Joseph E. Levine]] bought the film for distribution, but said he lost money on it. \"You cannot stay in business by making O'Neill pictures\", he said.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Joseph E. Levine says making of art films for U.S. is risky|author=MURRAY SCHUMACH|date=Dec 6, 1963|work=New York Times|id={{ProQuest|116525835}}}}</ref> Lumet later wrote that \"there actually were some profits.\"<ref name=\"Making Movies\"/>\n\n==Critical reception==\nThe film has received critical acclaim. [[Rotten Tomatoes]] gives a score of 94% based on 17 reviews, with an average score of 8.2/10.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/1012647-long_days_journey_into_night |title=''Long Day's Journey into Night'' (1962) |work=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] |publisher=[[Fandango Media]] |access-date=October 30, 2020}}</ref> Critics regularly praised Lumet's direction and Hepburn's performance.\n\nDwight MacDonald from [[Esquire (magazine)|Esquire]] magazine, wrote of the film \"In his screen version of Eugene O\u2019Neill\u2019s Long Day\u2019s Journey Into Night, Sidney Lumet has given us a superb cinematic translation of the only American play to which the much-abused adjective \u201cgreat\u201d can seriously be applied.\" When speaking of Hepburn's performance he said \"I have never been an addict of Katharine Hepburn; she struck me usually as mannered, to say the least; but here, stimulated by O\u2019Neill and Lumet, she emerges as a superb trag\u00e9dienne.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://classic.esquire.com/article/1962/12/1/oneill-lumet-kurosawa-and-the-great-goldwyn |title=O\u2019Neill, Lumet, Kurosawa, and the great Goldwyn |work=[[Esquire (magazine)|Esquire]] |date=1962-12-01 |access-date=2020-10-30}}</ref> [[Bosley Crowther]] of [[The New York Times]] said that \"Under the direction of Sidney Lumet, they charge the place with electricity. That is, on the whole they do so. They develop an overall sense of deep disquiet within the passionate individuals and an acrid air of smoldering savagery.\" And when commenting on Hepburn's performance he stated \"In the moments of deepest anguish, she is vibrant with hot and tragic truth, an eloquent representatation of a lovely woman brought to feeble, helpless ruin.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1962/10/10/archives/screen-long-days-journey-opensoneill-play-is-filmed-by-sidney-lumet.html|title=Screen: 'Long Day's Journey' Opens: O'Neill Play Is Filmed by Sidney Lumet |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=1962-10-10 |access-date=2020-10-30}}</ref>\n\n===Awards and nominations===\n{| class=\"wikitable plainrowheaders\"\n|-\n!
