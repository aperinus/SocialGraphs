The film is an adaptation of Fitzgerald's 2010 play of the same name.\n''Cloudburst'' premiered at the [[Atlantic Film Festival]] in [[Halifax Regional Municipality|Halifax]], Nova Scotia on September 16, 2011.<ref name=xtra>[http://www.xtra.ca/public/National/A_queer_Thelma_and_Louise_story-10748.aspx \"A queer Thelma and Louise story\"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110926160352/http://www.xtra.ca/public/National/A_queer_Thelma_and_Louise_story-10748.aspx |date=September 26, 2011 }}. ''[[Xtra!]]'', September 13, 2011.</ref> It opened in a [[limited theatrical release|limited release]] in Canada on December 7, 2012.<ref name=CArelease>{{cite news|last1=Johns |first1=Stephanie |title=Cloudburst's home run |url=https://www.thecoast.ca/halifax/cloudbursts-home-run/Content?oid=3508908 |work=[[The Coast (newspaper)|The Coast]] |date=December 7, 2012 |access-date=16 April 2021}}</ref>\n\n==Plot==\nStella and Dotty are an older lesbian couple from Maine who embark on a [[Thelma and Louise]]-style road trip to Nova Scotia to get married after Dotty is moved into a nursing home by her granddaughter.<ref name=xtra /> Along the way they pick up Prentice, a hitchhiker travelling home to Nova Scotia to visit his dying mother, and the three bond as they travel together.<ref>[http://thechronicleherald.ca/artslife/224571-cloudburst-stars-hit-halifax \"Cloudburst stars hit Halifax\"]. ''[[The Chronicle Herald]]'', December 6, 2012.</ref>\n\n==Cast==\n* [[Olympia Dukakis]] as Stella\n* [[Brenda Fricker]] as Dot\n* [[Ryan Doucette]] as Prentice\n* [[Kristin Booth]] as Molly\n* Michael McPhee as Tommy\n* [[Mary-Colin Chisholm]] as Ynez\n* Marlane O'Brien as Cat\n\n==Production==\nFitzgerald adapted his own stage play for the screen. The film version was produced by Doug Pettigrew and Fitzgerald, and executive produced by Sidney Kimmel, Vicki McCarty, William Jarblum, Trudy Pettigrew, Dana Warren and Shandi Mitchell.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cloudburst (2011)|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1466054/|work=imdb.com|publisher=Internet Movie Database|access-date=October 5, 2011}}</ref>  Fitzgerald had originally planned for the role of Dotty to be played by [[Joan Orenstein]], but as she died while he was writing it, he cast Fricker instead.<ref name=xtra /> In press for the film, Fricker praised the screenplay, \"I was so moved by it. The love story was so beautiful I couldn't say no to it.\"<ref>{{cite web|last=Hall|first=Jamie|title=Edmonton Film Festival opens with a Cloudburst|url=https://edmontonjournal.com/mobile/entertainment/top-stories/Edmonton+film+festival+opens+with+Cloudburst/5436998/story.html|work=Edmonton Journal|publisher=Edmonton Journal|access-date=October 5, 2011}}</ref> Three members of the original stage cast reprised their roles: Ryan Doucette, Marlane O'Brien, and Michael McPhee.\n\n==Critical reception==\nThe film received an approval rating of 100% on review aggregator [[Rotten Tomatoes]], with an average rating of 6.7/10, based on 9 reviews.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/cloudburst_2012|title=Cloudburst - Rotten Tomatoes|website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] |access-date=23 November 2021}}</ref> The film debuted to an enthusiastic standing ovation on September 16, 2011 at the [[Atlantic Film Festival]], where it won an Atlantic Canada Award for Best Screenplay and the People's Choice Audience Award for Best Film of the Festival. Its second festival appearance was October 20, 2011 at [[Cin\u00e9fest Sudbury International Film Festival]], where it also won the Audience Choice Award for Best Film, and on October 23, 2011 the film was the opening night selection of the [[Edmonton International Film Festival]] where it won the Audience Award for Best Canadian Film. ''Cloudburst'' was very well received at film festivals from coast to coast in Canada, winning awards at festivals in Halifax, Montreal, Kingston, Edmonton, Victoria, and others.\n\n''Cloudburst'' made its U.S. debut at the [[Palm Springs International Film Festival]] where it was named a Best of the Fest. The film made its Australian debut at the Breath of Fresh Air Tasmania Film Festival. It made its European debut as Opening Night Gala of the [[Dublin International Film Festival]]. It was also selected as Opening Night Gala of the [[British Film Institute]] [[London Lesbian and Gay Film Festival]] and as the closing-night film of [[Frameline Film Festival|Frameline]] 36.\n\n[[Brenda Fricker]] commented in 2012, \"Of all the films I've made, only three do I remember where I felt I'd moved forward as an actress: ''Cloudburst'', ''[[My Left Foot (film)|My Left Foot]]'' and ''[[The Field (film)|The Field]]''.\"<ref>{{cite news|last=Barnard|first=Elissa|title=Actress hails Cloudburst|url=http://thechronicleherald.ca/artslife/240891-actress-hails-cloudburst|access-date=6 January 2013|newspaper=The Chronicle-Herald|date=11 December 2012}}</ref>\n\n==Accolades==\n*
