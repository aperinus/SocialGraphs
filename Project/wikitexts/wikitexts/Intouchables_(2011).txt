|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210303055955/https://www.boxofficemojo.com/title/tt1675434/ |archive-date=3 March 2021}}</ref>\n| image_size     = \n}}\n'''''The Intouchables''''' ({{lang-fr|'''Intouchables'''}}, {{IPA-fr|\u025b\u0303tu\u0283abl\u0259|pron}}), also known as '''''Untouchable''''' in the UK and Ireland, is a 2011 French [[Buddy film|buddy]] [[comedy-drama]] film written and directed by [[Olivier Nakache & \u00c9ric Toledano]]. It stars [[Fran\u00e7ois Cluzet]] and [[Omar Sy]]. Nine weeks after its release in France on 2 November 2011, it became the biggest box office hit in France, just passing the 2008 film ''[[Welcome to the Sticks]]''.<ref>{{cite news|last=Bosio|first=Alice|title=Intouchables r\u00e9ussira-t-il \u00e0 battre les Ch'tis ?|url=http://www.lefigaro.fr/cinema/2012/01/09/03002-20120109ARTFIG00573-intouchables-reussira-t-il-a-battre-les-chti-s.php|access-date=17 October 2013|newspaper=Le Figaro|date=9 January 2012|language=fr|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131020235134/http://www.lefigaro.fr/cinema/2012/01/09/03002-20120109ARTFIG00573-intouchables-reussira-t-il-a-battre-les-chti-s.php|archive-date=20 October 2013}}</ref> The film was voted the cultural event of 2011 in France with 52% of votes in a poll by [[Fnac]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Le succ\u00e8s du film \"Intouchables\", \u00e9v\u00e9nement culturel de l'ann\u00e9e |trans-title= |url=http://www.lepoint.fr/culture/le-succes-du-film-intouchables-evenement-culturel-de-l-annee-23-12-2011-1411788_3.php|access-date=17 October 2013|newspaper=Le Point|date=23 December 2011|agency=Agence France-Presse|language=fr|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131017175019/http://www.lepoint.fr/culture/le-succes-du-film-intouchables-evenement-culturel-de-l-annee-23-12-2011-1411788_3.php|archive-date=17 October 2013}}</ref> Until it was eclipsed in 2014 by [[Lucy (2014 film)|''Lucy'']], it was the most viewed French film in the world with 51.5 million tickets sold.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.unifrance.org/actualites/12736/lucy-devient-le-plus-grand-succes-francais-a-l-international |title=Lucy devient le plus grand succ\u00e8s fran\u00e7ais \u00e0 l'international |trans-title= |website=www.unifrance.org |language=fr |access-date=2019-09-09}}</ref> The film received several award nominations. In France, the film won the [[C\u00e9sar Award for Best Actor]] for Sy and garnered seven further nominations for the [[C\u00e9sar Awards]], including the [[C\u00e9sar Award for Best Actor]]. Five percent of the movie's profit were given to ''Simon de Cyr\u00e8ne'', an association that helps paralyzed people.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.simondecyrene.org/simon-de-cyrene?27947008a737957232906cea318ff25e=44a88ff431792ec2255760d89ed1a5bf|title = Simon de Cyr\u00e8ne}}</ref>\n\n==Plot==\nAt night in [[Paris]], Driss (Sy) is driving Philippe's (Cluzet) [[Maserati Quattroporte]] at high speed. Chased through the streets by the police, they are eventually cornered. Driss claims the [[quadriplegic]] Philippe must get to the emergency room urgently; Philippe pretends to have a [[Epileptic seizure|seizure]] and the fooled police officers escort them. After arriving at the hospital, Driss drives away.\n\nThe story of friendship between the two men is then told as a [[Flashback (narrative)|flashback]]: Philippe, a wealthy quadriplegic owner of a luxurious ''[[h\u00f4tel particulier]]'', and his assistant Magalie are interviewing potential live-in caregivers. Driss has no ambitions to get hired; he only attends to get a signature on a document proving his interview was rejected, in order to continue receiving his benefits. He is told to come back the next morning to collect his signed document.\n\nThe next day when Driss returns, Philippe's aide Yvonne greets him, telling him he has the job on a trial basis. Despite being uninterested and inexperienced, he does well caring for Philippe, albeit using unconventional methods. Driss learns the extent of his employer\u2019s disability, aiding Philippe in every aspect of his life. A friend of Philippe's reveals that Driss was imprisoned for six months for robbery, but Philippe does not care about Driss's past. As Driss is the only one who does not treat Philippe with pity, he will not fire Driss as long as he does his job properly.\n\nPhilippe explains his disability resulted from a [[paragliding]] incident and that his wife died without bearing children. Gradually, Driss helps him to organise his private life, including disciplining his precocious adopted daughter Elisa. Driss discovers [[modern art]], [[opera]] and starts [[painting]]. For Philippe's birthday, a private classical music concert is performed in his living room. Philippe educates Driss on famous classical pieces, but Driss only recognizes them as advert music or cartoon themes. Feeling the concert is too boring, Driss plays [[Earth, Wind & Fire]]'s \"[[Boogie Wonderland]]\", livening up the party, with the guests also enjoying the music.\n\nDiscovering Philippe has a purely [[Letter (message)|epistolary]] relationship with a woman called El\u00e9onore, who lives in [[Dunkirk]], Driss encourages his employer to meet her, but Philippe fears her reaction when she discovers his disability. Driss convinces him to talk to her by phone. Philippe agrees to send a photo of himself in a [[wheelchair]] to her, but he hesitates and asks his aide, Yvonne, to send a picture as he was before his accident. A date between them is agreed, but at the last minute, Philippe is too scared to meet El\u00e9onore and leaves with Yvonne before she arrives. Philippe then calls Driss, inviting him to fly with him in his [[Dassault Falcon 900]] private jet for a paragliding weekend in the [[Alps]].\n\nDriss's younger cousin Adam, in trouble with a gang, comes to fetch Driss at the mansion on the pretext of delivering mail. Overhearing, Philippe recognizes Driss's need to be supportive to his family and releases him from his job, suggesting he may not want to push a wheelchair all his life.\n\nDriss returns home, joins his friends and manages to help his younger cousin. In the meantime, new caregivers have replaced Driss, but Philippe is not happy with any of them. His morale is very low and he stops taking care of himself. He grows a beard and looks ill. Worried, Yvonne calls Driss back.\n\nUpon arrival, Driss drives Philippe in the [[Maserati]], which brings the story back to the initial police chase. After they elude the police, Driss takes Philippe to the seaside. Upon Philippe\u2019s shaving and dressing elegantly, they arrive at a [[Cabourg]] restaurant on the sea front. Driss suddenly leaves the table, saying good luck to Philippe on his lunch date. A few seconds later, El\u00e9onore arrives. Emotionally touched, Philippe looks through the window and sees Driss outside, smiling at him. Driss bids Philippe farewell and walks away as Phillipe and El\u00e9onore chat and enjoy each other's company.\n\nThe film ends with shots of [[Philippe Pozzo di Borgo]] and Abdel Sellou, the people on whom the story is based, together on a hillside, reminiscent of the paragliding scene earlier in the film. The closing caption states that the men remain close friends to this day.\n\n==Setting==\nThe plot of the film is inspired by the true story of [[Philippe Pozzo di Borgo]] and his [[Arabs in France|French-Algerien]] caregiver Abdel Sellou,<ref name=\"Farndale\">{{cite web |url=https://www.gala.fr/stars_et_gotha/abdel_sellou |title=Abdel Sellou |work=Gala, Biography of Abdel Sellou |access-date=2022-10-06}}</ref> discovered by the directors in ''\u00c0 la vie, \u00e0 la mort'', a documentary film.<ref>{{cite web | title= A la vie, \u00e0 la mort | url= http://www.fipa.tm.fr/en/programs/2003/a-la-vie-a-la-mort-7201.htm | work= Aspects of French Production | publisher= [[Festival International de Programmes Audiovisuels]] | access-date= 21 October 2012 | url-status= dead | archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20120708035456/http://www.fipa.tm.fr/en/programs/2003/a-la-vie-a-la-mort-7201.htm | archive-date= 8 July 2012}}</ref>\n\n==Cast==\n[[File:Nakache Omar Sy Toledano Lumi\u00e8res 2012.jpg|thumb|upright|Directors [[Olivier Nakache & \u00c9ric Toledano]] with star [[Omar Sy]] at the 2012 [[Lumi\u00e8res Award]]s ceremony.]]\n* [[Fran\u00e7ois Cluzet]] as Philippe\n* [[Omar Sy]] as Bakary \"Driss\" Bassari\n* [[Audrey Fleurot]] as Magalie\n* [[Anne Le Ny]] as Yvonne\n* [[Clotilde Mollet]] as Marcelle\n* [[Alba Ga\u00efa Bellugi]] as Elisa\n* [[Jos\u00e9phine de Meaux]] as Nathalie Lecomte\n* Cyril Mendy as Adama, Driss's cousin\n* Christian Ameri as Albert\n* Gr\u00e9goire Oestermann as Antoine\n* Marie-Laure Descoureaux as Chantal\n* Absa Dialou Toure as Mina\n* Salimata Kamate as Fatou\n* Thomas Soliveres as Bastien\n* Doroth\u00e9e Bri\u00e8re-Meritte as \u00c9l\u00e9onore\n* Caroline Bourg as Fred\n* [[\u00c9milie Caen]] as The gallerist\n\n==Reception==\nThe film received mostly positive reviews from critics and holds a 75% approval rating at the film review aggregation site [[Rotten Tomatoes]], based on 122 reviews, and an average score of 6.7/10. The consensus states, \"It handles its potentially prickly subject matter with kid gloves, but ''Intouchables'' gets by thanks to its strong cast and some remarkably sensitive direction.\"<ref>{{cite Rotten Tomatoes | id = intouchables | type = m | title = The Intouchables | url-status = live | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20120517225638/http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/intouchables/ | archive-date = 17 May 2012 | access-date = 3 March 2021}}</ref> On [[Metacritic]], the film has a score of 57 out of 100, based on 31 ratings of professional critics.<ref>{{cite Metacritic |id=the-intouchables |type=movie |title=The Intouchables|access-date=3 March 2021 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121106004511/http://www.metacritic.com/movie/the-intouchables |archive-date=6 November 2012}}</ref>\n\n===Critical response in the UK===\nThe film divided critics in the UK. Maybe it's the fantasy it spins on racial/social/cultural mores, much as ''[[Driving Miss Daisy]]'' did 20-odd years ago \u2013 uptight rich white employer learns to love through black employee's life-force. That was set in the [[Racial segregation in the United States|segregationist America]] of the 1940s.
