Roger Primrose\n* James McCarthy as Norman\n* [[Daryl Bonilla]] as POW\n}}\n\n==Production==\nIt was filmed primarily on the island of [[Kauai]], [[Hawaii]], with some excerpt shots of [[Thailand]]. The film was [[Motion Picture Association of America film rating system|rated]] R in the U.S. for war violence and brutality, and for some [[foul language|language]].  The film was produced by Jack Hafer and David Cunningham.<ref name=\"Field2004\">{{cite book|author=Alex Field|title=The Hollywood Project: A Look Into the Minds of the Makers of Spiritually Relevant Films|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=78fLh1LXkhgC&pg=PA141|year=2004|publisher=Relevant Media Group|isbn=978-0-9746942-1-4|pages=141\u2013}}</ref>\n\nThe screenplay is based on the autobiography of [[Ernest Gordon]] and recounts the experiences of [[faith]] and hope of the interned men.<ref>[http://www.urbancinefile.com.au/home/view.asp?a=6806&s=reviews \"TO END ALL WARS\"]. ''Urban Cinefile''</ref> The autobiography was originally published under the name ''[[Through the Valley of the Kwai]]'',<ref name=nyt>[https://www.nytimes.com/2002/01/20/nyregion/no-headline-223891.html?_r=0 \"No Headline\"]. ''New York Times'', By DAVID STOUTJAN. 20, 2002</ref>  then later as ''Miracle on the River Kwai'' (not to be confused with the separate novel ''[[The Bridge over the River Kwai]]'' by Pierre Boulle). Gordon's book was finally re-issued with the title ''To End All Wars'' to tie in with the film.\n\nPost-production of the film footage was delayed because of lack of funding, which was eventually provided by Gold Crest Films.<ref>[http://archives.starbulletin.com/2001/11/01/features/story2.html \"Isle filmmaker makes his triumphant return\"]. ''Star-Bulletin'', Honolulu, November 1, 2001  Tim Ryan</ref>\n\n==Reception==\nOn the review aggregation website [[Rotten Tomatoes]] the film has a score of 62% based on reviews from 13 critics, with an average rating of 5.9/10.<ref>[http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/to_end_all_wars/ To End All Wars (2001)] rottentomatoes.com</ref> The film was awarded the Crystal Heart Award and Grand Prize for Dramatic Feature at the [[Heartland Film Festival]].<ref>[https://www.newspapers.com/newspage/108033946/ \"Heartland Winner Returns\"].
