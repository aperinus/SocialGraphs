It was released to [[IMAX]] theaters in March 1998 and became the highest-grossing film made in the IMAX format.\n\n== Production ==\nThe 45-minute documentary is narrated by Irish actor [[Liam Neeson]] and was filmed entirely in IMAX. It includes a description of the training required in order to climb the 29,029 feet to the summit of Mount Everest and the challenges faced during the ascent, such as [[avalanche]]s, blizzards, and oxygen deprivation.<ref name=\"Niemi p 295\">{{cite book|last=Niemi|first=Robert James|title=Inspired by True Events: An Illustrated Guide to More Than 500 History-Based Films|edition=2nd|publisher= ABC-CLIO|location=Santa Barbara, CA|year=2013|isbn=978-1-6106-9198-7|page=588}}</ref> The film centers on a team led by [[Ed Viesturs]] and ''Everest'' director [[David Breashears]];<ref name=\"Niemi p 295\" /> among their number are Spanish climber [[Araceli Segarra]], and [[Jamling Tenzing Norgay]], son of the pioneering [[Sherpa people|Sherpa]] mountaineer [[Tenzing Norgay]].<ref name=\"VanGelder/NYTimes\">{{cite web|author=Van Gelder, Lawrence|url=https://www.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9E0DE3D61E31F935A35750C0A96E958260 |title= Movie Review \u2013 Everest IMAX (1998) |work=[[The New York Times]] |date= March 6, 1998 |access-date=November 20, 2014}}</ref>\n\n''Everest'' was in production at the mountain during the [[1996 Mount Everest disaster]], in which another group of climbers became trapped by a blizzard near the summit. The film includes footage of these events,<ref name=\"Baumgarten\" /> as the IMAX team assist [[Beck Weathers]] and other survivors.<ref name=\"Niemi p 295\" /> Producer and co-director [[Greg MacGillivray]] later said that while editing the documentary for release, he and Breashears decided to focus more on the tragedy, due to the popularity of [[Jon Krakauer]]'s book about the 1996 disaster, ''[[Into Thin Air]]'' (1997). MacGillivray said \"Ten million people have read that book, so we had to address the issue. And I think it strengthened the film.\"<ref name=\"Matzer/LATimes1\">{{cite web|author=Matzer, Marla|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1998/mar/28/entertainment/ca-33430/2 |title= 'Everest' Lifts Imax to Dramatic New Peaks  |work=[[Los Angeles Times]] |date=March 28, 1998 |access-date=November 20, 2014}}</ref>\n\n==Reception==\n''Everest'' premiered at Boston's [[Museum of Science (Boston)|Museum of Science]] on March 4, 1998 before going on general release in IMAX cinemas across the United States two days later.<ref>{{cite book|last=Badman|first=Keith|title=The Beatles Diary Volume 2: After Switzerland on March 20, with other European premieres, including at the [[London Trocadero]], following during April and May.<ref name=\"Badman pp 588-89\">{{cite
