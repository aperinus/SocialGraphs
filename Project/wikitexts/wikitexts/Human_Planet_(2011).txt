         = \n| followed_by          = \n| related              =  \n}}\n\n'''''Human Planet''''' is an 8-part British television documentary series. It is produced by the [[BBC]] with co-production from [[Discovery Channel|Discovery]] and [[BBC Worldwide]]. It describes the human species and its relationship with the natural world by showing the remarkable ways humans have adapted to life in every environment on Earth.<ref name=\"Blogwelcome\">{{cite web |url=http://humanplanet.blogs.bbcearth.com/2009/04/07/welcome-to-the-bbcs-human-planet/ |title=Welcome to Human Planet |date=4 July 2009 |work=Human Planet blog |publisher=[[BBC Worldwide]] |access-date=22 January 2010}}</ref> The show drew attention for alleged fakery and the BBC eventually acknowledged that a number of scenes were inaccurately depicted or misleading and withdrew the series from distribution.<ref name=\":0\">{{Cite web|date=2018-04-04|title=BBC admits treehouse scene from Human Planet series was faked|url=http://www.theguardian.com/media/2018/apr/04/scene-from-human-planet-documentary-was-faked-bbc-admits|access-date=2021-04-27|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref><ref name=\"bbc.co.uk\" />\n\n''Human Planet'' was originally screened in the UK on [[BBC One]] each Thursday at 8pm over eight weeks, starting from 13 January 2011. Domestic repeats have been seen on [[Eden (British TV channel)|Eden]], with all 8 episodes aired over one week in April 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://uktv.co.uk/eden/item/aid/651327 |title=Human Planet on Eden |access-date=3 April 2012}}</ref> BBC Worldwide has since announced they have sold the broadcast rights to 22 international markets.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://realscreen.com/2011/02/25/bbc-worldwide-takes-human-planet-around-the-world/ |title=BBC Worldwide takes ''Human Planet'' around the world |first=Barry |last=Walsh |work=RealScreen.com |date=25 February 2011 |access-date=2 April 2011}}</ref>\n\n==Production==\nAnnounced in 2007,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/bbcworldwide/worldwidestories/pressreleases/2007/04_april/geldof_dictionary.shtml |title=BBC and Bob Geldof to map mankind |date=17 April 2007 |publisher=[[BBC]] Press Office |access-date=22 January 2010}}</ref> the production teams based at the [[BBC Natural History Unit]] in Bristol and [[BBC Wales]] spent three years shooting over 70 stories in some of the most remote locations on Earth spanning about 40 countries. Each episode of the series focuses on a different human-inhabited environment, including deserts, jungles, the [[Arctic]], grasslands, rivers, mountains, oceans, and the urban landscape.\n\nFor the first time on a BBC landmark series, the production had a dedicated stills photographer, [[Timothy Allen]], who documented the project photographically for the books and multimedia that accompany the series.<ref name=\"Blogwelcome\"/>\n\n==Broadcast==\n{|class=wikitable\n!width=190| Country/Region !!width=130| Channel !!width=190| Narrator \n|-\n| {{AUS}} || [[ABC1]] || \n|-\n| {{NZL}} || [[Prime (New Zealand)|Prime]] || \n|-\n| {{GBR}} || [[BBC One]]<br>[[Eden (British TV channel)|Eden]] || [[John Hurt]]\n|-\n| {{USA}} || [[Discovery Channel]]<br>[[Discovery en Espa\u00f1ol]] || Mike Rowe '''(English)'''<br>Gael Garcia Bernal '''(Spanish)''' \n|}\n\n==Episodes==\n{{quotation|Only one creature has carved a life for itself in every habitat on Earth. A first statement admitted that \"the portrayal of the [<nowiki/>[[Korowai people|Korowai]]] tribe moving into the treehouse as a real home is not accurate.\" In fact, the tribe built the treehouse on commission for the programme.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/statements/human-planet|title=BBC - Statement regarding Human Planet - Media Centre|website=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref> A second statement stated that a scene depicting \"a Lamaleran whale hunter named Benjamin Blikololong shown supposedly harpooning a whale\" is also inaccurate.<ref name=\"bbc.co.uk\">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/statements/human-planet-oceans|title=BBC - Further Statement concerning Human Planet natural history series - Media Centre|website=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>\n\nThe BBC also later disclosed that a scene from the Deserts episode, which supposedly showed a wild wolf being shot at by Mongolian herders, actually showed a semi-domesticated wolf running to its handler, who was kept out of frame. The wolf was not shot at.<ref name=\":0\" /> Another scene depicting Venezuelan children hunting tarantulas, while implied to be taking place in the jungle, actually was shot in a television studio.<ref name=\":0\" />\n\nAs a result, the BBC withdrew ''Human Planet'' from distribution pending a full editorial review.<ref name=\"bbc.co.uk\" />\n\n==Merchandise==\nThe Region 2 DVD and
