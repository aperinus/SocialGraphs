The film premiered at the 2009 [[Rome Film Festival]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Claudia Mormoglione|title=Valeria e Isabella, amore proibito E cos\u00ec una \"lei\" diventa \"lui\"...|url=http://www.repubblica.it/2009/10/sezioni/spettacoli_e_cultura/cinema/roma/violadimare/violadimare/violadimare.html?ref=search|accessdate=21 February 2014|newspaper=[[La Repubblica]]|date=16 October 2009}}</ref> It was nominated for two [[Nastro d'Argento]] Awards, for Best Actress ([[Valeria Solarino]]) and Best Original Song (\"Sogno\" by [[Gianna Nannini]]).<ref>{{cite news|title=Nastri d'Argento - tutte le cinquine|url=http://trovacinema.repubblica.it/news/dettaglio/nastri-dargento-tutte-le-cinquine/391356|accessdate=21 February 2014|newspaper=[[La Repubblica]]|date=27 April 2010}}</ref>\n\n==Plot summary==\nThe film tells the love story between Angela and Sara in 19th-century [[Sicily]] (circa 1860, during the [[Expedition of the Thousand]]). To survive the scandal, Angela's family winds up passing her off as a boy, going so far as to alter her birth certificate. Angela cuts her hair short and hides her own femininity, in defiance of the mentality of the place.\n\n==Production==\nProduced by the Italian Dreams Factory, with the support of the [[Italian Ministry of Cultural Heritage and Activities]] and the [[Regions of Italy|Region of]] [[Sicily]]. The movie, filmed in [[San Vito Lo Capo]], [[Trapani]], and [[Favignana]], was shown at the 2009 [[Rome Film Festival]] and distributed to theaters on October 16, 2009.\n\n==Meaning of the title==\n[[File:Coris julis 24-05-07 male.jpg|250 px|thumb|''Coris julis'', Tyrrhenian Sea]]\nThe film's title refers to the Sicilian name of the [[Mediterranean rainbow wrasse]] (''Coris julis''), a [[Sequential hermaphroditism#Protogyny|protogynous]] fish that is born female and turns male as it grows older.\n\n<blockquote>The ''Viola'' (violet) is a fish and God has willed it. When it is male it is called ''Minchia di Re'' (king's penis). For love it becomes female and has the colors of the flower. It turns male again after the water has taken its eggs.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Pilati|first1=Giacomo|title=Minchia di re|date=2004|publisher=Ugo Mursia Editore|location=Milan|isbn=9788842544203|url=http://www.mursia.com/index.php/it/romanzi/minchia-di-re-detail|accessdate=January 8, 2018}}</ref></blockquote>\n\n==Reception==\nThe film won two awards at the 2009 New Italian Cinematic Events festival: the Citt\u00e0 di Firenze Award for best film and the [[Susan Batson]] Award for best actress, Valeria Solarino in the role of Angela.
