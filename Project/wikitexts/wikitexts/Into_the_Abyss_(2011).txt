by [[Werner Herzog]] about two men convicted of a triple homicide that occurred in [[Conroe, Texas]]. [[Michael Perry and Jason Burkett|Michael Perry]] received a [[death sentence]] for the crime. The documentary also features an interview of Perry's accomplice, [[Michael Perry and Jason Burkett|Jason Burkett]], who was spared execution.\n\nThe film was first shown on September 3, 2011, at the [[Telluride Film Festival]],<ref name=\"hrtelluride\">{{cite news| url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/risky-business/telluride-2011-abyss-pina-a-231025 | work=The Hollywood Reporter | first=Jay A. | last=Fernandez | title=Telluride 2011: 'Into the Abyss' vs. 'Pina' in a Documentary Doubleheader | date=September 3, 2011}}</ref> and had its official world premiere on September 8, 2011, at the [[2011 Toronto International Film Festival]].<ref name=\"clips\">{{cite web |url= https://www.indiewire.com/2011/08/watch-3-powerful-clips-new-photos-from-werner-herzogs-death-row-documentary-into-the-abyss-117057/ |title=Watch: 3 Powerful Clips & New Photos From Werner Herzog's Death Row Documentary ''Into The Abyss'' |publisher=Indiewire |date=August 5, 2011 |access-date=November 28, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110812004933/http://blogs.indiewire.com/theplaylist/archives/watch_3_powerful_clips_new_photos_from_werner_herzogs_death_row_documentary/# |archive-date=2011-08-12 }}</ref> After strong festival showings and a surge of interest in the issue of [[capital punishment]] in the [[United States]], Herzog requested that the film be rushed into general theatrical release, which occurred on November 11, 2011.<ref name=\"deadline\"/>\n\n==Synopsis==\nThe film profiles [[Michael Perry and Jason Burkett|Michael James Perry]] (April 9, 1982 \u2013 July 1, 2010), a man on [[death row]] convicted of murdering Sandra Stotler, a 50-year-old nurse. He also confessed to two other murders which occurred in [[Conroe, Texas]]. Perry was convicted of the October 2001 murder 8 years before filming; the crimes apparently were committed to steal a car for a joyride. Perry denies that he was responsible for the killings.\n\nPerry's final interviews for the film were recorded only eight days before his execution<ref name=\"tiffnote\">{{cite web|title=Into the Abyss: Werner Herzog|url=http://tiff.net/filmsandschedules/tiff/2011/intotheabyss|publisher=Toronto International Film Festival|access-date=18 January 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111015160402/http://tiff.net/filmsandschedules/tiff/2011/intotheabyss|archive-date=15 October 2011}}</ref> on July 1, 2010.<ref name=\"officialsite\"/> The film also includes interviews with victims' families and law-enforcement officers.<ref>{{cite news|first=Kate |last=Taylor |url= https://www.theglobeandmail.com/amp/arts/awards-and-festivals/tiff/a-closer-look-at-3-tiff-2011-docs-to-get-excited-about/article4258636/ |title=A closer look at 3 TIFF docs to get excited about |publisher=The Globe and Mail |date=August 3, 2011 |access-date=November 28, 2021|location=Toronto}}</ref>\n\nThe film does not focus on Perry's guilt or innocence and features a minimal amount of narration, with Herzog never appearing onscreen, unlike in many of his films.<ref name=gazing>{{cite web |archive-date=2011-08-23}}</ref><ref name=\"varietyreview\">{{cite news| url= https://variety.com/2011/film/markets-festivals/into-the-abyss-a-tale-of-death-a-tale-of-life-1117945958/amp/ | work=Variety | first=Peter | last=Debruge | title=Into the Abyss: A Tale of Death, a Tale of Life | date=September 3, 2011|accessdate=November 28, 2021}}</ref>\n\n==Production==\nHerzog had long considered making a film about prison inmates. In fact, Herzog, at age 17, had intended to make his first film about the maximum-security [[Straubing]] prison in [[Bavaria]]. This concept was never realized, but the idea remained dormant until ''Into the Abyss'' decades later.<ref name=\"hrtelluride\"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.theparisreview.org/blog/2011/05/02/the-spring-issue-werner-herzog-and-jan-simek-on-caves/ |title=The Spring Issue: Werner Herzog and Jan Simek on Caves, John Jeremiah Sullivan |publisher= The Paris Review |date=May 2, 2011 |access-date=August 15, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110814044501/http://www.theparisreview.org/blog/2011/05/02/the-spring-issue-werner-herzog-and-jan-simek-on-caves/# |archive-date=2011-08-14 |url-status=dead }}</ref>\n\nThe film was financed by American [[cable TV]] channel [[Investigation Discovery]], which gave Herzog financing and creative freedom.<ref name=\"finalconfessions\">{{cite web|author=Adam Benzine |url=http://realscreen.com/2011/04/15/id-holds-final-confessions-with-herzog/ |title=ID holds \"Final Confessions\" with Werner Herzog |publisher=Realscreen |date=April 15, 2011 |access-date=August 15, 2011}}</ref>\n\nThe film went through several working titles during its production. For a time, it was called simply ''Death Row'', and this was later elaborated to ''Gazing Into The Abyss: A Tale of Death, a Tale Of Life''.<ref name=gazing/> The television broadcast of the film and the related series was briefly referred to as ''Werner Herzog\u2019s Final Confessions'',<ref name=\"finalconfessions\"/> but eventually aired with the title ''[[On Death Row]]''.\n\nCertain voices in the production wanted the film's title to be ''The Red Camaro'', but Herzog disliked the hint of [[product placement]] in the title, and called it \"not evocative at all\".<ref name=\"fm\">{{cite web|url=http://www.flickeringmyth.com/2012/03/into-abyss-conversation-with-filmmaker.html |title=Into the Abyss: A conversation with filmmaker Werner Herzog |publisher=Flickering Myth |access-date=2012-08-02}}</ref> By August 2011, when its premiere at TIFF was announced, the title was changed to ''Into the Abyss''. Herzog has often commented that ''Into the Abyss'' could have worked well as the title of many of his films.<ref name=\"fm\"/>\n\nHerzog originally planned and shot profiles of five<ref name=\"clips\"/> [[death-row]] inmates in [[Texas]] and [[Florida]],<ref name=\"screend\">{{cite web|last=Macnab |first=Geoffrey |url=http://www.screendaily.com/festivals/berlin/werner-herzog-exits-cave-goes-to-death-row/5023792.article |title=Werner Herzog exits Cave, goes to Death Row |publisher=Screendaily.com |date=February 15, 2011 |access-date=August 14, 2011}}</ref> including Michael Perry. After production began, Herzog decided to focus on Perry's case.<ref name=tiffnote/> His other interviews were compiled into a series of four 50-minute films which aired as the [[television miniseries]] ''[[On Death Row]]''.\n\nThe film was in production during promotion for Herzog's previous film ''[[Cave of Forgotten Dreams]]''. Herzog missed a special screening of ''Cave'' at the [[Berlinale]] festival because an opportunity to film one of his subjects arose suddenly.<ref name=\"screend\"/>\n\n==Release and distribution==\nThe film's premiere was in September 2011 at the [[2011 Toronto International Film Festival]].<ref name=\"clips\"/> The film was often portrayed as being one among several \"hot-button political documentaries\" at the festival.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.indiewire.com/2011/08/you-betcha-what-hot-political-docs-will-cause-a-stir-in-toronto-134583/amp/ |title=You Betcha! What Hot Political Docs Will Cause a Stir in Toronto? |publisher=IndieWire |date=August 3, 2011 |access-date=November 28, 2021 }}</ref> Herzog, however, has stated that he has no political intentions with the film. Herzog states at the film's outset that he opposes [[capital punishment]],<ref name=\"hrtelluride\"/> but he has said that his \"focus is elsewhere\" in the film.<ref name=\"screend\"/> In an interview with the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'', he said, \"This is not an issue film; it's not an activist film against capital punishment,\" and, \"Yes, it has an issue, but it's not the main purpose of the film.\"<ref>{{cite news| url=https://latimesblogs.latimes.com/movies/2011/09/toronto-2011-werner-herzog-looks-into-the-abyss-of-death-and-crime.html | work=Los Angeles Times | title= Toronto: Werner Herzog looks 'Into the Abyss' of death and crime | date=September 10, 2011}}</ref>\n\nBefore the film's festival premiere, North American theatrical distribution rights were bought by [[Sundance Selects]].<ref name=\"nabbed\">{{cite web |last1=Brooks |first1=Brian |title=Werner Herzog\u2019s \"Abyss\" Nabbed by Sundance Selects Ahead of Toronto Premiere |url=https://www.indiewire.com/2011/09/werner-herzogs-abyss-nabbed-by-sundance-selects-ahead-of-toronto-premiere-52544/amp/ |website=IndieWire |access-date=November 28, 2021 |date=September 1, 2011}}</ref> In October 2011, Sundance Selects announced that the film would be rushed to a theatrical release, opening in select cities on November 11, 2011. In recent months, controversies have arisen over American capital punishment in the case of [[Troy Davis]] and the cheers for [[Rick Perry]]'s execution record in a [[Republican Party (United States) presidential debates, 2012|Republican primary debate]].\nThe early release was demanded by Herzog himself, who wanted his film to contribute to this new national discussion of the death penalty. Producer [[Erik Nelson (filmmaker)|Erik Nelson]] said, \"Everyone in the country is focused on the death-penalty debate again thanks to Rick Perry and the bloodthirsty yahoos at the Republican debate, and I think Werner wants the film to be part of that discussion because timing is everything.\"<ref name=deadline>{{cite web|last=Hammond |first=Pete |url=https://www.deadline.com/2011/10/oscar-herzogs-controversial-torontotelluride-death-penalty-doc-being-rushed-into-release-in-november/ |title=OSCARS: Werner Herzog's Controversial Toronto/Telluride Death Penalty Doc Being Rushed Into Release In November |publisher=Deadline.com |date=2011-10-07 |access-date=2012-08-02}}</ref>\n\nAfter a theatrical release, the film was shown on [[Investigation Discovery]], which provided the film's financing.<ref name=\"finalconfessions\"/> It was available for on-demand video streaming on [[Netflix]] but no longer.<ref>[http://movies.netflix.com/Movie/Into-the-Abyss/70219767 ''Into the Abyss''] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120717070450/http://movies.netflix.com/Movie/Into-the-Abyss/70219767 |date=2012-07-17 }} Netflix.com</ref>\n\n==Reception==\nThe film has received positive reviews.
