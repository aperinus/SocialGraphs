On [[Rotten Tomatoes]] it has an approval rating of 52%, based on 21 reviews, with an average rating of 5.51/10.<ref>{{cite web |title= Ashby |url= http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/ashby/ |website= [[Rotten Tomatoes]] |accessdate= January 10, 2015 }}</ref> On [[Metacritic]], the film holds a rating of 46 out of 100, based on 8 critics, indicating \"mixed or average reviews\".<ref>{{cite web |title= Ashby Reviews |url= http://www.metacritic.com/movie/ashby |website= [[Metacritic]] |accessdate= January 10, 2016 }}</ref>\n\nDennis Harvey of ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' gave the film a mixed review, writing: \"Starring Mickey Rourke as a retired CIA assassin turned improbable mentor to Nat Wolff's next-door-neighbor misfit, 'Ashby' is a genre jumble that makes half-baked use of high-school sports, crime comedy, teen romance and other formulae to mildly diverting ends that are never quite convincing or funny enough. Paramount launched a limited theatrical rollout on Sept. 25, simultaneous with on-demand availability, but Aussie tube scribe Tony McNamara's U.S. feature will definitely fare best as a viable if innocuous cable/rental time-filler.\"<ref>{{cite web |url= https://variety.com/2015/film/reviews/ashby-review-1201603321/ |title= Film Review: 'Ashby' |work= Variety.com |first= Dennis |last= Harvey |date= September 26, 2015 |accessdate= January 10, 2016 }}</ref>\n\nKate Erbland of The Playlist for [[Indiewire.com]] also gave the film a mixed review, writing : \"It's inevitable that 'Ashby' will squish together its two most important plots \u2014 Ashby's mysterious quest and Ed's attempts to be a football star \u2014 but McNamara attempts to keep the movie ticking right along, and for all its half-cocked plotlines, 'Ashby' is able to maintain a consistently humorous and light tone.
