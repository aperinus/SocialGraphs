Slovenian [[philosophy|philosopher]] and [[psychanalysis|psychoanalytic theorist]] [[Slavoj \u017di\u017eek]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The Pervert's Guide to Ideology: Toronto Review|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/perverts-guide-ideology-toronto-review-368821|work=The Hollywood Reporter|accessdate=29 September 2012}}</ref> It is a sequel to Fiennes's 2006 documentary ''[[The Pervert's Guide to Cinema]]''. Though the film follows the frameworks of its predecessor, this time the emphasis is on [[ideology]] itself. Through [[psychoanalysis]] \u017di\u017eek explores \"the mechanisms that shape what we believe and how we behave\".<ref>{{cite web|title=Pervert's Guide to Cinema's Fiennes and \u017di\u017eek Reteam for ''Pervert's Guide to Ideology''|url=http://blogs.indiewire.com/thompsononhollywood/perverts_guide_to_cinemas_fiennes_and_zizek_reteam_for_perverts_guide_to_id|publisher=indiewire.com|accessdate=29 September 2012}}</ref> Among the films that are explored are ''[[Full Metal Jacket]]'' and ''[[Taxi Driver]]''.<ref>{{cite news|last=Leigh|first=Danny|title=Slavoj \u017di\u017eek: Blofeld rides again|url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2011/oct/16/slavoj-zizek-perverts-guide-ideology|accessdate=29 September 2012|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|date=16 October 2011}}</ref> The film was released in the [[United States]] by [[Zeitgeist Films]] in November 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Perverts Uprising! Zeitgeist Films Will Distribute Two New Documentaries|url=http://www.indiewire.com/article/perverts-uprising-zeitgeist-films-will-distribute-two-new-documentaries|work=IndieWire|accessdate=18 July 2013}}</ref>\n\n==Synopsis==\n\n\u017di\u017eek appears transplanted into the scenes of various movies, exploring and exposing how they reinforce prevailing ideologies. As the ideologies undergirding cinematic fantasies are revealed, striking associations emerge: from nuns advising following your desires at ''[[The Sound of Music (film)|The Sound of Music]]'' to the political dimensions of ''[[Jaws (film)|Jaws]]''. ''[[Taxi Driver]]'', ''[[Zabriskie Point (film)|Zabriskie Point]]'', ''[[The Searchers (film)|The Searchers]]'', ''[[The Dark Knight (film)|The Dark Knight]]'', [[John Carpenter]]'s ''[[They Live]]'' (\"one of the forgotten masterpieces of the Hollywood Left\"), ''[[Titanic (1997 film)|Titanic]]'', [[Kinder Surprise]] eggs, verit\u00e9 news footage, the emptiness of [[Ludwig van Beethoven|Beethoven]]'s [[Symphony No. 9 (Beethoven)|\"Ode to Joy\"]], and [[propaganda]] epics from [[Nazi Germany]] and [[Soviet Russia]] all inform \u017di\u017eek's psychoanalytic-cinematic argument.\n\n==List of films discussed in this documentary==\n* ''[[Triumph of the Will]]'' (1935)\n* ''[[The Eternal Jew (1940 film)|The Eternal Jew]]'' (1940)\n* ''[[Brief Encounter]]'' (1945)\n* ''[[The Fall of Berlin (film)|The Fall of Berlin]]'' (1950)\n* ''[[The Searchers (film)|The Searchers]]'' (1956)\n* ''[[West Side Story (1961 film)|West Side Story]]'' (1961)\n* ''[[The Sound of Music (film)|The Sound of Music]]'' (1965)\n* ''[[Loves of a Blonde]]'' (1965)\n* ''[[Seconds (1966 film)|Seconds]]'' (1966)\n* ''[[The Firemen's Ball]]'' (1967)\n* ''Oratorio for Prague'' (1968)\n* ''[[If....]]'' (1969)\n* ''[[MASH (film)|MASH]]'' (1970)\n* ''[[Zabriskie Point (film)|Zabriskie Point]]'' (1970)\n* ''[[A Clockwork Orange (film)|A Clockwork Orange]]'' (1971)\n* ''[[Cabaret (1972 film)|Cabaret]]'' (1972)\n* ''[[Jaws (film)|Jaws]]'' (1975)\n* ''[[Taxi Driver]]'' (1976)\n* ''[[Brazil (1985 film)|Brazil]]'' (1985)\n* ''[[Full Metal Jacket]]'' (1987)\n* ''[[The Last Temptation of Christ (film)|The Last Temptation of Christ]]'' (1988)\n* ''[[They Live]]'' (1988)\n* ''[[Titanic (1997 film)|Titanic]]'' (1997)\n* ''[[I Am Legend (film)|I Am Legend]]'' (2007)\n* ''[[The Dark Knight (film)|The Dark Knight]]'' (2008)\n* ''[[Coca-Cola marketing|Coca-Cola]] (commercial)''\n\nAlso included is some news footage of the [[September 11 attacks]], [[2011 Norway attacks]], and [[2011 England riots]].
