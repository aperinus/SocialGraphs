It is an [[co-production (media)|international co-production]] between [[Cinema of Israel|Israel]], [[Cinema of France|France]] and [[Cinema of the United States|the United States]].\n\nThe film received acclaim from critics and audiences. It won eight [[Ophir Award|Ophir Prize]]s awarded by the [[Israeli Film Academy]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ynetnews.com/articles/0,7340,L-3464872,00.html|title='The Band's Visit' wins Tokyo festival|first=Merav|last=Yudilovitch|date=28 October 2007|access-date=3 January 2022|newspaper=Ynetnews}}</ref> It was Israel's original Foreign Language Film submission for the [[80th Academy Awards]], but was rejected by the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] because it contained over 50% [[English language|English]] dialogue.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.haaretz.com/hasen/spages/930323.html|title=Israeli film wins country's first two European Academy Awards |website=Haaretz.com|access-date=3 January 2022}}</ref> Thus, Israel sent ''[[Beaufort (film)|Beaufort]]'' instead; ''Beaufort'' was eventually included in the five final nominees.<ref name=\"ynetnews.com\">{{cite news|url=https://www.ynetnews.com/articles/0,7340,L-3452011,00.html|title=The Band Visit's victory|date=21 September 2007|newspaper=Ynetnews|last1=Birenberg|first1=Yoav}}</ref>\n\n==Plot==\nThe eight men of the [[Alexandria]] Ceremonial Police Orchestra arrive in Israel from [[Egypt]]. They have been booked by an Arab cultural center in [[Petah Tikva]], but through a miscommunication (Arabic has no \"p\" sound, and regularly replaces it with \"b\"), the band takes a bus to ''Beit Hatikva'', a fictional town in the middle of the [[Negev|Negev Desert]].<ref>[http://www.baltimoresun.com/topic/ny-etver145567265feb08,0,6115326.story] {{Dead link|date=November 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>\n\nThe Egyptians encounter a few Israeli townspeople, who respond with curiosity about the band, are variously friendly and wary, and provide them with shelter, food, music and companionship during their visit. There is no transportation out of the town that day, and there are no hotels for them to spend the night in.\n\nThe band members dine at a small restaurant where the owner, Dina ([[Ronit Elkabetz]]) invites them to stay the night at her apartment, at her friends' apartment, and in the restaurant. No one quite falls in love, but a sense of unspoken longing and loneliness is expressed.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://movies.nytimes.com/2007/12/07/movies/07visi.html |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |title=Strangers in a land that's not so strange |first=Manohla |last=Dargis |date=7 December 2007 |access-date=25 May 2010}}</ref>\n\n==Cast==\n* [[Saleh Bakri]] as Khaled\n* [[Ronit Elkabetz]] as Dina\n* [[Sasson Gabai]] as Lieutenant-colonel Tawfiq Zacharya\n* [[Uri Gavriel]] as Avrum\n* [[Khalifa Natour]] as Simon\n* Rubi Moskovitz as Itzik\n* Imad Jabarin as Major-general Camal Abdel Azim\n* Hilla Sarjon as Iris\n* Shlomi Avraham as Papi\n* [[Tarak Kopty]] as Iman\n* [[Rinat Matatov]] as Yula\n* [[Tomer Yosef]] as Ars\n* Ahuva Keren as Lea\n* Fran\u00e7ois Khell as Makram\n* Hisham Khoury as Fauzi\n\n==Release==\n===Critical reception===\n''The Band's Visit'' received critical acclaim. [[Rotten Tomatoes]] reported that 98% of critics gave the film positive reviews, based on 116&nbsp;reviews with an average of 7.9/10. The site's consensus states: \"''The Band's Visit'' is both a clever, subtle, slice-of-life comedy, and poignant cross-cultural exploration.\" It also received a [[Rotten Tomatoes#Golden Tomato Awards|Golden Tomato]] for Best Foreign Film of 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/bands_visit/ |title=''The Band's Visit'' |access-date=8 February 2008 |website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]}}</ref> On [[Metacritic]], it has an 80 out of 100 rating, based on 29&nbsp;critics, indicating \"generally favorable reviews\".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.metacritic.com/movie/the-bands-visit |title=''The Band's Visit'' |publisher=Metacritic|access-date=17 July 2018}}</ref>\n\nRay Bennett of ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'' named the film the second best of 2007,<ref name=mc07top10>{{cite web |url=http://www.metacritic.com/film/awards/2007/toptens.shtml |title=2007 film critic Top Ten lists |access-date=5 January 2008 |publisher=[[Metacritic]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080102102034/http://www.metacritic.com/film/awards/2007/toptens.shtml <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archive-date = 2 January 2008}}</ref> V.A. Musetto of the ''[[New York Post]]'' named it the 8th best film of 2007,<ref name=mc07top10/> and both [[Ella Taylor]] of ''[[LA Weekly]]''<ref name=mc07top10/> and [[Associated Press]] film critic David Germain named it the 9th best film of 2007.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.columbiatribune.com/2007/Dec/20071227Go!013.asp |title=''No Country for Old Men'' earns nod from AP critics |access-date=31 December 2007 |author1=David Germain |author2=Christy Lemire |date=27 December 2007 |agency=Associated Press |newspaper=[[Columbia Daily Tribune]] |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120709070653/http://archive.columbiatribune.com/2007/Dec/20071227Go!013.asp |archive-date=9 July 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref>\n\n[[Roger Ebert]] of the ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]'' selected it as one of the 20 best films of 2008.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ebert's Top Movies of 2008 |url=https://www.rogerebert.com/rogers-journal/the-best-films-of-2008-and-there-were-a-lot-of-them |work=Chicago Sun-Times |author=Ebert, Roger |access-date=9 November 2013}}</ref> According to Ebert, \"''The Band\u2019s Visit'' has not provided any of the narrative payoffs we might have expected, but has provided something more valuable: An [[Entr'acte|interlude]] involving two 'enemies,' [[Arab\u2013Israeli conflict|Arabs and Israelis]], that shows them both as only ordinary people with ordinary hopes, lives, and disappointments. It has also shown us two souls with rare beauty.\"<ref>{{cite web |title=The Band's Visit |url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/the-bands-visit-2008 |author=Ebert, Roger |access-date=9 November 2013}}</ref>\n\nGabai's characterization and playing of Tawfiq Zacharya was hailed by critics, who described him as \"an actor of real skill, and an artist capable of the deepest understanding of the human condition.\"<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0299924/board/nest/128592452 |website=IMDb |department=Message Boards |title=Dracher}}</ref> During the [[Israeli Film Academy Awards]] in September 2007, Bakri, \"the band's handsome young trumpet player\"<ref name=\"jewishjournal.com\">{{cite news |url=http://www.jewishjournal.com/arts/article/film_israels_bands_visit_finally_plays_la_20080208/ |newspaper=Jewish Journal |department=Film |title=Israel's ''Band's Visit'' finally plays L.A. |first=Tom |last=Tugend}}</ref> thanked his parents and said, \"I want to thank my father, who taught me to love mankind, and my mother, who taught me to bear the burden of life in this country and stand strong,\" he said.\n\nFor her role, critics described Elkabetz as \"wonderful ... a kind-hearted lonely heart refusing to wilt in her desert town\".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0253813/bio|title=Ronit Elkabetz|website=IMDb}}</ref> During the Israeli Film Academy Awards Ceremony in September 2007, Elkabetz brought a message of reconciliation and hope, and after taking a bow before the film's director, she said to him, \"You reminded us of a thing or two that we have already managed to forget. Festival\n\n====Language disqualification====\n[[File:AMPAS.jpg|thumb|[[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] headquarters building]]\nUnder the rules of the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]], more than half the dialogue in a foreign film entry must be in the originating country's own language. However, ''The Band's Visit'', whose Egyptian and Israeli characters communicate mainly in broken English, didn't meet the requirement and was disqualified by the Oscar committee. Even so, [[Sony Pictures]], the film's distributor, entered it in the general Oscar categories of best picture, director, screenplay, actor and actress \u2013 none of which came through for the film.\n\n\"Nobody in Israel thought about the language problem,\" said Kolirin, who spent four years making the film. When he heard about the adverse American decision, \"I was pissed off for a few days, but I've gotten over it,\" he said during a visit to [[Los Angeles]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.jewishjournal.com/arts/article/film_israels_bands_visit_finally_plays_la_20080208/ |title=Film: Israel's 'Band's Visit' finally plays L.A. &#124; Arts &#124; Jewish Journal |access-date=20 July 2009 |archive-date=23 May 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100523191151/http://www.jewishjournal.com/arts/article/film_israels_bands_visit_finally_plays_la_20080208/ |url-status=dead }}</ref>\n\n==Stage adaptation==\n{{Main|The Band's Visit (musical)}}\nThe film has been adapted into a stage musical of the same name. The musical's book is written by [[Itamar Moses]] with music and lyrics by [[David Yazbek]]. Directed by [[David Cromer]], with choreography by Patrick McCollum, the cast stars [[Tony Shalhoub]], [[John Cariani]] and [[Katrina Lenk]].
