Leigh plays Sadie Flood, a [[Punk rock|punk]]y barroom singer who has a complicated relationship with her older sister, Georgia, played by Winningham.<ref name=\"metroactive\" /><ref name=\"auto1\">{{Cite web |last=Willistein |first=Paul |date=1996-01-21 |title=On her Mind: Jennifer Jason Leigh, Star of 'Georgia,' Gives Writer Mom Idea for Film |url=https://www.mcall.com/news/mc-xpm-1996-01-21-3081755-story.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.ph/2022.09.11-032641/https://www.mcall.com/news/mc-xpm-1996-01-21-3081755-story.html |archive-date=September 11, 2022 |work=[[The Morning Call]]}}</ref><ref name=\"auto3\">{{Cite web |last=Wilmington |first=Michael |date=1996-01-10 |title=Jennifer Jason Leigh's Heartfelt Sadie Will Keep 'Georgia' on Your Mind |url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/news/ct-xpm-1996-01-10-9601100254-story.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.ph/2022.09.11-033318/https://www.chicagotribune.com/news/ct-xpm-1996-01-10-9601100254-story.html |archive-date=2022-09-11 |work=[[Chicago Tribune]]}}</ref> \n\n''Georgia'' won the Grand Prix of the Americas Award for Best Picture at the [[Montreal World Film Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Evans |first=Greg |date=1995-09-25 |title=Miramax has \u2018Georgia\u2019 on its mind |url=https://variety.com/1995/film/features/miramax-has-georgia-on-its-mind-99129066/ |access-date=2022-09-20 |website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |language=en-US}}</ref> Leigh received Best Actress honors at the Montreal World Film Festival and the [[New York Film Critics Circle]] for her performance, while Winningham received an [[Independent Spirit Awards|Independent Spirit Award]] for Best Supporting Actress as well as Best Supporting Actress nominations at the [[Academy Awards]] and from the [[Screen Actors Guild]]. \n\n== Plot ==\nGeorgia Flood is a successful, talented and well-adjusted [[folk music]] singer who is happily married and a mother of two. Her younger sister Sadie is passionate but self-destructive and lacking in technical skill. She tries to match Georgia's accomplishments by becoming the lead singer for a [[punk rock]] band, but is unsuccessful and destroys herself through [[Substance abuse|drug abuse]]. Sadie comes to Georgia seeking help after she ends a relationship. Leigh's mother, [[Barbara Turner (actress)|Barbara Turner]], wrote the screenplay; Leigh and Turner co-produced it along with director [[Ulu Grosbard]], and [[Mare Winningham]], a longtime friend who had been Leigh's camp counselor during their teen years, co-starred.<ref name=\"auto1\"/><ref name=\"metroactive\" /><ref name=\":3\">{{Cite web |last=Turan |first=Kenneth |date=December 8, 1995 |title=MOVIE REVIEW : 'Georgia' Has Heart and Soul |url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1995-12-08-ca-11666-story.html |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090703162948/http://www.calendarlive.com/movies/reviews/cl-movie960406-25,0,1027700.story |archive-date=July 3, 2009 |website=[[Los Angeles Times]]}}</ref>\n\nThe music in the film consists of 13 songs; to create a realistic effect, Leigh and Winningham were both filmed singing live. The 13 songs included covers of songs by [[Gladys Knight & the Pips]], [[Elvis Costello]] and [[Van Morrison]].<ref name=\"auto1\"/> In the talked-about centerpiece of the film, Sadie drunkenly reception ===\nOn review aggregate website [[Rotten Tomatoes]], ''Georgia'' has an approval rating of 80% based on 25 reviews.<ref name=\":0\" /> \n\nSusan Wloszczyna of ''[[USA Today]]'' described the film as \"a painful though sadly humorous portrait of sisterhood\".<ref>{{Cite news |last=Wloszczyna |first=Susan |date=December 8, 1995 |title=Georgia film review |work=[[USA Today]]}}</ref> In a 3.5/4-star review, [[Roger Ebert]] said ''Georgia'' \"is not a simply plotted movie about descent and recovery, but a complex, deeply knowledgeable story about how alcoholism and mental illness really are family diseases; Sadie's sickness throws everybody off, and their adjustments to it don't make them healthier people.\"<ref name=\"ebert\" /> \n\n[[James Berardinelli]] of ''ReelViews'' praised it as \u201ca tour de force for Leigh... there are times when it's uncomfortable to watch this performance because it's so powerful\u201d, adding \u201c''Georgia'' doesn't possess an amazingly original narrative, but what distinguishes this picture is the depth of the characters and the amazing power with which the two leads breathe life into them.\u201d<ref>{{cite web|url=http://preview.reelviews.net/movies/g/georgia.html|title=Georgia :: reelviews.net :: Reviews|last=Berardinelli|first=James|work=reelviews.net|access-date=2017-07-01}}</ref> [[Kenneth Turan]] of the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' wrote that \u201cLeigh\u2019s exceptional performance tears you apart\u2026 we\u2019ve never seen anything like it before\u201d, adding that \"''Georgia'' is not an easy film, but in the American independent arena, it outperforms everything in sight.\u201d<ref name=\":3\" /> Barbara Shulgasser of the ''[[San Francisco Examiner]]'' wrote, \"What Leigh succeeds at conveying so well is the desperation of a young woman whose passion for art exceeds her capacity to express herself artistically...Because of [her] powerful performance we glean that '''Georgia''<nowiki/>' is really not about drug abuse or sibling rivalry, or the frustration of the untalented...but about talent [itself].\"<ref>{{Cite web |last=Shulgasser |first=Barbara |date=1996-01-12 |title=Georgia |url=https://www.sfgate.com/news/article/GEORGIA-3153696.php |access-date=2022-09-20 |website=[[San Francisco Examiner]] |language=en-US}}</ref>\n\nJennifer Jason Leigh was voted the year's [[New York Film Critics Circle Award for Best Actress|Best Actress]] by the [[New York Film Critics Circle]] and at the [[Montreal World Film Festival]], nominated for an [[Independent Spirit Award]], and was widely predicted to receive her first [[Academy Awards|Oscar]] nomination for the role.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/movies/features/jennifer-jason-leigh-19951130 |title=Rolling Stone: Jennifer Jason Leigh |last1=Dunn |first=Jancee |work=Rolling Stone |date=November 30, 1995 In my mind she will always be the greatest performance of that year, and a lot of other people thought so, too. [[Meryl Streep]] grabbed me at the Academy Awards.
