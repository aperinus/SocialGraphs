It stars [[Sumiko Sakamoto]] as Orin, [[Ken Ogata]], and [[Shoichi Ozawa]]. It is an adaptation of the book ''Narayama bushik\u014d'' by [[Shichir\u014d Fukazawa]]<ref>{{cite journal|last1=O\u2019Donoghue|first1=Darragh|title=Ballad of Narayama|journal=Cin\u00e9math\u00e8que Annotations on Film|date=February 2013|issue=66|url=http://sensesofcinema.com/2013/cteq/ballad-of-narayama/|access-date=19 August 2016}}</ref> and slightly inspired by the [[The Ballad of Narayama (1958 film)|1958 film]] directed by [[Keisuke Kinoshita]]. Both films explore the legendary practice of ''[[ubasute]]'', in which elderly people were carried to a mountain and abandoned to die. Imamura's film won the [[Palme d'Or]] at the [[1983 Cannes Film Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Narayama-Bushi-Ko|url=http://www.festival-cannes.fr/en/archives/ficheFilm/id/BF3E4E90-D7BD-41A8-A9C2-AD1B36C18136/year/1983.html|website=Festival de Cannes|access-date=19 August 2016}}</ref>\n\n==Production==\n''The Ballad of Narayama'' was filmed in [[Niigata Prefecture]] and [[Nagano Prefecture]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0084390/locations?ref_=tt_dt_dt|title=The Ballad of Narayama (1983) - IMDb|website=[[IMDb]]}}</ref>\n\n== Plot ==\nThe film is set in a small rural village in Japan in the 19th century. According to tradition, once a person reaches the age of 70 he or she must travel to a remote mountain to die of starvation, a practice known as [[ubasute]]. The story concerns Orin, who is 69 and of sound health, but notes that a neighbor had to drag his father to the mountain, so she resolves to avoid clinging to life beyond her term. She spends a year arranging all the affairs of her family and village: she severely punishes a family who are hoarding food, and helps her younger son lose his virginity.\n\nThe film has some harsh scenes that show how brutal the conditions could be for the villagers. Interspersed between episodes in the film are brief vignettes of nature \u2013 birds, snakes, and other animals hunting, watching, singing, copulating or giving birth.\n\n==Cast==\n* [[Ken Ogata]]
