It stars [[Ugo Tognazzi]] and [[Michel Serrault]] as a gay couple operating a [[drag (clothing)|drag]] nightclub in a French resort town, [[R\u00e9mi Laurent]] as the former's son, and [[Michel Galabru]] and [[Carmen Scarpitta]] as his new fianc\u00e9e's ultra-conservative parents. The French-language picture was a Franco-Italian co-production by [[United Artists]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=La Cage aux folles (1978)|url=https://www2.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2b6df51541|access-date=2021-09-01|website=BFI|language=en}}</ref> This was the first film adaptation of Poiret's play, which was later adapted into the 1996 American film ''[[The Birdcage]]''.\n\nThe film was released in Italy on October 20, 1978 and in France on October 25. It was a considerable commercial success, becoming one of the highest-grossing foreign-language films released in the United States of all time. It won the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Foreign Language Film]] and was nominated for three [[Academy Awards|Oscars]]: [[Academy Award for Best Director|Best Director]] (Molinaro), [[Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay|Best Adapted Screenplay]], and [[Academy Award for Best Costume Design|Best Costume Design]]. Michel Serrault won the [[C\u00e9sar Award for Best Actor]]. It was followed by two sequels, with Tognazzi, Serrault, and Galabru reprising their roles.\n\n==Plot==\nLike the play upon which it is based, the film tells the story of a middle-aged [[Homosexual|gay]] couple \u2013 Renato Baldi, the manager of a [[Saint-Tropez]] nightclub featuring [[drag (clothing)|drag]] entertainment, and Albin Mougeotte, his star attraction \u2013 and the madness that ensues when Renato's son Laurent brings home his fianc\u00e9e Andrea and her ultra-conservative parents to meet them.\n\n==Cast==\n{{castlist|\n* [[Ugo Tognazzi]] as Renato Baldi\n* [[Michel Serrault]] as Albin Mougeotte/'Zaza Napoli'\n* [[Claire Maurier]] as Simone Deblon\n* [[R\u00e9mi Laurent]] as Laurent Baldi\n* [[Michel Galabru]] as Simon Charrier\n* [[Carmen Scarpitta]] as Louise Charrier\n*Luisa Maneri as Andr\u00e9a Charrier\n* [[Benny Luke]] as Jacob\n*[[Liana Del Balzo]] as Madame Charrier\n*[[Venantino Venantini]] as the Charriers' chauffeur\n*Vinicio Diamanti as Mercedes\n*[[Carlo Reali]] as Bouncer\n*[[Guido Cerniglia]] as Doctor\n*Peter Boom as Waiter\n*Piero Mazzinghi as Journalist\n*Walter Lucchini as Blouson-Noir}}\n\n== Production ==\nExterior filming was on-location in [[Saint-Tropez]] and [[Nice]], while interiors were shot at Dear Film and [[Cinecitta Studios]] in [[Rome]], Italy.\n\n==Reception==\n===Box office===\n{{As of|2020}}, ''La Cage aux Folles'' has remained the no. 11 <ref>[https://www.boxofficemojo.com/genre/sg4208980225/?ref_=bo_gs_table_195 Foreign Language Movies at the Box Office]. Box Office Mojo.</ref> foreign-language film released in the United States of America. The film was the second highest-grossing film of the year in France with 5,406,614 admissions. In Germany, it received 2.65 million admissions, making it the 11th highest-grossing film of the year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jpbox-office.com/fichfilm.php?id=7776|title=La Cage aux Folles (1978) - JPBox-Office|website=www.jpbox-office.com}}</ref>\n\n===Critical response===\nOn Rotten Tomatoes, the film has a 95% rating based on 21 reviews, with an average rating of 7.82/10. The site's consensus reads: \"''La Cage aux Folles'' is a fine French-Italian farce with flamboyant, charming characters and deep laughs\".<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/la_jaula_de_las_locas/ | title=La Cage aux Folles (1979) | work=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] | publisher=[[Fandango (company)|Fandango Media]] | access-date=1 July 2019}}</ref>\n\nRoger Ebert gave the film three-and-a-half stars out of four and wrote that \"the comic turns in the plot are achieved with such clockwork timing that sometimes we're laughing at what's funny and sometimes we're just laughing at the movie's sheer comic invention. This is a great time at the movies.\"<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/la-cage-aux-folles-1979 |title=La Cage Aux Folles |last=Ebert |first=Roger |website=[[RogerEbert.com]] |access-date=December 23, 2018 }}</ref> [[Vincent Canby]] of ''The New York Times'' wrote in a negative review that the film \"is naughty in the way of comedies that pretend to be sophisticated but actually serve to reinforce the most popular conventions and most witless stereotypes.\"<ref>Canby, Vincent (May 13, 1979). \"Film: 'Cage aux Folles,' Farce in a French Club\". ''[[The New York Times]]''. 41.</ref> Gene Siskel of the ''Chicago Tribune'' gave the film two-and-a-half stars out of four and wrote, \"For me, 'La Cage aux Folles' was over soon after it began. It's all so predictable. This could have been a [[Lucille Ball|Luci]] & [[Desi Arnaz|Desi]] comedy routine. The film's only distinctive quality is the skill of its veteran actors in working with tired material.\"<ref>Siskel, Gene (July 19, 1979). \"Acting helps, but 'La Cage' material seems tired\". ''[[Chicago Tribune]]''. Section 2, p. 5.</ref> Kevin Thomas of the ''Los Angeles Times'' called the film \"a frequently hilarious French variation on ''[[Norman, Is That You?]]'' and has the same broad humor and appeal but has been put over with considerably more aplomb.\"<ref>Thomas, Kevin (July 18, 1979). \"A French Variation on 'Norman'\". ''[[Los Angeles Times]]''. Part IV, p. 13.</ref> Gary Arnold of ''The Washington Post'' panned the film for \"stale, excruciating sex jokes\" and direction that \"has evidently failed to devise a playing rhythm to compensate for whatever farcical tempo the material enjoyed on the stage.\"<ref>Arnold, Gary (July 18, 1979). \"Feeble Farce\". ''[[The Washington Post]]''. E6.</ref>\n\n[[David McGillivray (screenwriter)|David McGillivray]] of ''[[The Monthly Film Bulletin]]'' described the film as \"a crude amalgam of ''Norman, Is That You?'' and [[John Bowen (British author)|John Bowen]]'s play ''Trevor'' ... All shrieks, mincing and limp wrists, ''La Cage aux folles'' also looks positively antiquated beside the sophisticated gay comedy of such as [[Craig Russell (Canadian actor)|Craig Russell]].\"<ref>{{cite journal |last=McGillivray |first=David |date=January 1980 |title=La Cage aux folles |journal=[[The Monthly Film Bulletin]] |volume=47 |issue=552 |page=5 }}</ref>\n\n===Awards and nominations===\n{| class=\"wikitable\"\n|-\n!
