''King's Ransom'' was released in the United States on April 22, 2005, and was a critical and commercial failure, grossing $4,143,652 against a budget of $15 million.\n\n==Plot==\nMalcolm King ([[Anthony Anderson]]) is a wealthy, selfish, obnoxious businessman who is about to divorce his wife Renee ([[Kellita Smith]]). She plans to ruin him financially during the court proceedings, and King is willing to do anything to protect his fortune.\n\nHe enlists his mistress, Peaches ([[Regina Hall]]), and her brother, Herb ([[Charlie Murphy (actor)|Charlie Murphy]]), to stage a mock kidnapping. They are to make and receive a huge ransom demand, which would keep the money safe from his wife.\n\nUnfortunately for him, two other people have similar plans to kidnap him; Angela ([[Nicole Ari Parker]]), an aggrieved employee and Corey ([[Jay Mohr]]), a good-natured yet hapless nobody who lives in his grandmother's basement and needs $10,000 after being threatened by his adopted sister.\n\n==Cast==\n* [[Anthony Anderson]] as Malcolm King\n* [[Jay Mohr]] as Corey\n* [[Kellita Smith]] as Renee King\n* [[Donald Faison]] as Andre\n* [[Regina Hall]] as Peaches Clarke\n* [[Nicole Ari Parker]] as Angela Drake\n* [[Loretta Devine]] as Miss Gladys\n* [[Charlie Murphy (actor)|Charlie Murphy]] as Herb Clarke\n* [[Leila Arcieri]] as Kim Baker\n* [[Brooke D'Orsay]] as Brooke Mayo\n* [[Roger Cross]] as Byron\n* [[Jackie Burroughs]] as Granny\n* [[Christian Potenza]] as Officer Holland\n* [[Lawrence Dane]] as Detective Conley\n* [[Lisa Marcos]] as Raven\n* [[Robert Norman Smith]] as David\n* Brenda Chrichlow as Anita\n* Carrie Colak as Lori\n* [[Kwasi Songui]] as Ronald\n* Ingrid Hart as Sheila\n* Luis Oliva as Pablo\n* Ilona Elkin as Rachel\n* [[Nicolas Wright]] as Timmy\n* Lila Yee as Miss Ho\n\n==Reception==\n===Box office===\n''King's Ransom'' was produced on a $15 million budget, but only grossed $2,137,685 on its opening weekend and ranked at #10 at the box office.
