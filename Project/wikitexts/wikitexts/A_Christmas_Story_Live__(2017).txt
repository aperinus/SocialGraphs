[[Marc Platt (producer)|Marc Platt]] reprised his role as co-executive producer from the live television musical of ''[[Grease: Live]]'' (2016), joined by Adam Siegel, and television director Alex Rudzinski and theatrical director [[Scott Ellis]]. It was broadcast from the [[Warner Brothers Studios]] in [[Burbank, California]], primarily using outdoor sets. Ellis noted that emphasis on the \"feeling of family\" was an important aspect of the production, as \"it's not just razzle-dazzle stuff and big musical numbers. Our challenge is to never lose sight of the family aspect. And I think \u2014 I hope \u2014 we've succeeded in that\". Songwriting duo [[Pasek and Paul|Pasek & Paul]], who wrote the songs for ''A Christmas Story: The Musical'', wrote three new songs for the production, including \"In the Market for a Miracle\" for Mrs. Schwartz, \"Ralphie's Nightmare\" for the Parker family, and the opening theme \"Count on Christmas\".<ref name=\"latimes-xmasstorylive\">{{Cite news |url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/tv/la-et-st-christmas-story-live-20171216-story.html |title=The leg lamp! The frozen pole! Fox prepares to bring a holiday classic to life with 'A Christmas Story Live!' |last=Villarreal |first=Yvonne |date=2017-12-16 |newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]] |access-date=2017-12-18 |issn=0458-3035}}</ref>\n\nPasek noted that the team had to try to maintain a balance between preserving the familiar and iconic aspects of the film, while also expanding upon it through its musical format. He explained that \"there are certain moments where we want to stay true to what people remember and then there's other moments where we have permission to do what only musical theater can do and turn the dad's fantasy into a tap dancing number\". Platt remarked that, when working on a project such as this, \"you prepare for the unknown. That's what makes live TV exciting. There are certain things that happen that you can't anticipate\".<ref name=\"latimes-xmasstorylive\"/>\n\nUnlike previous years, [[NBC]] did not broadcast a live musical for the 2017 holiday season; while it did plan to produce a version of ''[[Bye Bye Birdie]]'' executive-produced by and starring [[Jennifer Lopez]], it was delayed and eventually abandoned.<ref name=\"Keveney\">{{cite news |date=17 December 2017 |last=Keveney |first=Bill |work=[[USA Today]] |title=Behold the leg lamp: Fox's 'A Christmas Story' offers live-musical take on holiday classic |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/tv/2017/12/14/christmas-story-live-fox-maya-rudolph-matthew-broderick/947183001/ |access-date=1 September 2021}}</ref><ref name=\"latimes-xmasstorylive\"/> The broadcast ultimately competed against [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]'s annual presentation of the film ''[[The Sound of Music (film)|The Sound of Music]]'', as well as NBC's ''[[NBC Sunday Night Football|Sunday Night Football]]'' between the [[Dallas Cowboys]] and [[Oakland Raiders]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://deadline.com/2017/12/cowboys-win-sunday-night-football-ratings-up-christmas-story-live-flounders-sound-of-music-movie-fox-nfl-nbc-1202229044/|title='Sunday Night Football' Ratings Up, Low 'Christmas Story Live!' Topped By 'Sound Of Music' Movie Encore In Viewers|last=Patten|first=Dominic|date=2017-12-18|work=Deadline|access-date=2017-12-18}}</ref>\n\nDuring the special, Fox also broadcast a live, 2-and-a-half-minute commercial for ''[[The Greatest Showman]]'', a then-upcoming musical film that also featured songs written by Pasek and Paul. The spot, which was directed by [[Michael Gracey]] and [[Beth McCarthy-Miller]], and also simulcast on [[Facebook]], featured a performance of the number \"Come Alive\" with the film's stars [[Hugh Jackman]], [[Zac Efron]], [[Keala Settle]], and [[Zendaya]]. The presentation was billed as the first live television commercial for a film.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://deadline.com/2017/12/the-greatest-showman-hugh-jackman-zac-efron-zendaya-keala-settle-live-movie-trailer-a-christmas-story-1202228961/|title='The Greatest Showman' Live Movie Trailer Electrifies During 'A Christmas Story Live'|last=Ramos|first=Dino-Ray|date=2017-12-18|work=Deadline Hollywood |access-date=2017-12-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=December 18, 2017 |last=Natividad |first=Angela |url=http://www.adweek.com/creativity/hugh-jackmans-live-ad-last-night-for-the-greatest-showman-was-quite-the-circus/ |title=Hugh Jackman's Live Ad Last Night for The Greatest Showman Was Quite the Circus|work=Adweek|access-date=2021-09-01}}</ref>\n\n==Reception==\n===Critical response===\n''A Christmas Story Live!'' received generally negative reviews, with the general audience reacting more harshly than professional critics.<ref>{{cite web |last=Melas |first=Chloe |title='A Christmas Story Live' didn't bring the holiday cheer |url=http://www.cnn.com/2017/12/18/entertainment/a-christmas-story-bad-reviews/index.html |website=CNN |quote=widely panned by both viewers and critics |access-date=1 September 2021}}</ref> On [[Rotten Tomatoes]], the production received a 43% rating based on 14 reviews.<ref>{{cite web |title=A Christmas Story Live! |url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/a_christmas_story_live/ |website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]}}</ref> The show's three-hour length and musical numbers drew the most negative reactions, as Fox did not promote the program as a musical, and the lavish Broadway musical numbers were perceived to clash with the story's Midwestern setting.<ref>{{cite web |last=Longeretta |first=Emily |title='A Christmas Story Live!' Viewers Confused by Musical Event \u2014 Read the Reactions |url=https://www.usmagazine.com/entertainment/news/a-christmas-story-live-viewers-confused-by-musical-event-read-the-reactions/ |website=Us Weekly |access-date=December 18, 2017 |date=December 18, 2017}}</ref> However, most of the individual actors' performances were positively received, as were the technical aspects of the special (such as scene and wardrobe changes).\n\nThe ''Los Angeles Times'' praised the performers: \"...Matthew Broderick, who found his own, gentler way through Shepherd's pointy prose...Maya Rudolph was affecting as Ralphie's mother, Chris Diamantopoulos gruff if less than epic as his father, the Old Man...Andy Walken was a good visual match for the movie's Peter Billingsley,...The whole company of kids, in fact, was energetic and on their marks,...\nSchwartz's mother, previously an offended voice on the other end of a phone call, was here gloriously embodied by Ana Gasteyer, who brought in a Hanukkah theme...\" but noted that \"...the virtues that have made the movie a seasonal perennial were somewhat swallowed by the narrative digressions and showstopping gestures of the musical\".<ref>{{cite web |last=Lloyd |first=Robert |title='A Christmas Story Live!' gets merry with Maya Rudolph and a winning Ralphie |url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/tv/la-et-st-a-christmas-story-live-review-20171217-story.html |website=Los Angeles Times |date=December 17, 2017}}</ref>\n\n===Ratings===\nThe overnight [[Nielsen ratings]] were a 2.8 rating/5 share with 4.52 million viewers,<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20171218232805/http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/daily-ratings/tv-ratings-sunday-dec-17-2017/ \"TV Ratings Sunday Dec 17, 2017\"] zap2it.com</ref> as compared to the Fox network live telecast of ''Grease: Live'' in January 2016, which earned a 7.4 overnight and 12 million viewers.<ref>{{cite web |last=McPhee |first=Ryan |title=''A Christmas Story Live!''
