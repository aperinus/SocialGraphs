'''''Zentropa''''' in North America) is a 1991 [[Experimental film|experimental]] [[political film|political]] [[psychological drama]] film<ref>{{Citation |title=Europa (1991) |url=https://www.filmaffinity.com/uk/film160114.html |language=en |access-date=2022-10-13}}</ref><ref>{{Citation |title=Zentropa (1991) - Lars von Trier {{!}} Synopsis, Characteristics, Moods, Themes It is von Trier's third theatrical feature film and the final film in his [[Europa trilogy]] following ''[[The Element of Crime]]'' (1984) and ''[[Epidemic (film)|Epidemic]]'' (1987).<ref name=\"festival-cannes.com\" />\n\nThe film features an [[Ensemble cast|international ensemble cast]], including French-American [[Jean-Marc Barr]], Germans [[Barbara Sukowa]] and [[Udo Kier]], [[expatriate]] American [[Eddie Constantine]], and Swedes [[Max von Sydow]] and [[Ernst-Hugo J\u00e4reg\u00e5rd]].\n\n''Europa'' was influenced by [[Franz Kafka]]'s ''[[Amerika (novel)|Amerika]]'', and the title was chosen \"as an echo\" of that novel.<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=voTOt3GaRJAC&pg=PA83&lpg=PA83&dq=europa+lars+von+trier+kafka&source=bl&ots=uDKThwAz6h&sig=sIQF7Op80s5yLQbXttKDz65a0Wo&hl=en&sa=X&ei=sCjvUdbPE4KHswb7qIGwCg&ved=0CCoQ6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=europa%20lars%20von%20trier%20kafka&f=false Lars Von Trier: Interviews, pp. 82-83]</ref>\n\n== Plot ==\nA young, idealistic American hopes to \"show some kindness\" to the [[Germany|German]] people soon after the end of [[World War II]]. In [[Allied Occupation Zones in Germany|US-occupied Germany]], he takes on work as a sleeping-car conductor for the Zentropa railway network, falls in love with a ''[[femme fatale]]'', and becomes embroiled in a pro-[[Nazism|Nazi]] [[Werwolf|terrorist conspiracy]].\n\n== Cast ==\n{{castlist|\n* [[Max von Sydow]] as the Narrator\n* [[Jean-Marc Barr]] as Leopold Kessler\n* [[Barbara Sukowa]] as Katharina Hartmann\n* [[Udo Kier]] as Lawrence Hartmann\n* [[Ernst-Hugo J\u00e4reg\u00e5rd]] as Uncle Kessler\n* Henning Jensen as Siggy\n* [[Erik M\u00f8rk]] as Pater\n* [[Eddie Constantine]] as Colonel Harris\n* [[J\u00f8rgen Reenberg]] as Max Hartmann\n* Benny Poulsen as Steleman\n* Erno M\u00fcller as Heifer\n* Michael Phillip Simpson as Robins\n* Holger Perfort as Mr. Ravenstein\n* Anne Werner Thomsen as Mrs. Ravenstein\n* [[Lars von Trier]] as Jew\n* [[Baard Owe]] as Man with papers\n}}\n\n== Style ==\n[[File:EuropaFilmStyle.jpg|thumb|left|Screenshot illustrating the film's use of black and white images mixed with colour, and of characters interacting with back projections]]\n''Europa'' employs an [[Experimental filmmaker|experimental]] style of cinema, combining largely [[black and white]] visuals with occasional intrusions of [[color|colour]], having actors interact with [[rear projection|rear-projected]] footage, and layering different images over one another to [[surrealism|surreal]] effect. The [[voice-over]] narration uses an unconventional [[second-person narrative]] imitative of a hypnotist (e.g. \"On the count of ten, you will be in Europa.\").\n\nThe film's characters, music, dialogue, and plot are self-consciously [[melodramatic]] and [[Irony|ironically]] imitative of [[film noir]] conventions.\n\n== Production ==\nThe film was shot throughout Poland ([[Chojna]] Cathedral (Marienkirche) and the Chojna Roundhouse) and in Denmark ([[Nordisk Film]] studios, Copenhagen and the Copenhagen Dansk Hydraulisk Institut)\n\nVon Trier's production company, [[Zentropa]] Entertainments, is named after the sinister railway network featured in this film, which is in turn named after the real-life train company [[Mitropa]].\n\n== Reception ==\n''Europa'' was released as ''Zentropa'' in [[North America]] to avoid confusion with ''[[Europa Europa]]'' (1990).\n\n=== Critical reception ===\n{{Expand section|date=February 2016}}\nThe film received largely positive reviews from critics. [[Review aggregator]] website [[Rotten Tomatoes]] reports an 85% score based on 13 reviews, with an average rating of 7.3/10.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/zentropa/ |title=Zentropa (Europa) (1992) |work=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] | access-date=8 March 2018}}</ref>\n\n=== Accolades ===\nThe film won three awards at the [[1991 Cannes Film Festival]] (Best Artistic Contribution, [[Jury Prize (Cannes Festival)|Jury Prize]], and Technical Grand Prize).<ref name=\"festival-cannes.com\">{{cite web |url=http://www.festival-cannes.com/en/archives/ficheFilm/id/91/year/1991.html |title=Festival de Cannes: Europa |access-date=8 September 2009 |work=festival-cannes.com}}</ref> Upon realizing that he had not won the [[Palme d'Or]], von Trier gave the judges [[Finger (gesture)|the finger]] and stormed out of the venue.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://rogerebert.suntimes.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/19920703/REVIEWS/207030301/1023 |work=Chicago Sun-Times |title=Zentropa}}</ref>\n\n== Home media ==\n[[The Criterion Collection]] released the film on DVD in 2008.
