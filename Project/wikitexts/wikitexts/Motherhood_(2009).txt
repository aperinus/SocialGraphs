I don't think any man can understand what it's like to face the day to day the way a woman can, what it means for a woman to be compromised by domesticity.|Dieckmann, on her film ''Motherhood''.<ref name=\"nytbelkin\"/>}}\nThe writer/director's \"real life was the inspiration for the film\";<ref name=\"nytbelkin\"/> Dieckmann's home consists of two [[Rent control in New York|rent-stabilized]] apartments on the same floor of a [[West Village]] building, with one apartment for the bedrooms, and the other containing a kitchen, office and living room. In the film; Thurman's character \"lives in [literally the] same building, in a bisected apartment.\"<ref name=\"nytbelkin\">{{cite web| url= https://www.nytimes.com/2009/10/18/movies/18belk.html | title= Mommy Tracks, on Screen and Off | date= October 16, 2009| first=Lisa | last= Belkin | work=The New York Times| accessdate=November 15, 2010}}</ref>  Filming took place in New York City starting in May 2008 and lasting about 25 days.<ref name=variety/>\n\n==Release==\n''Motherhood'' received a [[limited release]] in the United States on October 23, 2009, by [[Freestyle Releasing]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.variety.com/article/VR1118005821 |first=Dave | last= McNary| title=Freestyle sets date for 'Motherhood'|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]| date=July 9, 2009| accessdate=July 16, 2009}}</ref>\n\nIn March 2010, the film's British premiere was confined to a single [[London]] cinema: the [[Apollo Cinemas|Apollo Piccadilly Circus]]. The box office gross was \u00a39 on its opening night and \u00a388 on its opening weekend; eleven viewers purchased a ticket, with only one person attending its first showing.<ref name=\"guard\">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2010/mar/26/uma-thurman-motherhood-flop|title=The Uma Thurman film so bad it made \u00a388 on opening weekend|work=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=March 27, 2010|location=London|date=March 26, 2010|first=Amelia|last=Hill}}</ref><ref name=\"times\">{{cite web|url=http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/tol/arts_and_entertainment/film/article7078845.ece|title=Uma Thurman film is mother of all flops|work=[[The Sunday Times]]|first=Kit|last=Codling|date=March 28, 2010|accessdate=November 15, 2010|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110615074914/http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/tol/arts_and_entertainment/film/article7078845.ece|archivedate=June 15, 2011}}</ref> Veteran film critic [[Barry Norman]] said, \"It's a reasonable assumption that there was a marketing and advertising catastrophe, and people didn't know it was showing.\"<ref name=\"guard\"/>\n\n==Reception==\nThe film received generally negative reviews. On [[review aggregator]] [[Rotten Tomatoes]], the film holds an approval rating of 20% based on 51 reviews, with an average rating of 3.88/10. The website's critics consensus reads: \"Despite [[Uma Thurman]]'s comic skills, ''Motherhood''{{'}}s contrived set-ups and clich\u00e9d jokes keep this comedy from delivering laughs \u2013 or insights into modern parenting.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/motherhood/ |title=Motherhood |work=Rotten Tomatoes | accessdate=May 25, 2020}}</ref> In October 2009, [[Roger Ebert]] gave the film two stars out of four, saying the film is \"billed as a comedy, but at no point will you require oxygen. There are some smiles and chuckles and a couple of actual laughs, but the overall effect is underwhelming\"; Thurman is \"doing her best with a role that may offer her less than any other in her career, even though she's constantly onscreen.\"<ref>{{cite web| url= http://rogerebert.suntimes.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20091021/REVIEWS/910219996/1023 | title= Motherhood | date= October 21, 2009| first=Roger | last= Ebert| author-link=Roger Ebert| work=[[Chicago Sun-Times]]| accessdate=November 15, 2010}}</ref> [[A. O. Scott]] said Thurman's character is \"scattered, ambivalent, flaky and inconsistent &ndash; all of which is fine, and energetically conveyed by Ms. Thurman. But what are tolerable quirks in a person can be deadly to a narrative, and Ms. Dieckmann, trying for observational nuance, descends into trivia and wishful thinking. ... The humor is soft, the dramas are small, and the movie stumbles from loose and scruffy naturalism to sitcom tidiness.\"<ref>{{cite web| url= https://movies.nytimes.com/2009/10/23/movies/23mother.html | title= Motherhood (2009): Manhattan Mom, Burning Home Fires at Both Ends | date= October 23, 2009| first=A.O.| last= Scott | author-link=A.
