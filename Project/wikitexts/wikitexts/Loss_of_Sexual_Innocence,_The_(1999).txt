web|url=http://www.the-numbers.com/movie/Loss-of-Sexual-Innocence#tab=summary|title=The Loss of Sexual Innocence (1999) - Financial Information}}</ref>\n}}\n'''''The Loss of Sexual Innocence''''' is a 1999 film written and directed by [[Mike Figgis]].<ref name=figgis20000107>{{cite news |first=Mike |last=Figgis |date=January 7, 2000 |title=Hollywood Beyond |url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2000/jan/07/1 |work=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> It tells the story of the sexual development of a filmmaker through three stages of his life, in a non-linear and disjointed manner.<ref>Plot summary of the [https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0126859/plotsummary film]. [[IMDb]].</ref> The film stars British actress [[Saffron Burrows]],<ref name=figgis20000107/> whom Figgis dated for several years.<ref>{{cite news |first=Tim |last=Cooper |date=May 5, 2002 |title=A hint of Saffron |url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2002/may/05/features.magazine |work=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref>\n\n==Plot==\nThe story at the center of the movie is the tumultuous life of Nic (Julian Sands), a British [[film director|director]] beginning a new film project in [[Tunisia]].\n\n==Cast==\n* [[Julian Sands]] as Adult Nic\n** [[Jonathan Rhys Meyers]] as Nic, aged 16\n* [[Saffron Burrows]] as English/Italian Twin\n* [[Stefano Dionisi]] as Luca\n* [[Kelly Macdonald]] as Susan\n* [[Gina McKee]] as Susan's Mother\n* [[Bernard Hill]] as Susan's Father\n* [[Rossy de Palma]] as Blind Woman\n* [[Justin Chadwick]] as Flash Man\n* [[Femi Ogunbanjo]] as Adam\n* [[Hanne Klintoe]] as Eve\n\n==Production==\n''The Loss of Sexual Innocence'' was based on a script called \u201cShort Stories,\u201d which Figgis had written in 1982.<ref>[https://www.indiewire.com/1999/05/interview-mike-figgis-loss-of-sexual-innocence-82228/ Kaufman, Anthony. \"Interview: Mike Figgis' ''Loss of Sexual Innocence'',\" IndieWire, May 1999.] Retrieved October 27, 2020</ref> His original concept was to have been a multimedia show featuring film, live performance and music. The success of ''[[Leaving Las Vegas]]'' allowed him to finance the making of the nearly two-decades-old cinematic project.<ref name=\"dhowethomsontwp\">[https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/1999/06/25/at-a-loss-with-sexual-innocence/9a19f594-7f09-4a30-beec-aac7bd09d7d1/ Howe, Desson. \"At a Loss With ''Sexual Innocence'',\" ''The Washington Post'', Friday, June 25, 1999.] Retrieved October 27, 2020</ref>\n\n==Reception==\n[[Stephen Holden]] of ''[[The New York Times]]'' wrote, \"Those with no patience for avant-garde films will want to avoid ''The Loss of Sexual Innocence''...Everyone else will find moments of surpassing beauty in this courageous, deeply flawed film.\" He was effusive in his praise for Figgis creating the film's atmosphere \"through its mixture of music, beautiful outdoor cinematography and somber, silent acting.\" What he considered flaws were the Adam and Eve storyline and two scenes which he considered irrelevant involving an airport encounter between identical twins separated at birth and the humiliation of an obese 12-year-old Nic by a gym teacher.<ref>[https://archive.nytimes.com/www.nytimes.com/library/film/052899loss-film-review.html Holden, Stephen. \"''The Loss of Sexual Innocence'': The Story of Adam and Eve, Sort Of,\" ''The New York Times'', Friday, May 28, 1999.] Retrieved October 26, 2020</ref>\n\nLike Holden, [[Roger Ebert]] delineated what type of movie it is by explaining it as \"an 'art film,' which means it tries to do something more advanced than most commercial films (which tell stories simple enough for children, in images shocking enough for adults).\" He rated it {{frac|3|1|2}} [[Star (classification)|star]]s out of 4, adding that it \"plays like a musical composition, with themes drifting in and out, and dialogue used more for tone than speech....Not all of it works, but you play along, because it's rare to find a film this ambitious.\"<ref>[https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/the-loss-of-sexual-innocence-1999 Ebert, Roger. \"''The Loss of Sexual Innocence''\" (review), RogerEbert.com, Friday, June 18, 1999.] Retrieved October 26, 2020</ref>\n\nTaking a more playful approach in his review, [[Jeff Millar]] of the ''[[Houston Chronicle]]'' came to a similar conclusion as Holden and Ebert and explained, \"If you are an adventurous filmgoer, interested in technique, certainly go. If you like stories with 'stories,' which go from Point A to Point B with conflicts along the way, this film may be a little too atonal for you.\"<ref>[https://www.chron.com/entertainment/movies/article/The-Loss-of-Sexual-Innocence-1972353.php Millar, Jeff. \"''The Loss of Sexual Innocence''\" (review), ''Houston Chronicle'', Friday, July 9, 1999.] Retrieved October 27, 2020</ref>\n\n[[Emanuel Levy]] of ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' was critical of the movie as commentary on Nic\u2019s loss of innocence and the entire state of civilization, stating that \"it\u2019s in this intent that the film fails most conspicuously, giving Figgis\u2019 skeptics the strongest ammunition to dismiss his work as pretentious and overreaching.\"<ref name=\"elevyvariety\"/> [[Desson Thomson|Desson Howe]] of ''[[The Washington Post]]'' was more scathing in his analysis and declared, \"For me, at least, ''The Loss of Sexual
