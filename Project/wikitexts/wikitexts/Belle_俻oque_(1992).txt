Sobre Belle \u00c9poque, gui\u00f3n original de Rafael Azcona|first=Raquel|last=Macciuci}}</ref> and set in an idyllic countryside location during the transition to the [[Second Spanish Republic]], the film features [[Jorge Sanz]], [[Maribel Verd\u00fa]], [[Ariadna Gil]], [[Pen\u00e9lope Cruz]], [[Miriam D\u00edaz Aroca]], [[Fernando Fern\u00e1n G\u00f3mez]], [[Gabino Diego]] and [[Chus Lampreave]], among others.\n\nIt received the [[Goya Award for Best Film]] along with eight other Goya Awards and was named [[Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film|Best Foreign Language Film]] at the [[66th Academy Awards]].<ref name=\"Oscars1994\">{{Cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/1994 |title=The 66th Academy Awards (1994) Nominees and Winners |access-date=25 September 2015 |work=oscars.org}}</ref> It is a joint production among companies from Spain, Portugal and France.\n\n==Plot==\nIt is February 1931, some weeks after the failed [[Jaca uprising]] and the likewise failed {{ill|Sublevaci\u00f3n del aer\u00f3dromo de Cuatro Vientos|es|lt=Cuatro Vientos uprising}}. Spain is on the verge of the proclamation of the [[Second Spanish Republic|Second Republic]]. Fernando, a deserted private with [[Republicanism in Spain|Republican leanings]] and former [[seminarist]], is on the run from his assignment at the [[Madrid-Cuatro Vientos Airport|Cuatro Vientos base]]. After escaping from two [[Guardia Civil]] officers, he reaches the outskirts of a village, befriending Manolo, an old man with a semblance of a \"Dickensian observer of life\".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://festivaldemalaga.com/Content/source/img/publicaciones/20200401121258_51_.pdf|page=12\u201313; 111|first=Luis|last=Alegre|title=Belle \u00c9poque. Una pel\u00edcula de Fernando Trueba|year=2017|publisher=[[M\u00e1laga Film Festival|Festival de Cine de M\u00e1laga e Iniciativas Audiovisuales, S.A.]]|location=M\u00e1laga|isbn=978-84-617-8751-7}}</ref> Manolo owns a large house in the countryside, where Fernando stays for a while. Upon the arrival of Manolo's four daughters in a train, Fernando is enchanted by them all. As he meets each of the first three one by one, he falls in love and has sex with each of them, determining to marry. With each one, however, a complication arises: Clara, a widow who only recently lost her husband and who seeks solace with Fernando; Violeta, a [[lesbian]] who is attracted to Fernando only when he is dressed as a woman for a costume ball and Roc\u00edo, a social climber who is about to marry to Juanito into the village's richest family (with [[Carlism|Carlist leanings]]) for the security it would provide and who only momentarily succumbs to Fernando's charms. Heartbroken each time, the father of the girls encourages Fernando to have patience. Each of the daughters is beautiful and represents a different aspect of feminine sexuality. The youngest of the family, Luz, represents na\u00efvet\u00e9. While Fernando is pursuing her sisters, Luz gets progressively angry and jealous.
