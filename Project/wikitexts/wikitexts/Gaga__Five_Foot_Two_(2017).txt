critics. On [[Rotten Tomatoes]], it has an approval rating of 73%, based on 41 reviews, with an average rating of 6.40/10. The website's critical consensus reads, \"''Gaga: Five Foot Two'' offers an absorbing glimpse of its superstar subject's backstage life, albeit one weakened by inconsistent focus and a dearth of performance footage.\"<ref>{{cite web|title=Gaga: Five Foot Two (2017)|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/gaga_five_foot_two/|publisher=[[Fandango Media]]|work=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|access-date=December 17, 2021|archive-date=January 24, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220124062226/https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/gaga_five_foot_two|url-status=live}}</ref> On [[Metacritic]], the film has a weighted average score of 63 out of 100, based on 15 critics, indicating \"generally favorable reviews\".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/movie/gaga-five-foot-two|title=Gaga: Five Foot Two Reviews|work=[[Metacritic]]|publisher=[[CBS Interactive]]|access-date=March 7, 2018|archive-date=October 23, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201023001450/https://www.metacritic.com/movie/gaga-five-foot-two|url-status=live}}</ref>\n\nFilm critic [[Owen Gleiberman]] reviewed the documentary for ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' saying that \"Gaga radiates a potent energy \u2014 she's intensely funny and aware\", and compared it to other musical documentaries like ''[[Madonna: Truth or Dare]]'' (1990). He commended Moukarbel's direction saying that he was able to \"mix things up\", showcasing Gaga's meltdowns as well as her camaraderie with fans.<ref name=\"varrev\">{{cite magazine|url=https://variety.com/2017/film/reviews/gaga-five-foot-two-review-toronto-1202552930/|title=Toronto Film Review: 'Gaga: Five Foot Two'|last=Gleiberman|first=Owen|author-link=Owen Gleiberman|date=September 9, 2017|access-date=September 11, 2017|magazine=Variety|archive-date=September 10, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170910013147/http://variety.com/2017/film/reviews/gaga-five-foot-two-review-toronto-1202552930/|url-status=live}}</ref> Leslie Felperin from ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'' complimented the scenes in the documentary like Gaga playing \"[[Joanne (Lady Gaga song)|Joanne]]\" for her grandmother but found that despite all the jarring camerawork, the singer was more focused and scripted about her appearance. Felperin was positive about the technicalities of the film, saying \"package is assembled with competence and style, with graceful editing by Greg Arata helping to create a strong sense of story and continuity. Moukarbel and his sound editors frequently use swelling soundtrack choices and a flurry of fast edits to suggest the frenzy of Gaga's life.\"<ref name=\"holrev\">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/gaga-five-foot-two-review-1036987|title='Gaga: Five Foot Two': Film Review <nowiki>|</nowiki> TIFF 2017|last=Felperin|first=Leslie|date=September 9, 2017|access-date=September 11, 2017|magazine=The Hollywood Reporter|archive-date=September 11, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170911003139/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/gaga-five-foot-two-review-1036987|url-status=live}}</ref>\n\n==Artwork== \nThe documentary's poster and cover art, featuring rainbow waterfalls, flowers, and an extra eye streaming down Gaga\u2019s face, was created by Berlin-based German artist Pierre Schmidt (aka Dr\u00f8msjel). Dr\u00f8msjel's work, characterized by a surreal combination of vintage collage and digital manipulation, attracted the attention of the film's director, [[Chris Moukarbel]], on Instagram. According to Dr\u00f8msjel, the piece was a collaborative effort between himself, Gaga and her team, Netflix, and Dutch photography duo [[Inez and Vinoodh]], who shot the photo for the cover.<ref name=\"vogue\">{{cite web|last1=Pantano|first1=Italo|title=Dr\u00f8msjel, the artist behind the cover art for Gaga: Five Foot Two|url=https://www.vogue.it/en/news/vogue-arts/2017/09/22/dromsjel-the-artist-behind-the-cover-art-for-gaga-five-foot-two|website=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue Italia]]|language=it|date=September 22, 2017|access-date=November 27, 2018|archive-date=June 20, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180620151226/http://www.vogue.it/en/news/vogue-arts/2017/09/22/dromsjel-the-artist-behind-the-cover-art-for-gaga-five-foot-two|url-status=live}}</ref> The pair have photographed Gaga for a variety of past projects including the singer's cover art for her 2013 single, \"[[Applause (Lady Gaga song)|Applause]]\".<ref name=\"SC\">{{cite web|last1=Cain|first1=Spencer|title=Lady Gaga Releases Cover Art for New Single \"Applause\"|url=https://stylecaster.com/lady-gaga-new-single-applause/|website=StyleCaster|date=September 22, 2017|access-date=November 27, 2018|archive-date=September 25, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210925054553/https://stylecaster.com/lady-gaga-new-single-applause/|url-status=live}}</ref> According to Tilly Martin from website ''Creative Boom'', the artwork fits right in with the Dr\u00f8msjel's signature style which combines collage of vintage fashion imagery with digital manipulation to yield colorful surreal compositions.
