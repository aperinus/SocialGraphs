Dramatising real events using a documentary style, ''Battle for Haditha'' is Broomfield's follow up to ''[[Ghosts (2006 film)|Ghosts]].'' The film was aired on [[Channel 4]] in the UK on 17 March 2008.\n\n==Plot==\nThe film is inspired by the [[Haditha killings]] incident that occurred three months after the [[Battle of Haditha]] in the [[Iraq War]]. On 19 November 2005 in [[Haditha]], a city in the western Iraqi province of [[Al Anbar Governorate|Al Anbar]], 24 unarmed Iraqi men, women, and children were killed by a group of [[United States Marine Corps|United States Marines]] following an incident where an I.E.D killed one Marine and seriously wounded two others. Since the release of the film, the US military controversially<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-iraq-massacre-idUSTRE80P1JA20120126 |agency=Reuters |title=Iraq says to take legal action for Haditha victims |date=26 January 2012 |access-date=30 June 2017 |archive-date=8 October 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151008110016/http://www.reuters.com/article/2012/01/26/us-iraq-massacre-idUSTRE80P1JA20120126 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |first=Mark |last=Walker |title=Immunity grants may signal problems with Haditha prosecution |work=North County Times |date=20 April 2007 |url=http://www.nctimes.com/articles/2007/04/21/news/top_stories/1_02_204_20_07.txt |accessdate=26 August 2007}}</ref> dropped all charges to all Marines involved.<ref>[http://www.nctimes.com/articles/2008/06/04/military/ze9cdc09c13a485a08825745e0058b2c7.txt MILITARY: Haditha Marine acquitted]</ref> The names of the Marines have been changed in the film, while the Iraqi civilians retain their real names.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.timeout.com/film/features/show-feature/2905/ |title=Time Out London interview with Nick Bloomfield |accessdate=18 November 2007 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071201113001/http://www.timeout.com/film/features/show-feature/2905/ |archivedate=1 December 2007 |url-status=live}}</ref>\n\n==Production==\nShot in [[Jerash, Jordan]], the film uses former US Military personnel and Iraqi [[refugee]]s to play many of the roles.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.variety.com/review/VE1117934755.html?categoryid=31&cs=1 |title=Variety'' review of ''Battle for Haditha |accessdate=18 November 2007 |first=Robert |last=Koehler |date=17 September 2007}}</ref> However, the film was shot in an unconventional way \u2013 it was shot sequentially enabling the cast to build their characters as the story progressed. It also used real locations, and a very small documentary style film crew. This greatly added to the feeling of reality.
