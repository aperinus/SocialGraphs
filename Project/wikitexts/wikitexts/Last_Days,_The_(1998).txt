The film tells the stories of five [[Hungarian Jews]] during [[the Holocaust]] (also known as the Shoah), focusing on the last year of [[World War II]], when [[Nazi Germany]] [[Operation Margarethe|occupied Hungary]] and began [[The Holocaust in Hungary|mass deportations of Jews in the country]] to [[Nazi concentration camps|concentration]] and [[extermination camp]]s, primarily [[Auschwitz concentration camp|Auschwitz]]. It depicts on the horrors of life in the camps, but also stresses the optimism and perseverance of the survivors.<ref name=\"Guardian-2021\">{{Cite web|last=Simonpillai|first=Radheyan|date=2021-05-18|title='There is still so much hatred': looking back on Holocaust documentary The Last Days|url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2021/may/18/the-last-days-holocaust-documentary-re-release|work=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=2021-06-25}}</ref><ref name=\"Austin-1999\">{{Cite web|last=Savlov|first=Marc|date=1999-03-05|title=The Last Days|url=https://www.austinchronicle.com/events/film/1999-03-05/the-last-days/|work=[[The Austin Chronicle]]|access-date=2021-06-25}}</ref>\n\nThe film won the [[Academy Award]] for [[Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature|Best Documentary Feature]] at the [[71st Academy Awards]].<ref name=\"Guardian-2021\" /><ref name=\"NY Times\">{{cite web |url=https://movies.nytimes.com/movie/173760/The-Last-Days/details |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071204002229/http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/173760/The-Last-Days/details |url-status=dead |archive-date=2007-12-04 |department=Movies & TV Dept. |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=2007 |title=New York Times \u2022 The Last Days|accessdate=2008-11-22}}</ref> It was remastered and re-released on [[Netflix]] on May 19, 2021.<ref name=\"Guardian-2021\" />\n\n==Content==\nThe film includes archival footage, photographs, and documents, as well as new interviews with Holocaust survivors Bill Basch, [[Irene Zisblatt]], [[Ren\u00e9e Firestone]], [[Alice Lok Cahana]], [[Tom Lantos]], [[Randolph Braham]], and [[Dario Gabbai]].<ref name=\"Guardian-2021\" /> The filmmakers take the first five of those, who all immigrated to the United States after WWII, back to visit their hometowns and the sites of the camps to which they were sent. Former [[United States House of Representatives|Representative]] Lantos (D-CA) was the only Holocaust survivor ever elected to the [[United States Congress]].<ref name=\"Leonard-2000\">{{Cite web|last=Leonard|first=John|date=2000-05-29|title=Speak, Memory|url=https://nymag.com/nymetro/arts/tv/reviews/3231/|work=[[New York (magazine)|New York]]|access-date=2021-06-25}}</ref><ref name=\"Gorondi-2018\">{{Cite web|last=Gorondi|first=Pablo|date=2018-02-01|title=Statue of late Rep. Former [[SS]] doctor [[Hans M\u00fcnch]], who was [[acquittal|acquitted]] of [[war crime]]s at the [[Nuremberg trials]], is interviewed about his experiences at [[Auschwitz concentration camp]].<ref name=\"Austin-1999\" /><ref name=\"Leonard-2000\" />\n\n==Release==\n''The Last Days'' was first released in 1998, and it was remastered and re-released worldwide on [[Netflix]] on May 19, 2021.
