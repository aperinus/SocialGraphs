It premiered at the 2014 [[Cannes Film Festival]]<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.festival-cannes.fr/en/archives/ficheFilm/id/100011577/year/2014.html | title=Red Army | publisher=[[Cannes Film Festival]] | accessdate=4 November 2016}}</ref> and was released in limited theaters by [[Sony Pictures Classics]] on January 23, 2015.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://sonyclassics.com/redarmy/ | title=Red Army | work=SonyClassics.com | accessdate=6 November 2016}}</ref> The film tells the story of the [[Soviet Union national ice hockey team]] through the eyes of team captain [[Slava Fetisov]], in particular the famed 1990s five-man unit known as The [[Russian Five]].\n\nThe film details the link between sports and politics. The film also narrates how players were wooed by [[National Hockey League]] scouts and eventually flooded NHL rosters. The film is particularly harsh on the ruthless tactics of coach [[Viktor Tikhonov (ice hockey, born 1930)|Viktor Tikhonov]] about whom none of the players have a kind word. Tikhonov died in November 2014.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://variety.com/2014/film/reviews/film-review-red-army-1201211379/ | title=Film Review: 'Red Army' | work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] | date= 3 June 2014 | accessdate=6 November 2016 | author=Chang, Justin}}</ref>\n\nThe movie uses rare archival footage,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.rbth.com/arts/2015/01/26/documentary_about_legendary_soviet_hockey_team_to_hit_american_movie_the_43161.html |title=Documentary about legendary Soviet hockey team to hit American movie theatres |last=Bekreneva |first=Alexandra |date=26 January 2015 |website=Russia Beyond The Headlines |access-date=5 September 2017}}</ref> including children singing \"[[No Coward Plays Hockey]]\", song composed by [[Aleksandra Pakhmutova]] in 1968.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cultjer.com/red-army-is-simply-a-great-story-even-without-the-hockey |title=Red Army is simply a great story, even without the hockey (IDFA Review) |website=Cultjer |access-date=5 September 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://surlapatinoire.com/2015/02/08/red-army/ |title=Red Army |date=8 February 2015 |website=Sur La Patinoire |access-date=5 September 2017}}</ref>\n\n==Background==\nThe film relates the [[Soviet Union]]'s dominance of [[ice hockey]] during the [[Cold War]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2014/may/16/red-army-cannes-review-soviet-union-ice-hockey | title=Cannes 2014 review: Red Army -
