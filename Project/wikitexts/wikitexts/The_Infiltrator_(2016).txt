|website=The Numbers |access-date=May 27, 2022}}</ref>\n}}\n'''''The Infiltrator''''' is a 2016 American [[Biographical film|biographical]] [[crime film]] directed by [[Brad Furman]] and written by his mom Ellen Brown Furman. The film is based on the eponymous autobiography by Robert Mazur, a U.S. Customs special agent, who in the 1980s helped bust [[Pablo Escobar]]'s money-laundering organization by going undercover as a corrupt businessman.<ref>{{cite news |last=Hasan |first=Saad |url=https://tribune.com.pk/story/868778/the-man-behind-the-bust-a-chance-encounter-that-changed-history |title=Robert Mazur: The man behind the downfall of a Pakistani's greatest commercial achievement: Former US customs agent, Robert Mazur, reflects on the past; says it wasn't personal. |work=[[The Express Tribune]] |location=Pakistan |date=12 April 2015 |access-date=7 April 2021 |archive-date=14 April 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150414004043/http://tribune.com.pk/story/868778/the-man-behind-the-bust-a-chance-encounter-that-changed-history/}}</ref> The film stars [[Bryan Cranston]] as Mazur, with [[Diane Kruger]], [[Benjamin Bratt]], [[John Leguizamo]], [[Sa\u00efd Taghmaoui]], [[Joe Gilgun]]  and [[Amy Ryan]] in supporting roles. \n\nFilming began on February 23, 2015, in [[London]]. Both Mazur and Cranston received executive producer credits. The film premiered at the [[Tampa Theatre]] on July 6, 2016 and was released the following week in theaters. The film received a generally positive response from critics, but a [[box office bomb|poor response from audiences]], as it grossed $22 million against a budget of between $28 and $47 million.\n\n==Plot==\n{{more plot|date=August 2021}}\nDuring the 1980s, [[United States Customs Service|U.S. Customs Service]] [[special agent]] Robert Mazur uses his [[undercover]] alias \"Bob Musella\" to become a pivotal player for [[drug lords]] [[Money laundering|laundering]] their dirty cash. Later, he infiltrates the [[Medellin cartel|world's largest cartel]], and helps expose the money-laundering organization of drug lord [[Pablo Escobar]]<ref name=\"FirstLook\" /> and take down the [[Bank of Credit and Commerce International]] (BCCI), which had secretly taken illegal ownership of First American Bankshares in [[Washington, D.C.]]\n\n==Cast==\n* [[Bryan Cranston]] as US Customs Special Agent Robert Mazur / Bob Musella<ref name=CranstonCast>{{cite news |last1=Szalai |first1=Georg |title=Bryan Cranston Attached to Star in Thriller 'The Infiltrator' |date=October 8, 2014 |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/bryan-cranston-attached-star-thriller-739126 |work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |access-date=February 15, 2015}}</ref>\n* [[Diane Kruger]] as Kathy Ertz<ref name=\"KrugerCast\" />\n* [[John Leguizamo]] as Emir Abreu<ref name=\"DeadlineCast\" />\n* [[Benjamin Bratt]] as Roberto Alcaino<ref name=\"BrattCast\" />\n* [[Amy Ryan]] as Bonni Tischler\n* [[Jason Isaacs]] as Mark Jackowski\n* [[Joe Gilgun]] as Dominic\n* [[Daniel Mays]] as Frankie\n* [[Yul Vazquez]] as Javier Ospina\n* [[Sim\u00f3n Andreu]] as Gonzalo Mora Sr.\n* [[Rub\u00e9n Ochandiano]] as Gonzalo Mora Jr.\n* [[Juliet Aubrey]] as Evelyn Mazur<ref name=\"DeadlineCast\" />\n* [[Olympia Dukakis]] as Aunt Vicky\n* [[Sa\u00efd Taghmaoui]] as Amjad Awan\n* [[Tom Vaughan-Lawlor]] as Steve Cook<ref name=\"KrugerCast\" />\n* [[Elena Anaya]] as Gloria Alcaino<ref name=\"DeadlineCast\" />\n* [[Dinita Gohil]] as Farhana Awan\n* Carsten Hayes as Rudy Armbrecht\n* Juan Cely as The Informant\n* [[Andy Beckwith]] as Joe\n* [[Michael Par\u00e9]] as [[Barry Seal]]\n* [[Mark Holden (actor)|Mark Holden]] as Eric Wellman\n\n== Production ==\nThe project was first announced by ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'' on October 8, 2014, with [[Brad Furman]] as director and [[Bryan Cranston]] as Robert Mazur; Miriam Segal produced the film for her Good Films banner along with George Films.
