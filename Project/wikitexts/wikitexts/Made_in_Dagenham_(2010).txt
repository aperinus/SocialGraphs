It dramatises the [[Ford sewing machinists strike of 1968]] that aimed for [[equal pay for women]]. Its theme song, with lyrics by [[Billy Bragg]], is performed by [[Sandie Shaw]], a native of the area and former [[Ford Dagenham]] clerk.\n\nA stage [[Made in Dagenham (musical)|musical]] version of the film opened at [[London]]'s [[Adelphi Theatre]] in 2014.\n\n==Plot==\n{{more plot|date=October 2015}}\nBased on a true story, ''Made in Dagenham'' explores the movement that caused a significant law reform. Rita O'Grady (a fictional character) leads the [[Ford sewing machinists strike of 1968|1968 Ford sewing machinists strike]] at the [[Ford Dagenham|Ford Dagenham plant]], where female workers walk out to protest [[sexual discrimination]], demanding equal pay. The strike drew major attention around the world because it was considered contrary to women's traditional family roles. The successful strike led to the [[Equal Pay Act 1970]].\n<ref>{{cite news|last1=Allott|first1=Serena|title=Made in Dagenham: set review.<ref>{{rotten-tomatoes|id=made_in_dagenham}}</ref> ''[[Maclean's]]'', in a review for the film's [[2010 Toronto International Film Festival]] premiere, called it a \".. combination of ''[[Milk (2008 American film)|Milk]]'' and ''[[Mad Men]]''.. It\u2019s a film that blatantly condemns sexism and shows, despite its mostly light tone, the real cost of fighting for civil rights. The [[Beehive (hairstyle)|bee-hived]] and [[Bob cut|bobbed]] characters are fully fleshed and well-rounded even though they fit into \u201960s archetypes,  and the [[period piece]] balances optimism and realism in a way that\u2019s both compelling and fun to watch.\"<ref>{{cite web | url= http://www.macleans.ca/2010/09/09/made-in-dagenham/ | title= 'Made in Dagenham': Sweet 1960s sensibility and labour unions | date= 9 September 2010 | publisher= [[Maclean's]] | access-date= 2010-10-05 }}</ref>\n\nXan Brooks of ''[[The Guardian]]'' gave it three stars out of five, calling it \".. uncomplicated fare, overly spiced with 60s cliches.... But the film is also robust, amiable and so warm-hearted you'd be a churl to take against it.\",<ref>{{cite news| url= https://www.theguardian.com/film/2010/sep/21/made-in-dagenham-film-review | title= Made in Dagenham | date= 21 September 2010 | newspaper=[[The Guardian]]| access-date=2010-10-05}}</ref> while David Cox, also of ''The Guardian'', gave a less glowing review, suggesting that, despite initial potential, \".. a promising opportunity has been squandered.\"<ref>{{cite news| url= https://www.theguardian.com/film/filmblog/2010/sep/13/made-in-dagenham-first-look | title= Made in Dagenham: a squandered opportunity | date= 13 September 2010 | newspaper=[[The Guardian]]| access-date=2011-01-04}}</ref>\n\nRoger Ebert gave the film three and a half stars out of four.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/made-in-dagenham-2010|title=They thought they should be paid like men|date=23 November 2010|website=Rogerebert.com|access-date=17 May 2020}}</ref>  [[Mark Kermode]] praised the film highly on his weekly show on BBC ''[[Radio 5 Live]]''.
