O. Scott|author=Scott, A. O.|date=August 4, 2006}}</ref>\n\n==Plot==\nThe film concerns the relationships between men, women, brothers, mothers, fathers, and friends. The protagonist discovers on [[eBay]] a replica of a lounge chair that was used by his father long ago. The resulting road trip to pick up and deliver the chair as a birthday present for the father in [[Atlanta]] takes interesting twists.\n\n==Cast==\n* [[Mark Duplass]] as Josh\n* [[Katie Aselton]] as Emily\n* Rhett Wilkins as Rhett\n* Julie Fischer as Amber\n* Larry Duplass as Josh's Dad\n* Cindy Duplass as Josh's Mom\n* Jim Whalen as Doctor\n\n==Production==\nThe film was made for $15,000, money borrowed from the Duplass's parents.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indiewire.com/article/indiewire_interview_jay_duplass_and_mark_duplass_creators_of_the_puffy_chair|title=indieWIRE INTERVIEW: Jay Duplass and Mark Duplass, Creators of \"The Puffy Chair\"|publisher=Indiewire.com|first=Eugene|last=Herandez|date=July 17, 2006|accessdate=March 17, 2016}}</ref> All of the film's actors were paid $100 a day, with extensive [[improvisation]] used.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.yahoo.com/movies/the-puffy-chair-duplass-brothers-125509170977.html|title=The Puffy Chair,' 10 Years Later: How a Little Indie Flick Predicted the Future of Film|publisher=[[Yahoo.com]]|first=Jordan|last=Zakarin|date=August 4, 2015|accessdate=March 17, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://colinashby.org/mumblecore/puffychair.html|title=The Puffy Chair (2005)|publisher=ColinAshby.org|accessdate=March 17, 2016}}</ref> It was lensed with [[Panasonic AG-DVX100]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://grindmyreels.com/the-puffy-chair-stream-my-reels/|title=THE PUFFY CHAIR \u2013 STREAM MY REELS|publisher=Grindmyreels.com|first=Dan|last=Gvozden|date=April 8, 2013|accessdate=March 17, 2016}}</ref>\n\nThe scenes set in North Carolina were actually filmed in the small town of [[Milbridge, Maine]], hometown of Katie Aselton; It holds a 77% approval rating on review aggregator website [[Rotten Tomatoes]], based on 52 reviews, with an average rating of 6.78/10. The critical consensus reads: \"First-timer Duplass offers a realistic and thoughtful romantic comedy.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/the_puffy_chair/|title=The Puffy Chair (2005)|publisher=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|accessdate=May 27, 2020}}</ref> On [[Metacritic]], the film holds a rating of 73 out of 100 based on 17 critics, indicating \"Generally favorable reviews''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/movie/the-puffy-chair|title=The Puffy Chair|publisher=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|accessdate=March 17, 2016}}</ref>\n\nScott Founders of ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' gave the film a positive review writing : \"The delicate art of reupholstery, as it applies to furniture and human relationships, gives weight to the comic machinations of \u201cThe Puffy Chair,\u201d the smart and painfully funny debut feature by filmmaker brothers Jay and Mark Duplass. [It] is an unusually human comedy of manners that, even when it falters, feels like a breath of fresh air pumped into an asphyxiating genre. Warm Sundance reception suggests dirt-cheap pic could develop a strong word-of-mouth following, particularly among college auds(sic).\"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2005/film/markets-festivals/the-puffy-chair-1200527909/|title=Review: 'The Puffy Chair'|publisher=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|first=Scott|last=Founders|date=February 15, 2005|accessdate=March 17, 2016}}</ref> Nick Schager of ''[[SlantMagazine.com]]'' gave the film 2.5/4 writing : \"the film never quite strikes a comfortable or graceful balance between silliness and solemnity, so that when the informal story eventually transforms into a sobering portrait of a crumbling relationship, the effect\u2014compounded by the often-unlikable self-involvement of its characters\u2014is more off-putting than appealing.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.slantmagazine.com/film/review/the-puffy-chair|title=The Puffy Chair|first=Nick|last=Schager|publisher=SlantMagazine.com|date=July 18, 2006|accessdate=March 17, 2016}}</ref>\n\n==References==\n{{Reflist}}\n\n==External links==\n*{{IMDb title|0436689}}\n*{{AllRovi title|319595}}\n*[https://www.wired.com/wired/archive/14.09/netflix.html Wired magazine: Netflix Presents]\n\n{{Jay and Mark Duplass}}\n\n{{DEFAULTSORT:Puffy Chair}}\n[[Category:2005 films]]\n[[Category:Camcorder films]]\n[[Category:2000s road comedy-drama films]]\n[[Category:2005 romantic comedy-drama films]]\n[[Category:American road comedy-drama films]]\n[[Category:American independent films]]\n[[Category:American romantic comedy-drama films]]\n[[Category:Duplass Brothers Productions films]]\n[[Category:Mumblecore films]]\n[[Category:2005 directorial debut films]]\n[[Category:2005 comedy films]]\n[[Category:2005 drama films]]\n[[Category:2005 independent films]]\n[[Category:2000s English-language films]]\n[[Category:2000s American films]]"}]}}}}
