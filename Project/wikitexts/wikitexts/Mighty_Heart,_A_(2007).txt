name=\"Strupp\">{{cite web |url= http://www.editorandpublisher.com/eandp/news/article_display.jsp?vnu_content_id=1003601970 |title= 'WSJ' Editors Call 'A Mighty Heart' Fair and Accurate |author= Joe think Angelina Jolie captured Mariane very, very well.\" Managing Editor [[Marcus Brauchli]], who took over for Steiger and was national editor at the time of Pearl's death, also believed the film was fair. \"I don't feel that the Journal was portrayed badly in the film,\" he said. \"I think we were treated reasonably. The movie also covers efforts by [[Law enforcement in Pakistan|Pakistan's Security Forces]], [[United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] and the U.S. Department of State's [[Diplomatic Security Service]] (DSS) to track the kidnappers and bring them to justice. Sheikh claimed responsibility for kidnapping and beheading Pearl in 2002. [[Steve LeVine]], Wall Street Journal Central Asia Correspondent\n* [[Sean Chapman]] as US Journalist\n\n== Production ==\n''A Mighty Heart'' was filmed primarily in India and France, during the summer and fall of 2006.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://madeinatlantis.com/movies_central/2007/mighty_heart_production_details.htm |title=A Mighty Heart Full Production Notes |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110719035959/http://madeinatlantis.com/movies_central/2007/mighty_heart_production_details.htm |archive-date=2011-07-19 |url-status=dead |access-date=2008-08-25 }}</ref> Fearing for the safety of star Angelina Jolie and the rest of the cast and crew, the vast majority of the film, set in Karachi, was filmed in Pune, India.\n\nHowever, before production was officially announced, Winterbottom traveled with co-star Dan Futterman and a skeleton crew to Karachi for ten days of filming at actual locations from the events.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://jewishjournal.com/culture/arts/15020/ |title=Actor Dan Futterman and director Michael Winterbottom |author=Tom Tugend |work=[[The Jewish Journal of Greater Los Angeles|Jewish Journal]] |date=June 22, 2007 |access-date=June 22, 2020}}</ref>\n\nThe production also shot multiple scenes in Austin, Texas in early 2007, including a key sequence with Marianne Pearl giving an interview in a Karachi hotel.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.austinchronicle.com/screens/2007-01-26/439266/ | title=Four for the Weekend }}</ref> Also filmed in Austin were exteriors of the Pearl family house (set in Los Angeles) and scenes set at ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'' offices in New York, which were filmed in the offices of the ''[[Austin American-Statesman]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://sues-news.com/2012/08/13/my-desk-was-in-angelina-jolie-movie/ | title=My desk was in an Angelina Jolie movie | date=13 August 2012 }}</ref>\n\n==Box-office performance==\n''A Mighty Heart'' performed [[box-office bomb|poorly at the box office]], only earning a total revenue of $18 million compared to its production budget of $16 million.<ref name=\"mojo\">{{cite web|url=https://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=mightyheart.htm |title=A Mighty Heart (2007) |access-date=2007-11-03 |website=[[Box Office Mojo]]}}</ref> The film opened June 22, 2007 in the United States and Canada and grossed $3.9 million in 1,355 theaters its opening weekend, ranking #10 at the box office. It went on to gross $18,727,125 worldwide.<ref name=\"mojo\"/> As of December 16, 2007, the film has grossed an additional $5.4 million in DVD sales and rentals in the United States.<ref>[https://www.the-numbers.com/movies/2007/MTHRT.php A Mighty Heart - Box Office Data, Movie News, Cast Information - The Numbers]</ref>\n\n==Critical reception==\nThe film received positive reviews from critics. Rotten Tomatoes reports that 79% of critics gave the film positive reviews, based on 192 reviews. The site's consensus states: \"Angelina Jolie conveys the full emotional range of a woman in a desperate situation in ''A Mighty Heart'', an urgent yet tactful film about a difficult subject.\"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/mighty_heart/ |title=A Mighty Heart - Rotten Tomatoes |access-date=2008-01-05 |website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20080105154236/http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/mighty_heart/| archive-date= 5 January 2008 | url-status= live}}</ref> Metacritic reported the film had an average score of 74 out of 100, based on 38 reviews.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/film/titles/mightyheart |title=Mighty Heart, A (2007): Reviews |access-date=2008-01-05 |website=[[Metacritic]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071230191210/http://www.metacritic.com/film/titles/mightyheart |archive-date=30 December 2007 |url-status=live }}</ref>\n\nJolie's performance was widely touted by top critics as her finest artistic achievement to date. She believes Pearl would have \"rolled his eyes\" at that description. Describing her own response to the film, Nomani said, \"For me, watching the movie was like having people enter my home, rearrange the furniture and reprogram my memory.\"<ref>{{cite news | url = https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/06/22/AR2007062201673.html?hpid=opinionsbox2 | newspaper=The Washington Post | first=Asra Q. | last=Nomani | title=A Mighty Shame | date=2007-06-24}}</ref>\n\nThe announcement of the casting of Angelina Jolie in the role of [[Mariane Pearl]] drew criticism within the African-American community.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.nbcnews.com/id/14157112|title= Angelina Jolie sparks casting controversy |date= 2 August 2006 |access-date= 2007-12-02 }}</ref> [[Orville Lloyd Douglas]], a pop critic, has criticized the casting<ref name=\"Blackface\">{{cite news |url= http://www.nzherald.co.nz/section/1501119/story.cfm?c_id=1501119&objectid=10444455&pnum=0 |title= Shades of blackface |author= Orville Lloyd Douglas |author-link= Orville Lloyd Douglas |work= [[The New Zealand Herald]] |date= 9 June 2007 |access-date= 2007-06-19 |quote= When Angelina Jolie was cast to play Marianne Pearl in the biopic film A Mighty Heart there was an uproar by the African American community. She put her whole heart into it, and I think she understood why we should do this movie.
